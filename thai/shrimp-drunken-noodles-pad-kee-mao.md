# Shrimp Drunken Noodles Pad Kee Mao

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g fresh wide rice noodles (sen yai), gently separated
- 400 g raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 3 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 4–6 bird's eye chillies, bruised and sliced
- 1 red capsicum, thinly sliced
- 100 g baby corn, halved lengthwise
- 2 large tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 2 large handfuls fresh Thai basil
- 2 eggs

## Steps

1. Whisk together oyster sauce, fish sauce, dark soy sauce, light soy sauce, and sugar in a small bowl to make the sauce.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large wok over high heat until smoking. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook for 1–1.5 minutes per side until pink and just cooked. Remove and set aside.
3. Add remaining 2 tbsp oil. Add garlic and chillies and stir-fry for 30 seconds until golden and fragrant.
4. Add baby corn and capsicum. Stir-fry for 2 minutes over high heat until just beginning to soften.
5. Add the fresh rice noodles, pulling them apart as you add them. Pour the sauce over and toss vigorously, allowing the noodles to char slightly against the hot wok for 1–2 minutes.
6. Push noodles to the side. Crack in the eggs and scramble for 30 seconds, then fold through the noodles.
7. Return shrimp to the wok. Add tomato wedges and fold through gently. Remove from heat and fold through the Thai basil. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Drunken noodles get their name from being a popular late-night dish — the bold, spicy flavours make them a satisfying cure-all. Fresh wide rice noodles give the best texture; if using dried noodles, soak in cold water for 30 minutes, drain well, and reduce sauce amounts slightly as dried noodles absorb more liquid. Don't skimp on the bird's eye chillies — the heat is central to this dish's character.
