# Tom Yum Goong

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g large raw shrimp, shell-on (heads on if available)
- 1 L water or light chicken stock
- 3 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 5 cm pieces
- 5 cm fresh galangal (or ginger), thinly sliced
- 6 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 200 g oyster mushrooms, torn into pieces
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 3 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
- 1 tbsp chilli paste in oil (nam prik pao)
- 4–6 bird's eye chillies, bruised
- 2 ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges
- 1 handful fresh coriander, to serve
- 1 tsp sugar

## Steps

1. Peel and devein the shrimp, reserving the shells and heads. Place the shells and heads in a saucepan with the stock or water and bring to a boil. Simmer for 8 minutes to build a rich shrimp broth. Strain and discard solids, returning the broth to the pan.
2. Add the lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves to the broth. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat and simmer for 3 minutes to infuse the aromatics.
3. Add the mushrooms and tomato wedges. Cook for 2 minutes.
4. Stir in the chilli paste in oil (nam prik pao) and bruised bird's eye chillies.
5. Add the peeled shrimp and cook for 1–2 minutes until just pink and curled. Do not overcook.
6. Remove from heat. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Taste — the soup should be sharply sour, salty, and fiery in equal measure.
7. Ladle into bowls and top with fresh coriander. Serve immediately with steamed rice on the side if desired.

## Special Notice

> The lemongrass, galangal, and kaffir lime leaves are aromatics and are not meant to be eaten — warn guests or remove them before serving. Nam prik pao (chilli paste in oil) is available at Asian grocery stores and adds an essential smoky depth; do not substitute with fresh chilli paste. For the clear (tom yum nam sai) version, omit the chilli paste in oil and coconut milk.
