# Pineapple Fried Rice with Crab

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, preferably day-old and refrigerated
- 200 g fresh or canned crab meat, drained and picked over
- 250 g fresh pineapple, cut into 1.5 cm chunks (or canned in natural juice, drained)
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 small onion, finely diced
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp curry powder
- 3 eggs, lightly beaten
- 50 g frozen peas, thawed
- 4 spring onions, sliced
- 3 tbsp roasted cashews
- Fresh coriander and sliced red chilli, to garnish
- Lime wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Use day-old cold rice for best results — fresh rice is too moist and will clump. Break up any large lumps with your hands before cooking.
2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large wok over high heat until smoking. Add the garlic and onion and stir-fry for 1–2 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
3. Push the aromatics to the side of the wok. Pour in the remaining 1 tbsp oil and add the beaten eggs. Scramble for 30 seconds until just set, then break into pieces and mix through the aromatics.
4. Add the cold rice to the wok and toss vigorously over high heat, pressing the rice against the hot wok surface to separate grains and develop a lightly toasted flavour, about 2–3 minutes.
5. Season the rice with fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and curry powder. Toss well to distribute evenly.
6. Add the pineapple chunks and frozen peas. Toss for 1 minute until heated through.
7. Remove from heat. Gently fold through the crab meat, spring onions, and cashews. Serve garnished with coriander and chilli, with lime wedges on the side.

## Special Notice

> Day-old refrigerated rice is essential — freshly cooked rice contains too much moisture and will steam rather than fry, resulting in a sticky, clumped texture. Canned crab works perfectly well in this dish; fresh crab adds luxury but is not necessary. The fried rice is excellent served inside a hollowed-out pineapple half for an impressive presentation at dinner parties.
