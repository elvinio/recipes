# Pork Larb

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | lunch

## Ingredients

- 600 g ground pork (not too lean)
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 3 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 2 limes)
- 1 tbsp toasted rice powder (see step 1)
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 4 tbsp raw jasmine rice (for toasting)
- 3–4 dried red chillies, toasted and roughly crumbled, or 1.5 tsp chilli flakes
- 4 shallots, thinly sliced
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 large handful fresh mint leaves
- 1 large handful fresh coriander leaves
- 1 stalk lemongrass, white part only, very finely sliced
- Steamed sticky rice or lettuce cups, to serve

## Steps

1. Toast the raw jasmine rice in a dry frying pan over medium heat, stirring constantly for 4–5 minutes until golden and nutty smelling. Transfer to a mortar and grind to a coarse powder. Set aside.
2. Heat the oil in a large frying pan or wok over high heat. Add the ground pork and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, for 5–6 minutes until fully cooked through and beginning to colour.
3. Remove the pan from heat. Stir in fish sauce and lime juice while the pork is still hot, allowing the residual heat to meld the flavours.
4. Add the toasted rice powder, crumbled dried chillies, sliced shallots, and lemongrass. Toss to combine.
5. Taste and adjust the balance of fish sauce (salty), lime juice (sour), and chilli (heat) to your preference — larb should be boldly seasoned.
6. Fold through the spring onions, fresh mint, and coriander just before serving to keep them vibrant.
7. Serve immediately with steamed sticky rice or in lettuce cups as a wrap.

## Special Notice

> Larb is best eaten fresh, as the herbs wilt quickly; if making ahead, prepare the cooked pork base and add herbs just before serving. The toasted rice powder is non-negotiable — it gives the dish its distinctive nutty texture and body. For extra heat, increase the dried chillies or add a finely sliced fresh bird's eye chilli.
