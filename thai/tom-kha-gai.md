# Tom Kha Gai

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g boneless chicken thighs, thinly sliced
- 800 mL full-fat coconut milk (2 cans)
- 250 mL chicken stock
- 5 cm fresh galangal, thinly sliced into coins
- 3 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 5 cm pieces
- 6 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 200 g oyster mushrooms, torn into pieces
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice (about 1.5 limes)
- 1 tsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 3 bird's eye chillies, bruised (optional, for a gentle background heat)
- 2 tbsp coriander leaves, to serve
- 1 long red chilli, sliced, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine coconut milk and chicken stock in a medium saucepan. Add the galangal, lemongrass, and kaffir lime leaves. Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat — do not boil vigorously or the coconut milk may curdle and separate.
2. Simmer for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to allow the aromatics to deeply infuse the broth.
3. Add the sliced chicken and bruised bird's eye chillies (if using). Maintain a gentle simmer and cook for 6–8 minutes until the chicken is cooked through.
4. Add the oyster mushrooms and cook for a further 2 minutes until just tender.
5. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar. Stir gently and taste — the soup should be rich, coconutty, and gently tangy.
6. Remove from heat and stir in the lime juice. Taste once more and adjust with a little more fish sauce for saltiness or lime for brightness.
7. Ladle into bowls, discarding the large lemongrass pieces and galangal coins if preferred. Garnish with coriander and sliced red chilli.

## Special Notice

> Never allow tom kha to boil hard — a gentle simmer keeps the coconut milk silky and prevents graininess. The galangal, lemongrass, and kaffir lime leaves are for flavour infusion only and are quite tough; they are traditionally left in the bowl but not eaten. For a richer soup, use all coconut milk with no stock; for a lighter version, increase stock to 500 mL.
