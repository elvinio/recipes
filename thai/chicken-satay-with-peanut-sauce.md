# Chicken Satay with Peanut Sauce

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless chicken thighs, sliced into 2 cm thick strips
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tbsp coconut milk
- **Peanut Sauce:**
- 160 g (½ cup / 120 mL) smooth peanut butter
- 200 mL coconut milk
- 2 tbsp red curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 60 mL (¼ cup) water
- Cucumber slices and fresh coriander, to serve
- 8–12 bamboo skewers

## Steps

1. Combine vegetable oil, soy sauce, turmeric, cumin, ground coriander, and coconut milk in a bowl. Add the chicken strips and toss to coat. Leave to marinate for at least 10 minutes (or up to overnight in the refrigerator).
2. Thread the marinated chicken onto skewers, weaving the meat to lie flat. Use 2–3 strips per skewer.
3. Make the peanut sauce: combine peanut butter, coconut milk, red curry paste, fish sauce, palm sugar, and water in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir constantly for 3–4 minutes until smooth, thickened, and heated through. Stir in lime juice, taste, and adjust seasoning. Remove from heat and keep warm.
4. Heat a large griddle pan or non-stick frying pan over high heat until very hot. Brush with a little oil.
5. Cook the chicken skewers for 3–4 minutes per side until golden, caramelised, and cooked through with no pink remaining. Work in batches to avoid crowding.
6. Transfer to a serving plate and rest for 2 minutes.
7. Serve the skewers with warm peanut sauce, fresh cucumber slices, and coriander.

## Special Notice

> Soak bamboo skewers in water for 20 minutes before threading to prevent them from scorching. The peanut sauce thickens as it cools; thin with a splash of warm water if needed when reheating. Leftover peanut sauce keeps refrigerated for up to 5 days and doubles as a great dipping sauce for vegetables or a dressing for noodles.
