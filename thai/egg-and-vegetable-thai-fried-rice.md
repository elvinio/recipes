# Egg and Vegetable Thai Fried Rice

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, preferably day-old and cold
- 5 eggs
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 medium carrot, diced into 5 mm cubes
- 100 g broccoli florets, cut small
- 100 g frozen corn kernels, thawed
- 80 g frozen peas, thawed
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp white sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 4 spring onions, sliced
- White pepper, to serve
- Cucumber slices and lime wedges, to serve
- Sliced red chilli and fish sauce with chilli, to serve

## Steps

1. Break up the cold rice by hand into individual grains before cooking. Beat 4 of the eggs together in a small bowl with a pinch of salt. Fry the remaining egg sunny-side up in a small pan and set aside for garnish.
2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large wok over very high heat until smoking. Add the garlic and fry for 20 seconds. Add the diced carrot and broccoli and stir-fry for 2 minutes until slightly softened.
3. Add the corn and peas and toss for 30 seconds.
4. Push vegetables to the side. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil and pour in the beaten eggs. Scramble for 30 seconds until just beginning to set, then break up and toss through the vegetables.
5. Add the cold rice. Press and toss against the hot wok, breaking up any clumps, for 2–3 minutes until the rice is well separated and starting to turn golden in spots.
6. Season with fish sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil. Toss thoroughly for 1 minute over high heat.
7. Divide into bowls. Top each with spring onions and a crack of white pepper. Garnish with the fried egg and serve with cucumber, lime wedges, and chilli fish sauce on the side.

## Special Notice

> This versatile dish works as a base for any vegetables you have on hand — zucchini, capsicum, mushrooms, and bok choy are all excellent additions. Cold day-old rice is non-negotiable for proper fried rice texture; if you only have fresh rice, spread it on a tray and refrigerate uncovered for at least 1 hour before using. A splash of fish sauce on the side allows each person to season to their own taste.
