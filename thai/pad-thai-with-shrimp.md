# Pad Thai with Shrimp

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 300 g dried flat rice noodles (3 mm wide)
- 400 g raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 2 tbsp tamarind paste
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 eggs
- 200 g firm tofu, cut into 1 cm cubes
- 100 g bean sprouts
- 4 spring onions, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 4 tbsp roasted peanuts, roughly chopped
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- 1 tsp dried chilli flakes, to serve

## Steps

1. Soak the rice noodles in cold water for 20 minutes until pliable, then drain and set aside. Meanwhile, whisk together fish sauce, tamarind paste, oyster sauce, and palm sugar in a small bowl to make the pad thai sauce.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large wok or heavy-based frying pan over high heat until smoking. Add the tofu cubes and fry, tossing occasionally, for 3–4 minutes until golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
3. Add another 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook for 1–2 minutes per side until pink and just cooked through. Remove and set aside with the tofu.
4. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil and fry the garlic for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the drained noodles and pour over the pad thai sauce. Toss vigorously for 2 minutes, letting the noodles absorb the sauce.
5. Push the noodles to one side of the wok. Crack the eggs into the empty space and scramble lightly, then fold into the noodles before they fully set.
6. Return the shrimp and tofu to the wok. Add the bean sprouts and spring onions and toss everything together for 1 minute over high heat.
7. Divide into bowls. Top with chopped peanuts and serve with lime wedges and chilli flakes on the side.

## Special Notice

> Leftover pad thai keeps refrigerated for up to 2 days; reheat in a hot wok with a splash of water to loosen the noodles. Have all ingredients prepped and measured before you start — once the wok is hot, the dish comes together in under 10 minutes. Pre-soaking (not boiling) the noodles prevents them from becoming mushy during stir-frying.
