# Pork and Lemongrass Soup

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g pork shoulder or pork neck, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 1.2 L chicken or pork stock
- 3 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 5 cm pieces
- 5 cm fresh galangal (or ginger), thinly sliced
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 200 g oyster mushrooms, torn
- 3 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 shallots, halved
- 3 bird's eye chillies, bruised (optional)
- 1 medium tomato, cut into wedges
- 1 large handful fresh coriander
- 2 spring onions, sliced
- White pepper, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine the stock, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and shallots in a large saucepan. Bring to a boil over medium-high heat, then reduce to a steady simmer for 8 minutes to build a fragrant aromatic broth.
2. Add the pork pieces to the simmering broth. Skim off any foam that rises to the surface during the first few minutes.
3. Reduce heat to medium-low. Partially cover and simmer gently for 20–22 minutes until the pork is tender and cooked through — the cubes should offer no resistance when pierced with a chopstick.
4. Add the oyster mushrooms, tomato wedges, and bruised chillies (if using). Simmer for 3–4 minutes until mushrooms are tender.
5. Season with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar. Taste carefully — the broth should be delicately fragrant, lightly salty, and gently tangy.
6. Ladle into bowls, distributing the pork and vegetables evenly. Top with fresh coriander and spring onions.
7. Serve with a crack of white pepper and steamed rice on the side if desired.

## Special Notice

> This is a gentle, restorative soup — the lemongrass and galangal infuse the broth with a subtle citrus-ginger fragrance that complements the pork beautifully. Pork shoulder or neck is preferred for its natural fat content and tenderness after simmering; lean pork loin will become dry. The aromatic ingredients (lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves) are typically left in the bowl but not eaten — remove them before serving for a cleaner presentation.
