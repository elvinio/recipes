# Chicken Yellow Curry

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm chunks
- 400 mL full-fat coconut milk (1 can)
- 200 mL chicken stock
- 3 tbsp yellow curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 large onion, cut into thin wedges
- 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into 2.5 cm cubes
- 1 medium carrot, cut into 1 cm rounds
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised and halved
- Fresh coriander and sliced red chilli, to serve
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat. Add the yellow curry paste and fry for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly, until fragrant and slightly darkened.
2. Add 200 mL of the coconut milk and stir through the paste. Cook over high heat for 2 minutes until the oil begins to separate on the surface.
3. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat. Cook for 3 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sealed and opaque on the outside.
4. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and chicken stock. Add the lemongrass stalk and kaffir lime leaves. Bring to a boil.
5. Add the potato cubes and carrot rounds. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 12–14 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes and carrot are tender and the chicken is fully cooked.
6. Add the onion wedges and cook for a final 2 minutes — they should retain a slight bite.
7. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar. Remove lemongrass. Serve over jasmine rice, topped with coriander and sliced chilli.

## Special Notice

> Yellow curry is milder and sweeter than red or green curry, making it an excellent introduction to Thai cooking. Waxy potatoes (such as Kipfler or Desiree) hold their shape better than floury varieties during simmering. The curry keeps well refrigerated for up to 3 days — the flavour improves significantly on the second day as the spices continue to develop.
