# Tofu and Vegetable Jungle Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g firm tofu, pressed and cut into 2.5 cm cubes
- 700 mL vegetable stock
- 3 tbsp red or jungle curry paste (kang pa paste if available)
- 2 tbsp fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
- 1 tsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 2 stalks lemongrass, bruised and cut into 5 cm pieces
- 3 cm fresh galangal, sliced
- 150 g Thai eggplant, quartered (or regular eggplant, cubed)
- 100 g green beans, trimmed and cut into 4 cm pieces
- 1 medium zucchini, cut into 1.5 cm half-moons
- 100 g bamboo shoots, drained and sliced
- 4–6 bird's eye chillies, bruised
- 1 large handful fresh Thai basil
- Steamed jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Press the tofu firmly between layers of paper towel for 10 minutes to extract moisture. Cut into cubes.
2. Heat 1.5 tbsp oil in a large wok or saucepan over high heat. Add the tofu and fry, turning occasionally, for 4–5 minutes until golden and crispy on all sides. Remove and set aside.
3. Add the remaining oil to the wok. Add the jungle curry paste and fry for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly, until deeply fragrant.
4. Pour in the vegetable stock and bring to a boil. Add the lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and bruised bird's eye chillies.
5. Add the Thai eggplant and bamboo shoots. Reduce heat to medium and simmer for 8 minutes.
6. Add the green beans and zucchini. Continue to simmer for 6–8 minutes until all vegetables are tender but still retain some texture.
7. Return the tofu to the pan. Season with fish sauce and palm sugar. Stir through Thai basil and remove from heat. Serve with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Jungle curry (kang pa) is a dry, broth-based curry with no coconut milk — making it one of the lighter and more intensely flavoured Thai curries. The lack of coconut milk allows the herbal, fiery flavours of the paste and aromatics to shine through without richness. If you cannot find jungle curry paste, red curry paste works well as a substitute. The vegetables can be swapped freely for whatever is in season.
