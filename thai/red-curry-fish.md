# Red Curry Fish

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g firm white fish fillets (barramundi, snapper, or cod), cut into 4 cm chunks
- 400 mL full-fat coconut milk (1 can)
- 150 mL fish stock or water
- 3 tbsp red curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 1 red capsicum, thinly sliced
- 150 g green beans, trimmed and halved
- 1 large handful fresh Thai basil
- 1 long red chilli, sliced
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat the oil in a wide saucepan or deep frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the red curry paste and fry, stirring, for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and the paste darkens slightly.
2. Pour in half the coconut milk (200 mL) and stir together with the paste. Cook over high heat for 2–3 minutes, stirring regularly, until the coconut oil starts to separate and the sauce is fragrant and glossy.
3. Add the remaining coconut milk and fish stock. Stir in fish sauce and palm sugar. Add the kaffir lime leaves and bring to a gentle simmer.
4. Add the green beans and capsicum. Simmer for 3 minutes until the beans are just tender but still bright green.
5. Gently place the fish pieces into the simmering curry. Do not stir vigorously — use a spoon to submerge the fish and gently baste with the sauce. Cook for 4–5 minutes until the fish is just cooked through and flakes easily.
6. Taste the sauce and adjust with more fish sauce for saltiness or palm sugar for sweetness.
7. Scatter Thai basil and sliced chilli over the top. Serve immediately over jasmine rice.

## Special Notice

> Fish cooks quickly and can become rubbery if overcooked — watch carefully and remove from heat as soon as the flesh turns opaque and flakes at the thickest point. The curry base (without fish) can be prepared up to a day ahead and refrigerated; add the fish only when reheating to serve. Kaffir lime leaves are not eaten but add an essential citrus perfume — they can be found fresh or frozen at Asian grocery stores.
