# Lamb Panang Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 650 g lamb leg or shoulder, trimmed and cut into 3 cm cubes
- 400 mL full-fat coconut milk (1 can)
- 100 mL water
- 3 tbsp panang curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 3 tbsp roasted peanuts, ground or finely chopped
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, 2 torn, 2 very finely julienned for garnish
- 1 long red chilli, sliced diagonally
- 1 handful fresh Thai basil
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat the oil in a large heavy-based saucepan or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the panang curry paste and fry, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes until very fragrant and the paste has deepened in colour.
2. Add 200 mL of the coconut milk and stir into the paste. Cook over high heat for 3 minutes, stirring often, until the coconut oil separates and the sauce is aromatic and glossy.
3. Add the lamb pieces and toss to coat thoroughly. Cook for 4–5 minutes, turning occasionally, until the lamb is sealed and lightly browned on the outside.
4. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and water. Add the torn kaffir lime leaves. Stir in fish sauce and palm sugar.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 20–22 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the lamb is tender.
6. Remove the lid and stir in the ground peanuts. Increase heat to medium and simmer uncovered for 5 minutes until the sauce thickens to a rich, coating consistency. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Serve over jasmine rice, garnished with sliced chilli, julienned kaffir lime leaves, and fresh Thai basil.

## Special Notice

> Panang curry should be thick and creamy rather than soupy — if it thins out too much, simmer uncovered until reduced. Lamb shoulder gives the richest flavour due to its fat content, but leg works well for a leaner result. The ground peanuts are a signature of panang curry and must not be skipped; they add body, nuttiness, and subtle texture to the sauce.
