# Beef Massaman Curry

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g beef chuck, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 400 mL full-fat coconut milk (1 can)
- 200 mL beef stock
- 3 tbsp massaman curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 1 tbsp tamarind paste
- 400 g waxy potatoes, peeled and cut into 3 cm chunks
- 1 large onion, cut into 8 wedges
- 60 g roasted unsalted peanuts
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 3 cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 bay leaves
- Steamed jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Set the electric pressure cooker to sauté mode. Heat the oil, then add massaman curry paste and fry for 2 minutes, stirring, until fragrant. Add cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves and cook for a further 30 seconds.
2. Add the beef cubes and toss to coat in the paste. Sear for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned on the outside.
3. Pour in the coconut milk and beef stock. Stir in fish sauce, palm sugar, and tamarind paste. Scrape any browned bits from the bottom of the pot.
4. Seal the pressure cooker lid and cook on high pressure for 25 minutes.
5. Release the pressure using the quick-release valve. Remove the lid carefully. The beef should be very tender — test by pressing a piece with a spoon.
6. Switch back to sauté mode. Add the potato chunks and onion wedges. Simmer uncovered for 12–15 minutes until the potatoes are tender and the sauce has thickened slightly.
7. Stir in the peanuts. Taste and adjust seasoning with fish sauce or palm sugar. Remove cinnamon stick and bay leaves. Serve over jasmine rice.

## Special Notice

> Massaman curry tastes even better the next day as the flavours meld; store refrigerated for up to 3 days or freeze for up to 2 months. If the sauce is too thin after pressure cooking, simmer uncovered on sauté mode for an extra 5 minutes. Chuck beef is ideal for pressure cooking — avoid lean cuts as they turn dry under pressure.
