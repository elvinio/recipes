# Green Curry Chicken

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 400 mL full-fat coconut milk (1 can)
- 200 mL chicken stock
- 3 tbsp green curry paste
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 200 g Thai eggplant, quartered (or regular eggplant cut into 2 cm cubes)
- 100 g baby spinach
- 4 kaffir lime leaves, torn
- 1 stalk lemongrass, bruised and halved lengthwise
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- 1 handful fresh Thai basil leaves
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve
- 1 long red chilli, sliced, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the green curry paste and fry, stirring constantly, for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and the paste darkens slightly.
2. Pour in half the coconut milk (200 mL) and stir to combine with the paste. Increase heat to high and cook for 2 minutes, stirring frequently, until the coconut milk thickens and the oil begins to separate.
3. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat in the curry base. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken turns opaque on all sides.
4. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and chicken stock. Add the lemongrass and kaffir lime leaves. Bring to a gentle boil.
5. Add the Thai eggplant and reduce heat to medium. Simmer for 8–10 minutes until the eggplant is tender and the chicken is cooked through.
6. Stir in fish sauce and palm sugar. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove the lemongrass stalk.
7. Fold through the baby spinach and Thai basil. Serve over jasmine rice, garnished with sliced chilli.

## Special Notice

> Green curry intensifies in flavour overnight — make it a day ahead for a richer result. If the curry is too thick, thin with a little extra stock or water. Thai eggplant has a pleasantly bitter flavour; if substituting regular eggplant, salt and drain it first to remove bitterness.
