# Basil Pork Stir-Fry Pad Kra Pao

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g ground pork (or finely chopped pork collar)
- 3 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp vegetable oil
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 4–6 bird's eye chillies, finely sliced (adjust to taste)
- 2 long red chillies, sliced diagonally
- 1 large handful fresh holy basil leaves (or Thai basil if unavailable)
- 4 eggs, fried sunny-side up (one per serve)
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve
- White pepper, to serve

## Steps

1. Whisk together oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Heat the oil in a large wok or heavy frying pan over very high heat until smoking. Add the garlic and all the chillies. Fry for 30 seconds, tossing constantly, until the garlic is golden and fragrant — do not let it burn.
3. Add the ground pork and spread it out across the wok. Cook without stirring for 1–2 minutes until the bottom begins to brown and caramelise.
4. Break up the pork with a spatula and toss, continuing to cook for 3–4 minutes over high heat until cooked through and starting to crisp at the edges.
5. Pour the sauce over the pork and toss to coat evenly. Cook for 1 more minute until the sauce glazes the meat and begins to caramelise slightly.
6. Remove from heat and fold through the holy basil — the residual heat will wilt the leaves and release their anise-like fragrance.
7. In a separate pan, fry the eggs in a little oil over high heat until the whites are set and crispy-edged. Serve the pork over jasmine rice, topped with a fried egg and a crack of white pepper.

## Special Notice

> Holy basil (kra pao) has a distinct peppery, clove-like flavour essential to this dish — Thai basil is a passable substitute but the flavour will differ. This dish is intentionally bold and salty to contrast the plain rice; taste before adding extra seasoning. For the traditional Thai street-food experience, the fried egg with crispy edges is not optional — fry it in hot oil for the signature texture.
