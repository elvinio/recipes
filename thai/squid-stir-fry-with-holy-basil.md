# Squid Stir-Fry with Holy Basil

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Thai | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g cleaned squid tubes and tentacles
- 3 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 3 tbsp vegetable oil
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 4–6 bird's eye chillies, thinly sliced
- 2 long red chillies, sliced diagonally
- 1 red capsicum, cut into thin strips
- 1 large handful fresh holy basil (or Thai basil)
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm pieces
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Score the squid tubes with a shallow crosshatch pattern on the inside surface (this helps the squid curl attractively and cook evenly), then cut into 4 cm pieces. Leave tentacles whole or halve if large. Pat all squid pieces dry with paper towels — this is critical to prevent steaming.
2. Whisk together oyster sauce, fish sauce, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar in a small bowl.
3. Heat the oil in a large wok over very high heat until smoking hot. Add the garlic and all the chillies and fry for 20–30 seconds until the garlic begins to turn golden.
4. Add the squid in a single layer and leave undisturbed for 30 seconds. Toss and stir-fry vigorously for 1–2 minutes. Squid is cooked when it curls and turns white — do not overcook or it will become rubbery.
5. Add the capsicum strips and spring onions. Toss for 30 seconds — they should remain slightly crisp.
6. Pour the sauce over and toss to coat everything evenly. Cook for a final 30 seconds over high heat.
7. Remove from heat and fold through the holy basil. Serve immediately over jasmine rice.

## Special Notice

> Squid must be cooked fast and hot — it turns rubbery if cooked slowly or for too long. The total cooking time from adding squid to the wok should be no more than 2–3 minutes. Scoring the tubes allows them to curl into beautiful curls and ensures even, rapid cooking. Dry the squid very thoroughly before stir-frying, as surface moisture causes violent splattering in the hot oil.
