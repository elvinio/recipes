# Char Siu Pork Noodles

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g pork tenderloin or pork shoulder, cut into 3 cm strips
- 3 tbsp hoisin sauce
- 2 tbsp honey
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp five-spice powder
- 2 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp red food colouring (optional, traditional)
- 400 g fresh egg noodles (or dried, cooked per package)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 300 mL chicken stock
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 4 baby bok choy, halved lengthwise
- 3 spring onions, sliced

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Line a baking tray with foil and set a wire rack on top.
2. Combine hoisin, honey, soy sauce, oyster sauce, Shaoxing wine, five-spice, sesame oil, and food colouring (if using) in a bowl. Add pork strips and toss to coat thoroughly.
3. Arrange pork strips on the rack. Roast for 12 minutes, brush with remaining marinade, then roast a further 8 minutes until caramelised and slightly charred at the edges. Rest for 5 minutes, then slice at an angle.
4. While pork roasts, cook noodles according to package instructions. Drain and toss with a little sesame oil to prevent sticking.
5. Heat a wok over medium-high heat. Add neutral oil, then garlic. Stir-fry for 30 seconds. Pour in chicken stock, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Bring to a simmer.
6. Add bok choy to the broth and cook for 2 minutes until just tender.
7. Divide noodles into bowls. Ladle broth and bok choy over the noodles.
8. Top with sliced char siu pork and spring onions. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> The char siu marinade can be made up to 2 days ahead; marinate the pork overnight for deeper flavour and a more intense lacquered finish. The broiler/grill setting in the final 2 minutes creates extra char — watch carefully to avoid burning. Leftover char siu pork keeps refrigerated for 4 days and is excellent in fried rice or bao.
