# Shrimp Fried Rice

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g raw shrimp/prawns, peeled and deveined (medium-large)
- 800 g day-old cooked jasmine rice (cold, broken up)
- 3 eggs, lightly beaten
- 120 g frozen peas, thawed
- 3 spring onions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce (for colour)
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ½ tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp salt, or to taste

## Steps

1. Pat shrimp dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt and white pepper.
2. Heat a wok over high heat until smoking. Add 1 tbsp oil, then add shrimp in a single layer. Sear for 1 minute per side until pink and just cooked through. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add beaten eggs and scramble until just set but still slightly wet. Break into large chunks and push to one side.
4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil, then add garlic, ginger, and the white parts of the spring onions. Stir-fry for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Add the cold rice, breaking up any clumps. Press and toss continuously over high heat for 2 minutes until each grain is separate and heated through.
6. Add peas. Pour soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper over the rice. Toss everything together for 1–2 minutes.
7. Return shrimp to the wok and fold in gently. Drizzle with sesame oil. Taste and adjust salt.
8. Scatter green spring onion tops over the top and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Day-old cold rice is essential — fresh rice is too wet and will clump and steam. Spread freshly cooked rice on a tray and refrigerate uncovered for at least 4 hours, or overnight. Cook over the highest heat your stovetop allows; the "wok hei" (breath of the wok) smoky character only develops at very high temperatures.
