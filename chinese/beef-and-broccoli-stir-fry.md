# Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g flank steak or sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain
- 500 g broccoli, cut into small florets
- 3 tbsp oyster sauce
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tsp sesame oil
- 2 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp white pepper
- 2 tsp cornstarch, plus 1 tbsp for slurry
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 120 mL (½ cup) beef stock or water
- 1 tsp chili flakes or sambal oelek (optional, for heat)
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. In a bowl, combine sliced beef with 1 tbsp soy sauce, 2 tsp cornstarch, and 1 tsp sesame oil. Massage well and let sit for 10 minutes.
2. Mix oyster sauce, remaining 1 tbsp soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, remaining 1 tsp sesame oil, and white pepper in a small bowl. Stir in 1 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water to make a sauce slurry.
3. Blanch broccoli florets in boiling salted water for 90 seconds; drain and transfer to an ice bath, then drain again. This keeps them bright green and crisp-tender.
4. Heat a wok over very high heat until smoking. Add 2 tbsp oil, then spread beef in a single layer. Sear for 1 minute without stirring, then stir-fry until just browned, about 1 more minute. Remove to a plate.
5. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 30 seconds.
6. Add broccoli and toss to heat through for 1 minute.
7. Return beef to the wok, pour in stock and sauce slurry. Toss everything over high heat for 1–2 minutes until the sauce thickens and coats everything. Add chili flakes if using.
8. Serve immediately over steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Slicing the beef against the grain and marinating with cornstarch (velveting) keeps it tender even over high heat. For the best sear, cook the beef in two batches if your pan is not large enough — crowding drops the temperature and causes steaming instead of browning. Leftovers reheat well in a hot pan with a splash of water.
