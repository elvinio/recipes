# Pork and Cabbage Potstickers

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 300 g ground pork (not lean — some fat needed)
- 200 g napa cabbage, very finely shredded
- 1 tsp salt (for cabbage)
- 3 spring onions, finely chopped
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp sugar
- 30–32 round dumpling wrappers (store-bought gyoza wrappers work well)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, for frying
- 120 mL (½ cup) water, for steaming

**Dipping Sauce:**
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp chili oil
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar

## Steps

1. Toss shredded cabbage with 1 tsp salt in a colander. Let stand 10 minutes, then squeeze out as much liquid as possible with your hands — this prevents soggy filling.
2. Combine ground pork, squeezed cabbage, spring onions, ginger, soy sauce, sesame oil, Shaoxing wine, white pepper, and sugar. Stir vigorously in one direction for 2 minutes until the mixture becomes sticky and cohesive.
3. Place a wrapper on a clean surface. Add 1 heaped tsp of filling to the centre. Moisten the edge with water. Fold in half and pleat the front edge 4–5 times to seal, pressing firmly against the flat back edge. Repeat with remaining wrappers.
4. Mix all dipping sauce ingredients in a small bowl. Set aside.
5. Heat a large non-stick or cast iron skillet over medium-high heat. Add neutral oil and arrange potstickers flat-side down in a single layer. Fry undisturbed for 2–3 minutes until the bottoms are deep golden brown.
6. Carefully pour in 120 mL water — it will spit vigorously. Immediately cover with a lid. Steam for 6–7 minutes until the water has evaporated and the wrappers are translucent and tender.
7. Remove the lid and let any remaining moisture cook off for 1 minute until the bases are crisp again.
8. Serve immediately, golden side up, with the dipping sauce.

## Special Notice

> The two-stage fry-and-steam method gives potstickers their signature crispy bottom and soft top — do not skip either stage. Filling can be made a day ahead and stored covered in the refrigerator. Uncooked assembled potstickers can be frozen on a tray then transferred to a bag; cook from frozen, adding 2–3 extra minutes of steaming time.
