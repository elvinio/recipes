# Egg Fried Rice with Lap Cheong

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g day-old cooked jasmine rice (cold, broken up)
- 4 lap cheong (Chinese dried sausages), thinly sliced on the diagonal
- 4 eggs, lightly beaten
- 4 spring onions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 100 g frozen peas, thawed
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ½ tsp white pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt to taste

## Steps

1. Render the lap cheong: add sausage slices to a cold wok and place over medium heat. Cook for 3–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the fat renders and the edges turn glossy and lightly crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the wok.
2. Increase heat to high. Add neutral oil to the rendered fat, then add the beaten eggs. Let them set for 10 seconds, then scramble until just barely cooked — still slightly wet. Break into chunky pieces and push to the sides.
3. Add garlic and white spring onion pieces to the centre of the wok. Stir-fry for 30 seconds.
4. Add cold rice, pressing and breaking up any clumps with the spatula. Toss continuously over high heat for 2 minutes until each grain is separate, hot, and beginning to toast slightly.
5. Add peas and toss to combine for 30 seconds.
6. Pour soy sauce, oyster sauce, dark soy sauce, and white pepper over the rice. Toss to distribute evenly for 1 minute.
7. Return the lap cheong to the wok and fold in. Drizzle with sesame oil and taste for salt.
8. Plate and garnish with green spring onion tops. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Lap cheong releases enough fat to partially fry the rice, adding a unique sweet-savoury depth — do not drain off the rendered fat. Cold day-old rice is critical for texture. If lap cheong is unavailable, lop yuk (cured pork belly) or Chinese-style bacon are good substitutes; avoid substituting regular sausage as the flavour profile will differ substantially.
