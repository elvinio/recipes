# Shrimp and Snow Pea Stir-Fry

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw shrimp/prawns, peeled and deveined, tails on
- 250 g snow peas (mangetout), strings removed
- 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 2 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
- ½ tsp white pepper
- Pinch of salt
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Pat shrimp dry with paper towels. Season lightly with salt and white pepper.
2. Whisk together oyster sauce, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sesame oil in a small bowl. Set sauce aside.
3. Heat a wok over high heat until smoking. Add 2 tbsp oil. Add shrimp in a single layer and sear for 60–90 seconds per side until pink and just curled. Do not overcook. Transfer to a plate immediately.
4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 20 seconds.
5. Add snow peas and red bell pepper. Toss and stir-fry over high heat for 2 minutes — the snow peas should stay bright green and crisp-tender.
6. Pour the sauce over the vegetables. Stir the cornstarch slurry and pour it in; toss everything for 30 seconds until the sauce lightly coats the vegetables.
7. Return shrimp to the wok and toss for 30 seconds to combine and glaze everything with the sauce.
8. Serve immediately over steamed jasmine rice.

## Special Notice

> Shrimp overcooks in seconds — pull them from the wok the moment they turn pink and the tails curl tightly; they will finish cooking from residual heat when returned at the end. Snow peas are best when still crisp; the vibrant green colour fades the moment they overcook. This dish comes together very quickly — have all ingredients prepared and within reach before turning on the heat.
