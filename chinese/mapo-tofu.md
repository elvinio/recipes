# Mapo Tofu

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g soft or silken tofu, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 200 g ground pork (or beef)
- 2 tbsp doubanjiang (Pixian chili bean paste)
- 1 tbsp fermented black beans (douchi), roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 300 mL chicken stock
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tsp cornstarch mixed with 3 tbsp cold water
- 1 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns
- 3 spring onions, sliced
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Bring a medium saucepan of lightly salted water to a boil. Gently add tofu cubes and simmer for 2 minutes; this firms them slightly. Carefully drain and set aside.
2. Heat a wok over high heat. Add oil, then add ground pork. Break up with a spatula and stir-fry until browned and no pink remains, about 3 minutes. Push pork to the side.
3. Add doubanjiang and fermented black beans to the empty part of the wok. Stir-fry in the oil for 1 minute until the paste deepens in colour and the oil turns red.
4. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry everything together for 30 seconds until fragrant.
5. Pour in chicken stock, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar. Bring to a simmer.
6. Gently slide in the blanched tofu. Simmer for 3–4 minutes, tilting the wok to baste the tofu without stirring too aggressively.
7. Stir the cornstarch slurry, then pour it in while gently folding to thicken the sauce, about 1 minute.
8. Sprinkle with ground Sichuan peppercorns and spring onions. Serve immediately over steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Silken tofu breaks easily — use a spatula and tilt the wok to move it rather than stirring. Doubanjiang brands vary greatly in saltiness; taste before adding extra soy sauce. Leftovers keep for 2 days refrigerated but the tofu will release water — drain gently before reheating.
