# Red-Braised Tofu with Chili Bean Paste

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g firm tofu, cut into 2 cm thick rectangles
- 3 tbsp doubanjiang (Pixian chili bean paste)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 3 spring onions, sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 300 mL vegetable or chicken stock
- 1 tsp cornstarch mixed with 2 tbsp cold water
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ½ tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns (optional)
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Press tofu: wrap sliced tofu in a clean kitchen towel or several paper towels. Place a heavy pan on top for 10 minutes to press out excess moisture. This allows it to brown properly and absorb the braising sauce.
2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wide, non-stick skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add tofu in a single layer (work in batches). Fry undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until deep golden on the bottom, then flip and fry the other side for 2–3 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the pan over medium heat. Add doubanjiang and stir-fry for 2 minutes until the oil turns red and the paste darkens and becomes fragrant.
4. Add garlic, ginger, and white spring onion pieces. Stir-fry for 1 minute.
5. Pour in stock, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, and rice vinegar. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
6. Return browned tofu to the pan. Gently nestle pieces into the sauce. Simmer over medium-low heat for 15 minutes, turning tofu once halfway through, until the tofu has absorbed the braising liquid and turned a deep reddish-brown.
7. Stir the cornstarch slurry and pour into the sauce. Simmer for 1 minute until the sauce thickens to a glossy coat. Add Sichuan peppercorns if using.
8. Drizzle with sesame oil, scatter green spring onion tops over the top, and serve with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Pressing and pan-frying the tofu before braising is the critical step that prevents it from falling apart and allows it to absorb colour and flavour like a sponge. Extra-firm tofu is recommended for best structural results. This dish tastes better the next day as the tofu continues to absorb the braising sauce — it keeps refrigerated for up to 4 days.
