# Chicken Congee

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | lunch

## Ingredients

- 200 g jasmine rice, rinsed
- 800 g bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (2–3 pieces)
- 1.8 L chicken stock
- 400 mL water
- 30 g fresh ginger, cut into 4 thick coins
- 2 cloves garlic, smashed
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- Salt and white pepper to taste

**Toppings (serve in small bowls alongside):**
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 30 g fresh ginger, peeled and finely julienned
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tsp chili oil
- 50 g crispy fried shallots (store-bought)
- 4 century eggs, peeled and quartered (optional)

## Steps

1. Combine rinsed rice, chicken stock, water, ginger coins, and garlic in a large, heavy-bottomed saucepan. Bring to a boil over high heat.
2. Add chicken thighs. Reduce heat to medium-low, partially cover, and simmer briskly for 25 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes and scraping the bottom to prevent sticking.
3. Remove chicken thighs to a plate. The rice should be very broken down and porridge-like — if still too thin, simmer uncovered for another 5–10 minutes, stirring frequently.
4. While congee continues to cook, shred the chicken meat from the bones, discarding skin and bones. Set shredded chicken aside.
5. Remove and discard ginger coins and garlic from the congee. Stir in soy sauce, sesame oil, and season generously with salt and white pepper.
6. Return shredded chicken to the pot and stir in. Taste and adjust seasoning — congee should be well seasoned on its own.
7. Ladle into deep bowls. Invite diners to customise with toppings at the table.

## Special Notice

> Frequent stirring is important — the starch released from the rice can scorch on the bottom of the pot. The congee will continue to thicken as it sits; thin it back down with a splash of hot stock or water. Congee keeps well refrigerated for 3 days and freezes for up to 1 month; reheat with extra liquid and stir vigorously to restore the smooth texture.
