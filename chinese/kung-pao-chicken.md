# Kung Pao Chicken

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 3 tbsp soy sauce, divided
- 2 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 2 tsp cornstarch
- 3 tbsp neutral oil (e.g. canola)
- 10–12 dried Sichuan or Chinese red chilies, seeds partially removed
- 1 tsp whole Sichuan peppercorns
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 100 g dry-roasted peanuts
- 4 spring onions, cut into 2 cm pieces
- 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 2 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. In a bowl, combine chicken with 1 tbsp soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch. Toss well and set aside to marinate for 10 minutes.
2. Mix remaining 2 tbsp soy sauce, dark soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and sesame oil in a small bowl to make the sauce. Set aside.
3. Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat until smoking. Add 2 tbsp oil, then spread marinated chicken in a single layer. Sear undisturbed for 90 seconds, then stir-fry until golden and just cooked through, about 2 more minutes. Transfer to a plate.
4. Reduce heat to medium-high. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil, then add dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry for 30–45 seconds until fragrant and the chilies begin to darken; do not burn.
5. Add garlic and ginger; stir-fry for 30 seconds. Return chicken to the wok and toss to coat.
6. Pour in the sauce and toss everything together over high heat for 1 minute until the sauce thickens and glazes the chicken.
7. Add peanuts and spring onions, toss for 30 seconds, and remove from heat.
8. Serve immediately over steamed jasmine rice.

## Special Notice

> Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days; the peanuts will soften, so stir them in fresh if making ahead. Sichuan peppercorns provide the characteristic numbing heat (ma la) — do not substitute black pepper. For a milder version, reduce the dried chilies and remove all seeds.
