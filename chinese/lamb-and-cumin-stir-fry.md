# Lamb and Cumin Stir-Fry

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless lamb leg or shoulder, thinly sliced against the grain
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 2 tsp cornstarch
- 3 tbsp neutral oil, divided
- 1 large white onion, halved and thinly sliced
- 2 long green chilies, thinly sliced
- 1 long red chili, thinly sliced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp whole cumin seeds
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp chili flakes (Xinjiang-style or Korean gochugaru)
- 1 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- Salt to taste
- Small handful of fresh coriander leaves, to serve

## Steps

1. Slice lamb as thinly as possible (partially freezing it for 20 minutes makes this easier). Combine in a bowl with soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and cornstarch. Toss well and marinate for 10 minutes.
2. Heat a wok over the highest possible heat until it begins to smoke. Add 2 tbsp oil and spread the lamb in a single layer — work in two batches if needed to avoid crowding. Sear for 1 minute, then stir-fry until browned, about 2 minutes total. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok. Add onion slices and stir-fry over high heat for 2 minutes until softened and beginning to char at the edges.
4. Add garlic, green and red chilies, whole cumin seeds, and chili flakes. Stir-fry for 1 minute until cumin seeds are toasted and fragrant.
5. Return lamb to the wok. Add ground cumin, light soy sauce, and dark soy sauce. Toss everything together vigorously for 1–2 minutes over high heat.
6. Taste and adjust salt. Remove from heat.
7. Transfer to a serving plate and scatter fresh coriander over the top. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> This Xinjiang-style dish (zi ran yang rou) relies on bold, unapologetic cumin — don't reduce it. Whole cumin seeds should pop and toast in the hot oil before the lamb returns; this is what gives the dish its characteristic smoky aroma. Pairs well with flatbread, steamed rice, or even just on its own as a shared plate.
