# Steamed Fish with Ginger and Scallion

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1 whole sea bass or snapper, about 1 kg (or 4 fillets, 150 g each)
- 30 g fresh ginger, peeled and cut into fine matchsticks
- 6 spring onions (scallions), cut into 6 cm lengths, then finely julienned
- 3 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp sugar
- 4 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 small red chili, thinly sliced (optional)
- Small handful of fresh coriander leaves
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. If using a whole fish, score both sides diagonally to the bone at 3 cm intervals — this ensures even cooking. Pat dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt inside the cavity.
2. Set up a steaming rack over a wok or large pot with about 4 cm of water. Bring to a vigorous boil.
3. Place fish on a heatproof plate. Scatter half the ginger inside the cavity (or over fillets) and under the fish. Place on the steaming rack.
4. Cover tightly and steam a whole fish for 10–12 minutes (fillets 6–8 minutes) until the flesh just flakes and the eyes turn white.
5. While the fish steams, combine soy sauce, dark soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, and sugar in a small bowl. Stir until sugar dissolves.
6. Carefully remove the plate from the steamer. Pour off and discard any accumulated liquid from the plate.
7. Scatter remaining ginger and spring onion julienne over the fish. Pour the soy mixture evenly over the top.
8. Heat neutral oil in a small pan over high heat until shimmering and just beginning to smoke. Carefully pour the hot oil over the ginger and scallions — it will sizzle dramatically and release their fragrance. Drizzle with sesame oil and garnish with chili and coriander. Serve immediately with rice.

## Special Notice

> The hot-oil pour is the defining technique — it must be done at the last second before serving to release maximum fragrance from the aromatics. Use the freshest fish possible; this dish is a showcase of quality ingredients. Do not steam fish ahead of time; serve straight from the steamer.
