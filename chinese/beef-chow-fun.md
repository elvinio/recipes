# Beef Chow Fun

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g fresh flat rice noodles (he fen), about 1 cm wide — or dried, soaked per package
- 400 g flank steak or beef sirloin, thinly sliced against the grain
- 200 g bean sprouts
- 6 spring onions, cut into 5 cm batons
- 3 tbsp neutral oil with high smoke point (e.g. avocado or refined peanut)
- **Beef marinade:**
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 2 tsp cornstarch
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp baking soda (tenderising)
- **Sauce:**
- 3 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- ½ tsp sugar
- ½ tsp white pepper

## Steps

1. Combine beef with all marinade ingredients. Toss thoroughly and marinate for 10–15 minutes. The baking soda is key for the restaurant-style tender texture (velveting).
2. If using fresh rice noodles, gently separate strands by hand — they tend to clump. If refrigerated, microwave for 30 seconds to loosen. Mix all sauce ingredients in a small bowl.
3. Heat a wok over the highest possible heat until it is visibly smoking. Add 2 tbsp oil. Spread beef slices in a single layer and sear undisturbed for 60 seconds. Stir-fry for 1 more minute until just cooked through. Transfer to a plate, leaving all juices and oil in the wok.
4. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the smoking wok. Add noodles and spread them out. Press them against the wok surface and let them sit undisturbed for 30 seconds to develop colour and slight char.
5. Pour half the sauce over the noodles. Toss gently — rice noodles are delicate and will tear. Toss and press in intervals rather than stirring continuously.
6. Add spring onion batons and bean sprouts. Toss for 1 minute.
7. Return beef to the wok, pour remaining sauce over everything, and toss for 30 seconds to combine. The noodles should be coated in a deep mahogany colour from the dark soy.
8. Plate immediately and serve.

## Special Notice

> Beef chow fun is one of the most heat-dependent Chinese dishes — a domestic stovetop will struggle to fully replicate restaurant "wok hei". To compensate, keep ingredients at room temperature, heat the wok for at least 2 minutes before adding any oil, and never crowd the wok. Rinse the baking soda off the beef before marinating if you prefer a less springy texture. Fresh rice noodles are far superior to dried for this dish.
