# Dan Dan Noodles with Ground Pork

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g dried thin wheat noodles (or fresh thin egg noodles)
- 300 g ground pork
- 2 tbsp ya cai (Yibin preserved vegetables) or finely chopped kimchi as a substitute
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp soy sauce, divided
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 4 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini)
- 2 tbsp chili oil (with sediment)
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar
- 120 mL (½ cup) hot chicken stock or pasta water
- 1 tsp ground Sichuan peppercorns
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp roasted peanuts, roughly chopped

## Steps

1. Cook noodles according to package instructions. Reserve 240 mL (1 cup) of cooking water, then drain. Rinse briefly under cold water and drain again.
2. Heat a wok over high heat. Add oil, then ground pork. Stir-fry, breaking up lumps, until the pork is browned and slightly crispy at the edges, about 4 minutes.
3. Add ya cai (or substitute), 2 tbsp soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine to the pork. Stir-fry for 1 minute until fragrant. Set aside.
4. In a large bowl, whisk together sesame paste, chili oil, light soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, garlic, ginger, and ground Sichuan peppercorns. Gradually whisk in hot stock or reserved pasta water until a smooth, pourable sauce forms — it should coat a spoon.
5. Divide noodles between four bowls. Spoon the sesame sauce generously over each bowl.
6. Top with a portion of the pork and ya cai mixture.
7. Garnish with spring onions and crushed peanuts.
8. Serve immediately; toss thoroughly at the table before eating.

## Special Notice

> The sauce thickens as it sits — if it becomes too thick, loosen it with a spoonful of warm water. Authentic ya cai gives a unique fermented, slightly tangy flavour; seek it out at Asian grocery stores for the most traditional result. Sichuan peppercorn quantity can be adjusted from 1/2 tsp (subtle) to 2 tsp (full numbing effect).
