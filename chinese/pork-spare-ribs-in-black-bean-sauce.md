# Pork Spare Ribs in Black Bean Sauce

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1 kg pork spare ribs, cut into 4–5 cm pieces (ask your butcher, or use a cleaver)
- 3 tbsp fermented black beans (douchi), rinsed and roughly chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp fresh ginger, minced
- 2 long red chilies, deseeded and thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp cornstarch
- ½ tsp white pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 120 mL (½ cup) water or chicken stock
- 2 spring onions, sliced, to garnish
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Blanch rib pieces in boiling water for 3 minutes to remove impurities. Drain and pat dry.
2. In a large bowl, combine ribs with fermented black beans, garlic, ginger, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, oyster sauce, Shaoxing wine, sugar, sesame oil, cornstarch, and white pepper. Mix thoroughly and let marinate for 10 minutes.
3. Set the electric pressure cooker to sauté/browning mode. Add neutral oil. In batches, brown the ribs on all sides for 2–3 minutes per batch. Remove and set aside.
4. Add chilies to the pot and sauté for 30 seconds. Return all ribs to the pot. Add stock or water and scrape up any browned bits.
5. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 18 minutes. Allow natural pressure release for 5 minutes, then manually release remaining pressure.
6. Open lid. If the sauce is thin, set to sauté and reduce for 3–5 minutes, stirring, until it thickens to a glossy coating consistency.
7. Transfer ribs to a serving dish and spoon sauce over the top.
8. Garnish with spring onions and serve with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Fermented black beans are the soul of this dish — do not substitute black bean paste (which is a different, processed product) as it will make the sauce too thick and sweet. Rinsing the black beans before use reduces excessive saltiness. This dish is a classic dim sum item (pai guat) traditionally steamed; the pressure cooker version yields fall-off-the-bone ribs in a fraction of the time with a similar depth of flavour.
