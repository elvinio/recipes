# Garlic and Ginger Steamed Clams

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg fresh clams (littleneck, Manila, or pipis), scrubbed clean
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 30 g fresh ginger, finely julienned or minced
- 2 long red chilies, thinly sliced (optional, for gentle heat)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 3 tbsp Shaoxing rice wine
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tsp dark soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) chicken stock or water
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- Handful of fresh coriander leaves

## Steps

1. Soak clams in cold salted water (1 tbsp salt per litre) for 20 minutes to purge sand. Drain, rinse thoroughly, and discard any clams that are cracked or do not close when tapped.
2. Heat a wok or large lidded skillet over high heat. Add neutral oil. When shimmering, add garlic and ginger. Stir-fry for 1 minute until fragrant and golden at the edges, but not burnt.
3. Add chilies if using and stir-fry for 15 seconds.
4. Add Shaoxing wine — it will bubble vigorously. Let it reduce for 30 seconds.
5. Add soy sauce, dark soy sauce, sugar, and stock. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
6. Add all clams in a single layer (as much as possible). Cover immediately with a lid and cook on high heat for 3–5 minutes, shaking the pan once halfway through, until all clams have opened.
7. Discard any clams that have not opened after 6 minutes. Drizzle with sesame oil.
8. Transfer to a wide serving bowl. Pour all the cooking broth over the clams. Scatter with spring onions and coriander. Serve immediately with crusty bread or steamed rice to soak up the broth.

## Special Notice

> Fresh, live clams are essential — do not use clams that smell strongly of ammonia or that remain open and unresponsive before cooking. The purging soak step should not be skipped; gritty broth ruins the dish. The aromatic cooking broth is just as prized as the clams — serve with bread or rice specifically to enjoy it. Clams do not reheat well; serve and eat immediately.
