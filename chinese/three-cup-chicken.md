# Three Cup Chicken

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks), chopped into 4 cm pieces
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) sesame oil
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) Shaoxing rice wine
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) soy sauce
- 2 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sugar (or rock sugar)
- 10 cloves garlic, peeled and left whole
- 30 g fresh ginger, cut into 5 mm coins
- 4 dried red chilies
- 1 tsp white pepper
- Large handful (about 25 g) fresh Thai basil leaves
- 800 g cooked jasmine rice, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat a clay pot or a heavy-bottomed casserole over medium-high heat. Add sesame oil; when it shimmers, add garlic cloves and ginger slices. Fry, stirring occasionally, for 2 minutes until lightly golden and fragrant.
2. Increase heat to high. Add chicken pieces in a single layer (work in batches if needed). Sear on all sides until golden brown, about 5–6 minutes total.
3. Push chicken to the sides. Add dried chilies to the centre and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
4. Pour in Shaoxing wine, soy sauce, dark soy sauce, and sugar. Stir to combine. The liquid should come about one-third of the way up the chicken.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and simmer for 20 minutes, turning the chicken halfway through.
6. Uncover, increase heat to high, and cook for 5–8 minutes, stirring frequently, until the sauce reduces to a thick, glossy glaze that coats the chicken.
7. Remove from heat and immediately add the fresh basil leaves. Toss vigorously — residual heat will wilt them in about 30 seconds.
8. Serve straight from the pot with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> The three "cups" (sesame oil, rice wine, soy sauce) are measured in equal parts — this ratio is the foundation of the dish. Fresh Thai basil is non-negotiable; Italian basil is too delicate and will taste wrong. The chicken is intentionally bold and salty — it is meant to be eaten with plain rice, which balances the intensity.
