# Tofu and Mushroom Hot and Sour Soup

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Chinese | dinner

## Ingredients

- 300 g firm tofu, cut into thin matchsticks or 1 cm cubes
- 100 g shiitake mushrooms, stems removed, caps thinly sliced
- 50 g dried wood ear mushrooms, rehydrated in warm water 15 minutes, drained and sliced
- 80 g bamboo shoots (canned, drained), cut into thin strips
- 1.2 L chicken or vegetable stock
- 3 tbsp rice vinegar (adjust to taste)
- 2 tbsp light soy sauce
- 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
- 2 tsp white pepper (the key heat source — be generous)
- 1 tsp chili oil or chili paste
- 3 tbsp cornstarch mixed with 4 tbsp cold water
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
- Salt to taste

## Steps

1. Bring stock to a boil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat.
2. Add shiitake mushrooms, wood ear mushrooms, bamboo shoots, and tofu. Return to a boil, then reduce heat to a steady simmer. Cook for 5 minutes.
3. Add soy sauce, dark soy sauce, vinegar, white pepper, and chili oil. Stir and taste — adjust vinegar and pepper to achieve the balance of hot and sour you prefer.
4. Stir the cornstarch slurry, then pour it into the soup in a slow, steady stream while stirring constantly. Simmer for 1–2 minutes until the soup thickens to a light, silky consistency.
5. Reduce heat to low. While stirring the soup in a slow circle, pour the beaten eggs in a thin, steady stream from a height. The eggs will cook into delicate ribbons — stop stirring once all the egg is added and let it set for 15 seconds.
6. Remove from heat. Drizzle with sesame oil and scatter spring onions over the top.
7. Taste for salt and serve immediately, very hot.

## Special Notice

> White pepper, not chili, provides the traditional "hot" in hot and sour soup — use a freshly ground amount that makes you sneeze slightly; it should have a back-of-the-throat heat. Pour the egg slowly and with the soup barely moving to create fine, silky ribbons rather than scrambled clumps. This soup is best eaten fresh; the cornstarch thickening breaks down on reheating, but it can be re-thickened with a small additional cornstarch slurry.
