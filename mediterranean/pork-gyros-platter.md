# Pork Gyros Platter

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g pork shoulder, sliced very thinly (5 mm) — ask your butcher, or freeze for 30 minutes for easier slicing
- 4 large pita flatbreads
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**For the gyros marinade:**
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**For the tzatziki:**
- 250 g full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 Lebanese cucumber (about 150 g), grated and squeezed dry
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp fresh dill, finely chopped
- Salt to taste

**Platter accompaniments:**
- 2 ripe tomatoes, sliced
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 head iceberg lettuce, shredded
- Lemon wedges

## Steps

1. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large bowl. Add pork slices and toss well. Marinate for at least 15 minutes at room temperature.
2. Meanwhile, make tzatziki: combine yogurt, squeezed cucumber, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and dill. Season generously with salt. Refrigerate.
3. Preheat oven to 200°C. Pile marinated pork slices onto a lightly oiled baking tray in a compact mound (stacking them loosely). Roast for 15 minutes.
4. Remove tray from oven. Switch oven to grill/broil setting at maximum heat (or use the stovetop). Transfer pork to a hot, lightly oiled skillet over high heat. Sear the pork, stirring and pressing it down, for 3–4 minutes until deeply caramelised and slightly crisp at the edges.
5. Warm pita breads directly on the stovetop over medium heat for 30 seconds per side until pliable and lightly charred.
6. Arrange pita, pork, tzatziki, tomatoes, red onion, and lettuce on a large platter. Each diner assembles their own: spread tzatziki on pita, top with pork and salad, and squeeze over lemon.
7. Serve immediately while the pork is hot and sizzling.

## Special Notice

> The stovetop finishing step after oven-roasting is what gives gyros its signature crispy, caramelised edges — do not skip it. Thin slicing is key; a sharp knife and briefly frozen meat makes this much easier. Gyros meat does not keep well once cooked, so serve immediately.
