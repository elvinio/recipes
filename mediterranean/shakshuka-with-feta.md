# Shakshuka with Feta

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mediterranean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 800 g canned crushed tomatoes (two 400 g tins)
- 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1–2 tsp harissa paste (adjust to heat preference)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 120 g feta cheese, crumbled
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley or cilantro, to serve
- Crusty bread or pita, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large, wide skillet or shallow pan with a lid over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper and cook for 5–6 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
2. Add garlic, harissa, cumin, paprika, and coriander. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Pour in crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and stir to combine. Simmer for 8–10 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate on the surface.
4. Taste the sauce and adjust seasoning and heat level. The sauce should be bold and well-seasoned.
5. Using a spoon, create 8 small wells in the sauce. Crack one egg into each well. Season each egg with a pinch of salt and pepper.
6. Scatter feta crumbles over the sauce and around the eggs. Cover the pan with a lid and cook over medium-low heat for 5–7 minutes, until egg whites are just set but yolks remain runny.
7. Remove from heat immediately to prevent overcooking. Scatter fresh herbs over the top and serve straight from the pan with plenty of bread.

## Special Notice

> Watch the eggs closely in the final minutes — they go from runny to overcooked quickly. For a smokier flavour, substitute smoked paprika for regular paprika. The sauce base can be made up to 3 days ahead and stored in the fridge; simply reheat and poach the eggs fresh.
