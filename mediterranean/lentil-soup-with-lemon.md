# Lentil Soup with Lemon

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 300 g red lentils, rinsed until water runs clear
- 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), peeled and diced
- 1.2 L vegetable or chicken stock
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Juice of 2 lemons (about 80 mL), divided
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**To serve:**
- Olive oil, for drizzling
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley
- Crusty bread

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion and carrots and cook for 6–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and the onion is lightly golden.
2. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, and turmeric. Cook for 1–2 minutes until very fragrant.
3. Add rinsed lentils and stir to coat in the spiced oil for 1 minute.
4. Pour in stock and crushed tomatoes. Season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20–22 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and have broken down.
5. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup partially — blending about half of it creates a creamy texture while leaving some texture. Alternatively, transfer half the soup to a blender, blend, and return to the pot.
6. Stir in half the lemon juice and taste. Adjust seasoning and add more lemon juice as desired — the soup should be bright and well-seasoned.
7. Ladle into bowls. Top with a drizzle of good olive oil, a pinch of smoked paprika, and fresh parsley. Serve with crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> Red lentils break down quickly, so watch the soup carefully after 18 minutes to avoid over-thickening. If the soup thickens too much upon standing, stir in a little hot water. Leftovers keep for up to 5 days and freeze very well for up to 3 months.
