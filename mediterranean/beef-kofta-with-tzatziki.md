# Beef Kofta with Tzatziki

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g ground beef (15–20% fat)
- 1 small yellow onion, grated and excess moisture squeezed out
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 30 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp olive oil, for cooking
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 4 flatbreads or pita breads, to serve
- Sliced tomato, cucumber, and red onion, to serve

**For the tzatziki:**
- 250 g full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 Lebanese cucumber (about 150 g), grated and squeezed dry
- 2 cloves garlic, finely minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp fresh dill or mint, chopped
- Salt to taste

## Steps

1. Make tzatziki first: combine Greek yogurt, squeezed cucumber, garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and herbs. Season with salt. Refrigerate until ready to serve.
2. In a large bowl, combine beef mince, grated onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, cinnamon, and parsley. Season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Mix with your hands until well combined but do not overwork. Divide mixture into 12 equal portions and shape each into a short, oval sausage, about 8 cm long.
4. Heat a large skillet or griddle pan over medium-high heat. Brush with olive oil.
5. Cook koftas in batches without crowding, for 3–4 minutes per side, turning once, until browned on all sides and cooked through to 70°C internal temperature.
6. Rest koftas on a plate loosely covered with foil for 3 minutes.
7. Warm flatbreads in the pan for 30 seconds per side. Serve koftas with flatbreads, tzatziki, and sliced vegetables.

## Special Notice

> Squeezing the moisture from the grated onion is important — excess liquid will prevent the koftas from holding their shape. The kofta mixture can be shaped and refrigerated up to 24 hours ahead, making dinner assembly very fast.
