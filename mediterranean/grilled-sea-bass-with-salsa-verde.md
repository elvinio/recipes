# Grilled Sea Bass with Salsa Verde

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 sea bass fillets (about 160 g each), skin scored with a sharp knife
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Lemon wedges, to serve

**For the salsa verde:**
- 30 g fresh flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- 15 g fresh basil leaves, finely chopped
- 15 g fresh mint leaves, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp capers, drained and roughly chopped
- 4 anchovy fillets in oil, finely minced
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 80 mL extra virgin olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Make salsa verde first: combine all herbs, capers, anchovies, and garlic in a bowl. Stir in mustard, red wine vinegar, and olive oil. Mix well and season with salt and pepper. The salsa should be bright, punchy, and slightly acidic. Set aside at room temperature.
2. Pat sea bass fillets completely dry with paper towel. Brush both sides with olive oil and season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat a large non-stick or cast iron skillet over high heat until very hot — a drop of water should evaporate instantly.
4. Place fillets skin-side down in the pan. Press down firmly with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent the skin from curling. Cook for 3–4 minutes without moving until the skin is deeply golden and crisp.
5. Carefully flip fillets and cook for a further 1–2 minutes on the flesh side until just cooked through — the flesh should be opaque and flake easily at the thickest part.
6. Transfer fillets to warmed plates, skin-side up to preserve crispness.
7. Spoon salsa verde generously beside and partially over each fillet. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Scoring and thoroughly drying the skin is the secret to perfectly crisp sea bass skin. Do not move the fish during the first 3 minutes — patience here is rewarded. The salsa verde keeps for up to 2 days refrigerated; bring to room temperature before serving.
