# Greek Lemon Chicken with Orzo

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 300 g orzo pasta
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 lemons (zest and juice, about 80 mL juice)
- 800 mL chicken stock
- 400 mL water
- 60 mL olive oil
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 60 g baby spinach
- 40 g Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh parsley, roughly chopped, to serve

## Steps

1. Season chicken pieces generously with salt, pepper, 1 tsp oregano, and cumin. Toss to coat evenly.
2. Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet over high heat. Add chicken in a single layer and sear for 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add onion to the same pan and cook for 3 minutes until softened. Add garlic and remaining 1 tsp oregano and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add orzo to the pan and stir for 1 minute to lightly toast the pasta in the aromatics.
5. Pour in chicken stock, water, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Stir well, scraping up any browned bits from the pan.
6. Return chicken to the pan. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered for 12–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until orzo is al dente and has absorbed most of the liquid.
7. Stir in baby spinach and olives. Cook for 1–2 minutes until spinach wilts.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately, topped with fresh parsley and an extra squeeze of lemon if desired.

## Special Notice

> Leftovers thicken considerably as the orzo continues to absorb liquid — add a splash of stock when reheating. For extra richness, finish with 30 g of crumbled feta stirred through just before serving.
