# Pork Souvlaki with Pita

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g pork shoulder or pork neck, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 60 mL olive oil
- Juice of 1 large lemon (about 40 mL)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 4 large pita breads
- 1 large tomato, sliced
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/2 head iceberg lettuce, shredded

**For the tzatziki:**
- 200 g full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 Lebanese cucumber (about 150 g), grated and squeezed dry
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Salt to taste

## Steps

1. Combine olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, oregano, paprika, and thyme in a bowl. Add pork cubes, season generously with salt and pepper, and toss to coat. Marinate for at least 20 minutes at room temperature (or up to 12 hours refrigerated).
2. Make tzatziki: combine Greek yogurt, squeezed cucumber, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice. Season with salt and refrigerate.
3. Thread pork cubes onto 8 metal skewers (or pre-soaked wooden skewers), packing them snugly together.
4. Heat a large griddle pan or skillet over high heat until very hot. Cook skewers for 3–4 minutes per side, turning two to three times, until nicely charred and cooked through to 70°C internal temperature.
5. Rest skewers for 3 minutes loosely covered with foil.
6. Warm pita breads in the pan for 30–45 seconds per side.
7. To serve: spread tzatziki over each pita, add lettuce, tomato, and red onion, then slide pork from skewers on top. Fold or roll the pita.

## Special Notice

> Pork neck is preferred over loin as its higher fat content keeps the meat juicy during high-heat cooking. If marinating in the fridge, bring the pork to room temperature for 15 minutes before cooking for more even results.
