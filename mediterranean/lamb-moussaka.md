# Lamb Moussaka

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g ground lamb
- 2 medium eggplants (about 700 g total), sliced 1 cm thick
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp ground allspice
- 60 mL olive oil, divided, plus extra for brushing
- 500 mL whole milk
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 40 g plain flour
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 60 g grated kefalotyri or Parmesan cheese, divided
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Brush eggplant slices on both sides with olive oil and season with salt. Arrange on two lined baking trays and roast for 18–20 minutes, flipping once, until golden and tender. Reduce oven to 200°C.
2. Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 4 minutes. Add garlic, cinnamon, and allspice and cook 1 minute more.
3. Add lamb mince, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook for 6–8 minutes until browned and cooked through. Stir in tomato paste and cook 1 minute, then add crushed tomatoes. Simmer for 8 minutes until sauce is thick. Season well and set aside.
4. Make béchamel: melt butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 1 minute. Gradually add milk, whisking constantly, until the sauce is smooth and thick, about 5 minutes. Remove from heat, let cool slightly, then whisk in eggs and half the cheese. Season with salt and pepper.
5. Lightly oil a 20×30 cm baking dish. Layer half the eggplant on the base. Spread all the meat sauce on top. Layer remaining eggplant over the meat.
6. Pour béchamel evenly over the top and smooth with a spatula. Scatter remaining cheese over the surface.
7. Bake at 200°C for 25–28 minutes until the top is deep golden and the dish is bubbling at the edges.
8. Rest for at least 10 minutes before slicing and serving.

## Special Notice

> Moussaka reheats beautifully — cover with foil and warm at 180°C for 15 minutes. The dish benefits greatly from resting after baking, as it firms up for cleaner slices. For deeper flavour, make the meat sauce a day ahead and refrigerate overnight.
