# Lamb Stuffed Peppers

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 large bell peppers (red, yellow, or a mix), tops cut off, seeds and membranes removed
- 400 g ground lamb
- 150 g long-grain white rice, rinsed
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes, divided
- 100 mL water
- 2 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 3 tbsp olive oil, divided
- 60 g pine nuts, lightly toasted
- 30 g raisins or currants
- 60 g feta cheese, crumbled
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh mint or parsley, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 190°C. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 4 minutes. Add garlic, oregano, cinnamon, and cumin and cook for 1 minute.
2. Add lamb mince, breaking it up. Cook for 5–6 minutes until browned. Stir in half the crushed tomatoes (200 g), rice, pine nuts, and raisins. Season generously with salt and pepper. Stir well and remove from heat.
3. Combine remaining crushed tomatoes with 100 mL water in the base of a deep baking dish that fits the peppers snugly.
4. Season the inside of each pepper with a pinch of salt. Stand peppers upright in the baking dish and fill each one generously with the lamb and rice mixture, packing lightly — the rice will expand as it cooks. Replace the pepper tops.
5. Drizzle remaining 1 tbsp olive oil over the peppers. Cover the baking dish tightly with foil.
6. Bake for 35–40 minutes until rice is fully cooked and tender and the peppers have softened.
7. Remove foil, scatter feta over the peppers, and return to oven uncovered for 5 minutes. Rest for 5 minutes before serving, scattered with fresh mint or parsley.

## Special Notice

> Choose peppers with flat bases so they stand upright without tipping. If the rice is still slightly firm at 35 minutes, add 2 tbsp water to the base of the dish, re-cover, and cook for a further 5–10 minutes. Leftovers keep for up to 3 days refrigerated and reheat well.
