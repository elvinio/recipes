# Shrimp Saganaki

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined, tails on
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 200 g feta cheese, broken into large chunks
- 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp chilli flakes (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 60 mL ouzo or dry white wine
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley, to serve
- Crusty bread or pita, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large heavy skillet or saganaki pan over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 3–4 minutes until softened.
2. Add garlic, chilli flakes, oregano, and paprika. Cook for 1 minute, stirring, until fragrant.
3. Pour in the ouzo (or white wine) and let it bubble and reduce for 1 minute.
4. Add crushed tomatoes, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 6–8 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
5. Pat shrimp dry and nestle them into the tomato sauce in a single layer. Cook for 2 minutes, then flip each shrimp and scatter feta chunks over the top.
6. Cover the pan and cook for a further 2–3 minutes until shrimp are pink, cooked through, and feta has softened.
7. Remove from heat and scatter fresh basil or parsley over the top. Serve immediately, straight from the pan, with plenty of crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> Shrimp saganaki is best eaten immediately — shrimp toughen if left in hot sauce. Use the freshest shrimp available for the best flavour. If using ouzo, the anise note mellows considerably once cooked.
