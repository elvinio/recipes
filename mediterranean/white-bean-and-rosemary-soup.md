# White Bean and Rosemary Soup

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 x 400 g cans cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 large yellow onion, finely diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), peeled and diced
- 2 stalks celery, finely sliced
- 3 sprigs fresh rosemary (or 1.5 tsp dried rosemary)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 L vegetable or chicken stock
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 60 mL extra virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 30 g Parmesan rind (optional but adds great depth)
- Fresh rosemary sprigs and extra olive oil, to serve
- Crusty bread, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat 3 tbsp olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook for 7–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and the onion is lightly golden.
2. Add garlic, paprika, and rosemary sprigs. Cook for 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add crushed tomatoes, stock, bay leaves, and Parmesan rind (if using). Season with salt and pepper.
4. Add cannellini beans and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are very tender and the soup has thickened.
5. Remove rosemary sprigs, bay leaves, and Parmesan rind. Using an immersion blender, partially blend the soup — blend about one-third to create a creamy base while leaving plenty of whole beans and vegetables intact.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning. The soup should be hearty, savoury, and fragrant with rosemary.
7. Ladle into bowls and finish each serving with a generous drizzle of the remaining extra virgin olive oil and a small fresh rosemary sprig. Serve with thick slices of crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> Adding a Parmesan rind during simmering contributes a remarkable savoury depth — save rinds in a zip-lock bag in the freezer. The soup thickens considerably upon refrigeration; thin with additional stock when reheating. It keeps for up to 5 days and freezes well for up to 3 months.
