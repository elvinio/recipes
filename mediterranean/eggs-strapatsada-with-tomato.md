# Eggs Strapatsada with Tomato

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mediterranean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 600 g ripe tomatoes, roughly grated on a box grater (discard skins), or 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 1 small yellow onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1/2 tsp sweet paprika
- Pinch of sugar (if tomatoes are acidic)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 60 g feta cheese, crumbled (optional but traditional)
- Fresh oregano or basil, to serve
- 4 thick slices crusty bread or 4 pita breads, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large non-stick or stainless-steel skillet over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 4–5 minutes until softened and translucent.
2. Add garlic and oregano, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add grated tomato (or crushed tomatoes) and paprika. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar if the tomatoes are very acidic. Simmer over medium heat for 10–12 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thick and most of the liquid has evaporated — this step is crucial for the right texture.
4. Crack eggs directly into the thickened tomato sauce. Gently stir and fold the eggs into the sauce with a wooden spoon or spatula, breaking up the yolks.
5. Cook over medium-low heat, gently folding and stirring continuously, for 3–4 minutes until eggs are just set — they should be creamy, soft, and slightly custardy, not dry or rubbery. Remove from heat while still slightly underdone as carryover heat will finish them.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Scatter feta over the top if using.
7. Serve immediately on or alongside thick slices of toasted or plain crusty bread, topped with fresh herbs.

## Special Notice

> Strapatsada is about the sauce-to-egg ratio — the tomato sauce must be thick before adding eggs or the result will be watery. The eggs should be creamy and soft; err on the side of undercooking and rest in the pan off the heat for 1 minute. This dish does not keep well and should be eaten fresh.
