# Tuna Nicoise Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mediterranean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 320 g high-quality canned tuna in olive oil, drained
- 400 g small waxy potatoes, boiled and halved (can use pre-cooked)
- 200 g green beans, blanched and trimmed
- 4 large eggs, hard-boiled and halved
- 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 80 g Kalamata olives, pitted
- 60 g anchovies in oil (optional)
- 2 baby cos lettuces, leaves separated
- 4 tbsp capers, drained

**For the dressing:**
- 60 mL extra virgin olive oil
- 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 small shallot, very finely minced
- 1 tsp honey
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. If boiling potatoes and eggs fresh: place potatoes in a pot of cold salted water, bring to a boil, and cook for 12–15 minutes until tender. In a separate pot, hard-boil eggs for 9 minutes, then transfer immediately to ice water. Blanch green beans in boiling salted water for 2 minutes, then transfer to ice water. Drain all and set aside.
2. Make dressing: whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, shallot, and honey. Season with salt and pepper.
3. While potatoes are still warm, toss them with 1 tbsp of the dressing and a pinch of salt.
4. Arrange cos lettuce leaves as the base in four bowls or on a large platter.
5. Arrange potatoes, green beans, cherry tomatoes, and olives over the lettuce in separate sections.
6. Place halved eggs around the bowl. Break tuna into large chunks and place in the centre.
7. Drape anchovies over the tuna (if using), scatter capers over everything, and drizzle the remaining dressing generously over the bowl. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> This recipe is categorised as no-cook because all components can be assembled from pre-cooked or canned ingredients. The dressing can be made up to 3 days ahead and stored in a jar in the fridge. Use the best-quality tuna you can find — it makes a significant difference.
