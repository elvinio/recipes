# Chickpea and Spinach Stew

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 x 400 g cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 200 g baby spinach
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 200 mL vegetable stock
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- 1/2 tsp chilli flakes
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- 3 tbsp olive oil
- Juice of 1 lemon (about 40 mL)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh cilantro or parsley, to serve
- Crusty bread, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat. Add onion and cook for 5–6 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
2. Add garlic, cumin, coriander, paprika, chilli flakes, and turmeric. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring constantly, until very fragrant.
3. Add crushed tomatoes and vegetable stock. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer. Cook for 8 minutes until the sauce thickens.
4. Add chickpeas to the pot and stir to combine. Simmer for 12–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chickpeas have absorbed the flavours and the sauce is thick and glossy.
5. Using the back of a spoon or a potato masher, roughly mash about one-quarter of the chickpeas to create a thicker, creamier texture.
6. Stir in baby spinach in two batches, allowing each batch to wilt before adding the next, about 1–2 minutes total.
7. Remove from heat and stir in lemon juice. Taste and adjust seasoning — the stew should be well-seasoned and slightly tangy. Serve with fresh cilantro and crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> This stew thickens as it sits — add a splash of stock or water when reheating. It keeps well in the fridge for up to 4 days and freezes beautifully. For a heartier meal, poach an egg directly in the stew in step 6.
