# Tofu and Olive Tapenade Bowl

> Serves 4 | 10 minutes | Mediterranean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 400 g firm tofu, drained and patted dry, cut into 1.5 cm cubes
- 4 thick slices sourdough or rustic country bread (or 4 pita breads)

**For the tapenade:**
- 180 g Kalamata olives, pitted
- 2 tbsp capers, drained
- 2 anchovy fillets in oil (optional, omit for vegan)
- 1 small clove garlic
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 tsp dried)
- Black pepper to taste

**For the bowl:**
- 1 large cucumber (about 250 g), diced
- 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1/2 red onion, finely sliced
- 60 g sun-dried tomatoes in oil, roughly chopped
- 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley, to serve

## Steps

1. Make tapenade: place olives, capers, anchovies (if using), garlic, lemon juice, olive oil, and thyme in a food processor or blender. Pulse 8–10 times until a chunky paste forms — do not over-blend. Season with black pepper. Taste and adjust lemon juice. Transfer to a bowl.
2. In a separate bowl, combine cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and sun-dried tomatoes. Dress with olive oil and red wine vinegar. Season with salt and pepper and toss gently.
3. Pat tofu cubes dry once more. Season lightly with salt and a drizzle of olive oil and toss to coat.
4. Divide tofu between four serving bowls. Spoon the dressed vegetable salad generously alongside the tofu.
5. Add a thick slice of bread to each bowl or plate. Spread a generous spoonful of tapenade directly onto the bread.
6. Scatter fresh basil or parsley over the bowls and finish with a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> For the best texture, press the tofu under a heavy plate for 15 minutes before serving to remove excess moisture — this is especially rewarding if you have time. The tapenade keeps for up to 1 week refrigerated in a sealed jar, covered with a thin layer of olive oil.
