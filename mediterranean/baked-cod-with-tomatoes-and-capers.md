# Baked Cod with Tomatoes and Capers

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 cod fillets (about 180 g each), skin-on or skinless
- 500 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp capers, drained and rinsed
- 80 mL dry white wine
- 60 mL olive oil, divided
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp sweet paprika
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 30 g pitted Kalamata olives, halved
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 210°C. Pat cod fillets dry with paper towel and season on both sides with salt, pepper, and paprika.
2. In a large oven-safe baking dish, combine cherry tomatoes, red onion, garlic, capers, olives, dried thyme, lemon zest, white wine, and 3 tbsp olive oil. Season with salt and pepper and toss to combine.
3. Roast the tomato mixture for 15 minutes until tomatoes have softened and started to collapse.
4. Remove dish from oven. Nestle the seasoned cod fillets into the tomato mixture, pressing them down gently so they are partially submerged.
5. Drizzle remaining 1 tbsp olive oil over the fish. Return to the oven and bake for a further 12–15 minutes, depending on thickness, until fish flakes easily when tested with a fork.
6. Remove from oven and rest for 2 minutes. Spoon the tomato and caper sauce generously over each fillet.
7. Scatter fresh parsley over the top and serve directly from the dish with crusty bread to mop up the juices.

## Special Notice

> Cod is delicate — avoid overbaking as it dries out quickly. The fish is done when it just turns opaque in the centre. Halibut or hake make excellent substitutes if cod is unavailable.
