# Chicken Shawarma Rice Bowl

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- 300 g basmati rice, rinsed
- 560 mL water or chicken stock
- 2 tbsp olive oil

**For the shawarma marinade:**
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground turmeric
- 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
- 1/2 tsp ground allspice
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 1 tsp salt

**For the garlic sauce:**
- 150 g full-fat Greek yogurt
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Salt to taste

**To serve:**
- 1/4 red cabbage (about 200 g), finely shredded
- 2 tomatoes, diced
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Lemon wedges

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Combine all marinade ingredients in a large bowl. Add chicken thighs and toss well to coat. Marinate for 10 minutes while the oven heats.
2. Cook the rice: bring 560 mL water or stock to a boil in a saucepan. Add rinsed rice and 1/2 tsp salt. Stir once, cover tightly, and reduce heat to very low. Cook for 12 minutes, then remove from heat and steam covered for 5 minutes.
3. Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in an oven-safe skillet over high heat. Add chicken thighs and sear for 3 minutes without moving until a dark crust forms.
4. Flip chicken and transfer the skillet to the oven. Roast for 12–14 minutes until internal temperature reaches 74°C and chicken is cooked through.
5. Rest chicken for 5 minutes, then slice thinly against the grain.
6. Make garlic sauce: stir together yogurt, garlic, and lemon juice. Season with salt.
7. Fluff rice with a fork and divide between four bowls. Top with sliced chicken, shredded cabbage, and diced tomato. Drizzle garlic sauce generously over the top and scatter with fresh parsley. Serve with lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> The key to deeply flavoured shawarma is the combination of warm spices and the high-heat sear before oven finishing. Chicken thighs are strongly preferred over breast — they stay juicy even if slightly over-cooked. Leftover sliced chicken reheats well in a dry pan.
