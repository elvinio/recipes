# Chicken and Artichoke Fricassee

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mediterranean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into large 5 cm pieces
- 400 g canned artichoke hearts, drained and quartered
- 1 large leek (white and light green part, about 200 g), thinly sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 200 mL dry white wine
- 300 mL chicken stock
- 150 mL heavy cream (35% fat)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 20 g unsalted butter
- 2 tsp dried dill or 3 tbsp fresh dill, chopped
- Juice of 1 large lemon (about 40 mL)
- 2 large egg yolks
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Fresh dill fronds, to serve

## Steps

1. Season chicken pieces generously with salt and white pepper. Heat olive oil and butter in a large, wide, deep skillet over medium-high heat until butter foams.
2. Add chicken in a single layer, working in batches if needed. Brown for 3–4 minutes per side until golden. Remove and set aside.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add leek to the same pan and cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
4. Pour in white wine, scraping up any browned bits from the pan. Let it reduce by half, about 2–3 minutes.
5. Add chicken stock and return chicken to the pan. Bring to a gentle simmer, cover partially, and cook for 12 minutes. Add artichoke hearts and cook for a further 5 minutes.
6. In a small bowl, whisk together egg yolks and cream. Remove the pan from heat and let it stop bubbling completely. Slowly pour the egg-cream mixture into the pan, stirring constantly — the residual heat will thicken the sauce without scrambling the eggs.
7. Return pan to the lowest possible heat setting. Stir in lemon juice and dill. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring, until sauce is smooth and slightly thickened. Do not allow to boil.
8. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve scattered with fresh dill fronds, with crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

## Special Notice

> The egg yolk and cream liaison (avgolemono-style) is the defining step — ensuring the pan is off the boil before adding it prevents curdling. Stir gently but continuously as you incorporate it. This dish does not freeze well due to the cream sauce, but keeps in the fridge for 2 days.
