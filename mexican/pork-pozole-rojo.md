# Pork Pozole Rojo

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless pork shoulder, cut into 4 cm chunks
- 800 g canned hominy (white maize), drained and rinsed
- 4 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried chile de árbol (for heat), stems removed
- 1 medium white onion, half roughly chopped, half thinly sliced for garnish
- 6 garlic cloves, 4 peeled whole, 2 minced
- 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 litre chicken or pork stock
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Garnishes (serve at the table):**
- 150 g shredded green cabbage
- 4 radishes, thinly sliced
- 1 ripe avocado, diced
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- 30 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Tostadas, to serve
- Chilli flakes or hot sauce

## Steps

1. Toast all dried chiles in the pressure cooker pot on sauté-medium for 30 seconds per side until fragrant. Remove, soak in 500 mL boiling water for 10 minutes, then drain, reserving 125 mL soaking liquid.
2. Blend drained chiles with 4 whole garlic cloves, roughly chopped onion half, oregano, cumin, and reserved soaking liquid. Process until completely smooth. Pass through a fine sieve for a silky broth if desired.
3. Season pork generously with salt and pepper. Heat oil in the pressure cooker on sauté-high. Sear pork in batches for 3 minutes per side until browned. Remove and set aside.
4. Add minced garlic to the pot and sauté 30 seconds. Add the blended chile sauce and cook, stirring, for 3 minutes until darkened.
5. Return pork to the pot. Add stock and drained hominy. Stir to combine. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 25 minutes.
6. Release pressure manually. Remove pork and shred coarsely, discarding any large fat pieces. Return shredded pork to the soup.
7. Taste the broth and adjust salt generously — pozole needs confident seasoning. The hominy should be tender and the broth a deep brick-red colour.
8. Ladle into deep bowls. Set all garnishes on the table in individual small bowls so each person can customise their own: cabbage, radish, avocado, cilantro, lime, oregano, and chilli. Serve with tostadas alongside.

## Special Notice

> Pozole is a ceremonial Mexican dish best served as a meal in itself — the garnishes are not optional decoration but an integral part of the flavour. The soup base keeps refrigerated for up to 4 days and the flavours deepen overnight. If canned hominy is unavailable, dried hominy can be soaked and pre-cooked, but increase overall time accordingly.
