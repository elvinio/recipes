# Chicken Enchiladas Rojas

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 12 corn tortillas (15 cm diameter)
- 400 g canned whole peeled tomatoes
- 3 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 1 medium white onion, quartered (approx. 200 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, peeled
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 250 mL (1 cup) chicken stock
- 200 g shredded Oaxaca cheese or mozzarella
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt to taste
- 60 g sour cream, to serve
- 15 g fresh cilantro, roughly chopped, to serve

## Steps

1. Place chicken thighs in a saucepan, cover with cold water, and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce to a simmer and cook for 15 minutes until cooked through. Remove chicken, let cool slightly, then shred with two forks. Reserve 125 mL of the cooking liquid.
2. While chicken cooks, toast the guajillo and ancho chiles in a dry skillet over medium heat for 30 seconds per side until fragrant. Soak in 500 mL of boiling water for 10 minutes to rehydrate.
3. Drain soaked chiles and blend with canned tomatoes, onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, chicken stock, and reserved cooking liquid. Blend until completely smooth, about 2 minutes. Season with salt.
4. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wide saucepan over medium heat. Pour in the blended sauce, bring to a simmer, and cook for 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it deepens in colour and thickens slightly. Adjust seasoning.
5. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Spread 60 mL of sauce across the bottom of a 30 × 20 cm baking dish.
6. Heat the remaining 2 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium heat. Working one at a time, briefly fry each tortilla for about 15 seconds per side to soften and prevent tearing. Drain on paper towels.
7. Dip each tortilla in the warm red sauce to coat both sides. Place 50 g of shredded chicken along the centre of each tortilla, roll tightly, and arrange seam-side down in the baking dish.
8. Pour remaining sauce evenly over the enchiladas and scatter shredded cheese on top. Bake for 12–15 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbling at the edges.
9. Remove from oven and rest for 3 minutes. Drizzle with sour cream, scatter cilantro over the top, and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Enchiladas are best eaten straight from the oven — the tortillas soften further as they sit. Leftover enchiladas keep well covered in the fridge for up to 2 days; reheat covered with foil at 180°C for 15 minutes. For extra depth, add a small square of dark chocolate (70%) to the sauce while it simmers.
