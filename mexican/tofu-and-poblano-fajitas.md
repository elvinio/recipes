# Tofu and Poblano Fajitas

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g extra-firm tofu
- 8 medium flour tortillas (25 cm diameter)
- 3 poblano chiles (approx. 400 g), stems and seeds removed, sliced into strips
- 2 large red bell peppers, sliced into strips (approx. 350 g)
- 1 large white onion, sliced into half-rings (approx. 200 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp chipotle chilli powder
- 3 tbsp soy sauce or tamari
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice (approx. 1 lime)
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**To serve:**
- 2 ripe avocados, sliced
- 120 mL (½ cup) sour cream or vegan crema
- 15 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- Hot sauce

## Steps

1. Drain tofu and press firmly between paper towels or a clean cloth for 5 minutes to remove excess moisture. Cut into 1.5 cm thick slabs, then cut each slab into 1.5 × 4 cm batons.
2. Combine cumin, smoked paprika, oregano, chipotle powder, soy sauce, lime juice, and 1 tbsp oil in a bowl. Add tofu batons and toss gently to coat. Let marinate for 5 minutes.
3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large heavy skillet over high heat. Add onion and cook, stirring occasionally, for 3 minutes until edges are charring. Add poblano and bell pepper strips. Cook over high heat, stirring every minute or so, for 5–6 minutes until the vegetables are tender with good char. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more. Season with salt. Remove from pan and set aside.
4. Heat remaining 1 tbsp oil in the same skillet over high heat. Add tofu batons in a single layer. Cook undisturbed for 2–3 minutes until a golden crust forms. Flip and cook the other side for 2 minutes. Add any remaining marinade from the bowl, toss, and cook 30 seconds more. Remove from heat.
5. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet over medium heat for 20 seconds per side, or directly over a gas flame until lightly charred and pliable. Wrap in a cloth to keep warm.
6. Return vegetables to the skillet briefly to reheat with the tofu if needed.
7. Serve the tofu and vegetable mix sizzling from the pan. Set out warm tortillas, avocado slices, sour cream, cilantro, lime wedges, and hot sauce at the table.
8. Let each person assemble their own fajitas.

## Special Notice

> Pressing the tofu well before cooking is the single most important step — undrained tofu steams rather than sears. For extra firm, chewy tofu batons that hold their shape better, freeze the tofu block overnight and thaw completely before pressing and cutting. The fajita filling keeps refrigerated for up to 2 days; reheat in a hot pan.
