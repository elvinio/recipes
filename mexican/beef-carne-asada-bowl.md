# Beef Carne Asada Bowl

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g skirt steak or flank steak
- 300 g long-grain white rice
- 525 mL (2¼ cups) water (for rice)
- 3 limes, juiced (approx. 90 mL), plus wedges to serve
- 3 tbsp fresh orange juice
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 jalapeño, finely diced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp chipotle chilli powder
- 2 tbsp neutral oil, plus extra for cooking
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Bowl toppings:**
- 400 g canned black beans, drained and rinsed
- ½ tsp cumin (for beans)
- 2 ripe avocados, sliced or roughly mashed
- 200 g pico de gallo (store-bought or fresh)
- 100 g sour cream
- 80 g shredded cheddar cheese
- 30 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 1 lime, cut into wedges

## Steps

1. Combine lime juice, orange juice, garlic, jalapeño, cumin, smoked paprika, chipotle powder, oil, and 1 tsp salt in a shallow dish. Add steak, turning to coat. Marinate for at least 15 minutes at room temperature (or refrigerate for up to 24 hours).
2. Rinse rice under cold water until clear. Place in a saucepan with water and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, stir once, cover tightly, and reduce to the lowest heat. Cook 15 minutes, then rest covered off heat for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork.
3. While rice cooks, warm black beans in a small saucepan with a splash of water and ½ tsp cumin over medium heat. Season with salt and keep warm.
4. Remove steak from marinade and pat very lightly dry on one side only — you want some marinade remaining for flavour and caramelisation. Season the patted side with a little extra salt.
5. Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron or stainless steel) over the highest heat until nearly smoking. Add a thin film of oil. Add steak and press down with a spatula to ensure full contact with the pan.
6. For skirt steak (1.5 cm thick): cook 2–3 minutes per side for medium-rare. For flank steak: cook 3–4 minutes per side. The exterior should be deeply charred and fragrant. Rest on a board for 5 minutes.
7. Slice steak thinly against the grain at a slight angle.
8. Build bowls: start with a generous base of rice, add beans to one side, then arrange sliced carne asada over the top. Add avocado, pico de gallo, a dollop of sour cream, and shredded cheese. Scatter cilantro and serve with lime wedges.

## Special Notice

> Slicing against the grain is non-negotiable with skirt or flank steak — cutting with the grain will result in tough, chewy meat. The marinade also works beautifully on chicken thighs or portobello mushrooms. Leftover sliced steak keeps for 2 days refrigerated; reheat briefly in a very hot pan to restore the sear.
