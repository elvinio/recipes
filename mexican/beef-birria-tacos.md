# Beef Birria Tacos

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Steps

1. Toast the guajillo, ancho, and chipotle chiles in the pressure cooker pot (using the sauté function) over medium heat for 30 seconds per side. Add 500 mL of hot water and soak for 8 minutes, then drain.
2. Blend drained chiles with canned tomatoes, onion, garlic, apple cider vinegar, cumin, oregano, cinnamon, cloves, and 250 mL beef stock. Process until completely smooth. Season generously with salt.
3. Cut beef chuck into 6 cm chunks and season all over with salt and pepper. Switch pressure cooker to sauté on high. Add oil and sear beef in batches for 2–3 minutes per side until browned. Set aside.
4. Return all beef to the pot. Pour blended chile sauce over the top. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 30 minutes.
5. Release pressure manually. Remove beef and shred with two forks. Reserve all the cooking consommé in the pot — this is your dipping broth.
6. Heat a wide non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Dip each corn tortilla briefly in the consommé to coat, then place in the dry skillet. Add a generous handful of shredded beef and Oaxaca cheese to one half of each tortilla. Fold over and press gently with a spatula.
7. Cook for 2 minutes per side until the tortilla is crispy and the cheese is melted. Work in batches, keeping finished tacos warm in a low oven.
8. Ladle warm consommé into small bowls. Serve tacos immediately alongside consommé for dipping, garnished with diced white onion, cilantro, and lime wedges.

## Ingredients

- 800 g beef chuck, trimmed of excess fat
- 12 corn tortillas (15 cm diameter)
- 3 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried chipotle chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 1 medium white onion, roughly chopped (approx. 200 g), plus extra diced to serve
- 5 garlic cloves, peeled
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ¼ tsp ground cinnamon
- 2 cloves, whole
- 500 mL beef stock
- 200 g shredded Oaxaca cheese or mozzarella
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- 20 g fresh cilantro, chopped

## Special Notice

> The consommé is just as important as the tacos — serve it hot and well-seasoned. Birria improves greatly the next day once the spices mellow; the shredded beef and broth keep refrigerated for up to 3 days. For a richer broth, add a split beef marrow bone when pressure cooking.
