# Beef Chiles Rellenos

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 8 large poblano chiles (approx. 900 g total)
- 400 g lean beef mince
- 150 g Oaxaca cheese or mozzarella, shredded
- 1 small white onion, finely diced (approx. 120 g)
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 medium tomato, finely diced (approx. 150 g)
- 30 g raisins
- 30 g pimento-stuffed olives, sliced
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Batter and sauce:**
- 4 large eggs, separated
- 50 g plain flour, plus extra for dusting
- ½ tsp salt
- 300 mL neutral oil (for shallow frying)
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- ½ tsp cumin
- ½ tsp oregano
- 125 mL (½ cup) chicken stock
- 15 g fresh cilantro, to serve
- Sour cream, to serve

## Steps

1. Char the poblano chiles directly over a gas flame or under a very hot grill, turning with tongs, until completely blistered and blackened all over, about 8–10 minutes. Place in a bowl, cover tightly with cling film, and steam for 10 minutes to loosen the skin.
2. While chiles steam, heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Cook onion for 4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Add beef mince and cook, breaking up, for 5–6 minutes until browned. Add tomato, raisins, olives, cinnamon, cumin, oregano, salt, and pepper. Cook for 5 more minutes until the mixture is fragrant and mostly dry. Remove from heat and stir in half the shredded cheese. Cool slightly.
3. Make the tomato sauce: heat 1 tbsp oil in a saucepan. Add 2 minced garlic cloves, cook 1 minute, then add crushed tomatoes, stock, cumin, and oregano. Simmer for 10 minutes. Season with salt. Keep warm.
4. Peel the steamed chiles gently under cold running water. Make a lengthways slit in each chile, leaving the stem attached. Carefully remove seeds and veins with a small spoon, trying to keep the chile intact. Pat dry.
5. Preheat oven to 180°C. Stuff each chile generously with the beef mixture. Close the slit by pressing the edges together. Dust lightly with flour on the outside.
6. Beat egg whites with a pinch of salt to stiff peaks. Whisk egg yolks with the flour and ½ tsp salt, then gently fold into the whites to make a light batter.
7. Heat 300 mL oil in a wide, deep skillet over medium-high heat to 175°C. Working in pairs, dip each stuffed chile in the batter to coat evenly. Fry for 2–3 minutes per side until deep golden. Drain on paper towels.
8. Place fried chiles in a baking dish, spoon warm tomato sauce over them, and bake at 180°C for 8 minutes. Scatter remaining cheese over the top for the last 3 minutes of baking. Serve with sour cream and cilantro.

## Special Notice

> Chiles rellenos require patient multi-step work — charring, stuffing, battering, and frying — but each step is straightforward. The egg white batter must be used immediately; stiff peaks collapse quickly. Prepare the tomato sauce and filling ahead if desired (up to 1 day), but batter and fry the chiles just before serving.
