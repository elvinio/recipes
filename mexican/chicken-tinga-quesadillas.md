# Chicken Tinga Quesadillas

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless skinless chicken breasts or thighs
- 8 large flour tortillas (25 cm diameter)
- 2 chipotles in adobo sauce, finely chopped, plus 1 tbsp adobo sauce
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 1 medium white onion, thinly sliced (approx. 180 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 250 mL (1 cup) chicken stock
- 300 g shredded Oaxaca cheese or mozzarella
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) sour cream, to serve
- 1 avocado, sliced, to serve
- 15 g fresh cilantro, chopped, to serve
- Lime wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Place chicken in a saucepan, cover with cold water, add a pinch of salt, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes until cooked through. Remove and let cool for 5 minutes, then shred with two forks.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wide skillet over medium heat. Add sliced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, for 6–7 minutes until softened and lightly golden at the edges.
3. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add chipotles, adobo sauce, crushed tomatoes, thyme, oregano, and cumin. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
4. Add chicken stock and shredded chicken. Cook uncovered over medium heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and coats the chicken. Season with salt and pepper. The mixture should be saucy but not wet. Remove from heat.
5. Heat a clean large skillet or griddle over medium heat. Place one flour tortilla in the pan. Scatter a quarter of the shredded cheese over one half, then top with a quarter of the chicken tinga. Fold the tortilla in half to encase the filling.
6. Cook for 2–3 minutes until the underside is golden and crisp. Flip carefully and cook another 2 minutes until both sides are golden and the cheese is fully melted.
7. Remove from pan and rest for 1 minute before cutting into 3 wedges. Repeat with remaining tortillas.
8. Serve with sour cream for dipping, sliced avocado, chopped cilantro, and lime wedges.

## Special Notice

> Chicken tinga makes excellent meal prep — the filling keeps refrigerated for up to 4 days and the quesadillas themselves are best assembled and cooked fresh. Don't rush the stovetop step: high heat will burn the outside before the cheese melts. Use a lid for the first minute on each side to help melt the cheese faster.
