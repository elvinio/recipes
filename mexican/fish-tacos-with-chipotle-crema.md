# Fish Tacos with Chipotle Crema

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g firm white fish fillets (cod, tilapia, or mahi-mahi), skin removed
- 12 small corn tortillas (15 cm diameter)
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- ½ tsp onion powder
- ¼ tsp cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 150 g shredded green cabbage
- 150 g shredded purple cabbage
- 2 limes, juiced (approx. 60 mL), plus wedges to serve
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for cabbage slaw)
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Chipotle Crema:**
- 120 mL (½ cup) sour cream
- 1–2 chipotle chiles in adobo sauce, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp adobo sauce (from the can)
- 1 lime, juiced
- 1 small garlic clove, finely grated
- Pinch of salt

## Steps

1. Make the chipotle crema: combine sour cream, chopped chipotles, adobo sauce, lime juice, and grated garlic in a small bowl. Mix well, season with salt, and refrigerate until needed.
2. Toss shredded green and purple cabbage with olive oil, half the lime juice, and a pinch of salt. Set aside to lightly pickle while you cook the fish.
3. Pat fish fillets completely dry with paper towels. Combine smoked paprika, cumin, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne, salt, and pepper in a small bowl. Sprinkle evenly over all sides of the fish, pressing gently to adhere.
4. Warm corn tortillas directly over a medium gas flame or in a dry skillet over high heat, 20–30 seconds per side, until pliable and lightly charred. Stack in a cloth to keep warm.
5. Heat 2 tbsp neutral oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. When shimmering, add fish fillets and cook for 3 minutes without moving. Flip and cook for another 2–3 minutes until the fish is opaque throughout and flakes easily with a fork.
6. Remove fish from pan and break into rough flakes using a fork — do not over-shred; chunky pieces work best.
7. To assemble: spoon a drizzle of chipotle crema onto each tortilla, add a small pile of cabbage slaw, then top with fish flakes. Finish with another thin drizzle of crema.
8. Serve immediately with lime wedges and remaining crema on the side.

## Special Notice

> Drying the fish thoroughly before applying the spice rub is essential for a good sear and not a steam. Do not walk away during cooking — fish can go from perfect to overdone in under a minute. Leftover seasoned fish is best used the same day; the chipotle crema keeps refrigerated for up to 4 days.
