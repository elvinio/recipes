# Pork and Cheese Gorditas

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

**Gordita dough:**
- 350 g masa harina (instant corn masa flour)
- 430 mL (1¾ cups) warm water
- ½ tsp salt
- 1 tsp neutral oil

**Pork filling:**
- 400 g pork mince
- 1 small white onion, finely diced (approx. 120 g)
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 medium tomato, finely diced (approx. 150 g)
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Assembly and toppings:**
- 150 g shredded Oaxaca cheese or Chihuahua cheese
- 100 g shredded green cabbage
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) sour cream
- 80 g fresh salsa or pico de gallo
- 15 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2 limes, cut into wedges

## Steps

1. Make the dough: combine masa harina and salt in a large bowl. Add warm water and oil gradually, mixing with your hands until a soft, pliable dough forms — it should not crack at the edges when pressed but should not be sticky. Cover with a damp cloth and rest for 10 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 3 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute. Add pork mince and break up with a spoon. Cook for 5–6 minutes over high heat until browned and cooked through.
3. Add diced tomato, cumin, oregano, and smoked paprika. Cook for 3 more minutes until the tomato breaks down and the mixture is fragrant. Season with salt and pepper. Set aside.
4. Divide the masa dough into 8 equal balls (approx. 90 g each). Place each ball between two pieces of plastic wrap and press with a flat-bottomed pan to a 1.5 cm thick disk, approximately 10 cm in diameter.
5. Heat a dry comal or heavy skillet over medium heat. Cook gorditas for 4–5 minutes per side until dry-looking, lightly speckled brown, and cooked through — they will puff slightly. Work in batches.
6. While still hot, use a small knife to carefully slit each gordita open around the edge, like a pita pocket, leaving one side attached. Open carefully without tearing.
7. Stuff each gordita with shredded cheese first (so it melts from the residual heat), then a generous spoonful of the pork filling.
8. Top with shredded cabbage, a spoonful of sour cream, fresh salsa, and cilantro. Serve immediately with lime wedges.

## Special Notice

> The masa dough hydration varies by brand — add water gradually and stop when the dough is soft but not sticky. Gorditas are best eaten immediately once split and filled; they toughen as they cool. For a richer version, mix 30 g lard into the masa dough in place of the neutral oil.
