# Lamb Barbacoa Tacos

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g bone-in lamb shoulder or leg, cut into large pieces
- 12 corn tortillas (15 cm diameter)
- 3 dried chipotle chiles (or chipotles in adobo, 2 tbsp)
- 2 dried guajillo chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 4 garlic cloves, peeled
- 1 medium white onion, quartered (approx. 200 g), plus extra thinly sliced to serve
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cloves
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 2 dried bay leaves
- 250 mL beef stock
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 2 limes, cut into wedges
- 20 g fresh cilantro, roughly chopped
- 80 g diced white onion, to serve
- 1 avocado, sliced, to serve

## Steps

1. Toast the chipotle and guajillo chiles in the dry pressure cooker pot (sauté mode) for 30 seconds per side. Cover with 375 mL boiling water and soak for 8 minutes. Drain, reserving the soaking liquid.
2. Blend drained chiles with garlic, quartered onion, apple cider vinegar, cumin, coriander, cloves, cinnamon, thyme, and 125 mL of the soaking liquid. Process until a smooth paste forms. Season well with salt.
3. Season lamb pieces generously with salt and pepper. Heat oil in the pressure cooker on sauté-high. Sear lamb in batches for 3 minutes per side until well browned all over.
4. Pour the chile paste over the lamb. Add beef stock and bay leaves. Stir to coat the lamb. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 35 minutes.
5. Release pressure manually. Discard bay leaves. Remove lamb and shred the meat from the bones using two forks, discarding bones and any large pieces of fat. Reserve the braising liquid.
6. Return shredded lamb to the pot and stir to coat in the sauce. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Warm corn tortillas over a gas flame or in a dry hot skillet for 20 seconds per side until lightly charred. Keep warm wrapped in a cloth.
8. Serve lamb heaped into double-stacked tortillas, topped with diced white onion, cilantro, avocado slices, and a squeeze of lime. Offer extra braising liquid in small cups on the side for dipping.

## Special Notice

> Bone-in lamb adds significant flavour to the braising liquid, which makes a deeply aromatic dipping broth — strain and season it well before serving. The barbacoa keeps refrigerated for up to 3 days and freezes well for 2 months. For extra smokiness, add a splash of mezcal to the pot before sealing.
