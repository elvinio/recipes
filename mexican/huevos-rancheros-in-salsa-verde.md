# Huevos Rancheros in Salsa Verde

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mexican | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 8 corn tortillas (15 cm diameter)
- 500 g fresh tomatillos, husks removed and rinsed, or 2 × 400 g cans tomatillos, drained
- 2 jalapeños, roughly chopped (seeds in)
- 1 small white onion, roughly chopped (approx. 150 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, peeled
- 30 g fresh cilantro, plus extra leaves to serve
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 250 mL (1 cup) chicken or vegetable stock
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 400 g canned black beans, drained and rinsed
- ½ tsp cumin (for beans)
- 100 g queso fresco or feta, crumbled
- 1 ripe avocado, sliced
- Salt to taste
- Hot sauce, to serve

## Steps

1. If using fresh tomatillos, place them in a dry skillet over high heat and char on all sides, turning occasionally, for about 6–8 minutes until blistered and softened. If using canned, skip this step.
2. Transfer tomatillos to a blender with jalapeños, onion, garlic, cilantro, cumin, and stock. Blend until smooth. Season with salt.
3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a wide saucepan over medium heat. Pour in the blended salsa verde — it will spit, so stand back. Cook, stirring, for 5 minutes until the sauce darkens slightly and thickens. Taste and adjust salt.
4. Warm black beans in a small saucepan with a splash of water and ½ tsp cumin over medium heat. Season with salt. Mash lightly to a rough paste if desired.
5. Warm tortillas in a dry skillet over high heat for 20 seconds per side. Keep warm wrapped in a cloth.
6. Ladle salsa verde into a wide skillet over medium heat to a depth of about 1.5 cm. When the sauce is barely simmering, crack eggs directly into the sauce, spacing them out. Cover with a lid and cook for 3–4 minutes until whites are set but yolks remain runny.
7. While eggs cook, spread a spoonful of black beans on each tortilla. Place two tortillas per plate.
8. Use a wide spoon to carefully transfer two eggs per plate, placing them over the bean-spread tortillas. Spoon extra salsa verde around and over the eggs. Top with crumbled queso fresco, avocado slices, and cilantro leaves. Serve with hot sauce.

## Special Notice

> The salsa verde can be made 3 days ahead and refrigerated; it also freezes well for up to 1 month. For the eggs, a tight-fitting lid is essential to set the whites through steam without flipping. Serve immediately — the tortillas soften quickly under the sauce.
