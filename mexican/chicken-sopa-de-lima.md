# Chicken Sopa de Lima

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless skinless chicken thighs
- 1.5 litres chicken stock
- 3 limes (2 juiced for approx. 60 mL, 1 thinly sliced into rounds)
- 1 medium white onion, half left whole for poaching, half finely diced
- 1 head of garlic, halved horizontally
- 2 medium tomatoes, diced (approx. 300 g)
- 1 green bell pepper, finely diced (approx. 150 g)
- 1 habanero chile (or 1 jalapeño for less heat), left whole
- 1 tsp dried oregano (Mexican oregano preferred)
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt and black pepper to taste

**Toppings:**
- 6 corn tortillas, cut into thin strips and fried crisp, or use store-bought totopos
- 1 ripe avocado, diced
- 30 g fresh cilantro, leaves picked
- 100 g queso fresco or feta, crumbled

## Steps

1. Place chicken thighs in a large saucepan with chicken stock, the onion half (whole), halved garlic head, habanero, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Cook for 15 minutes until chicken is cooked through. Skim any foam from the surface.
2. While chicken poaches, heat 2 tbsp oil in a separate skillet over medium-high heat. Add diced onion and cook for 4 minutes. Add diced bell pepper and cook 3 more minutes. Add diced tomato and cook for a further 3 minutes until softened and saucy. Season with a pinch of salt and set aside.
3. If making tortilla strips: cut tortillas into 5 mm strips. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over high heat and fry strips in batches, turning frequently, for 2–3 minutes until golden and crisp. Drain on paper towels and season with salt.
4. Remove chicken from the broth. Strain the broth through a sieve, discarding the onion, garlic, and habanero. Return broth to the pot.
5. Shred the poached chicken with two forks into bite-sized pieces.
6. Add the cooked tomato-pepper mixture to the strained broth. Add oregano and cumin. Bring to a simmer over medium heat for 5 minutes.
7. Return shredded chicken to the soup. Add lime juice and stir through. Taste and adjust salt — the soup should taste bright and distinctly limey. Add half the lime slices to the pot and simmer 1 minute more.
8. Ladle into deep bowls. Top generously with crispy tortilla strips, diced avocado, cilantro leaves, crumbled queso fresco, and a lime round. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Sopa de Lima is the iconic soup of the Yucatán Peninsula — its defining characteristic is the generous use of Yucatecan lima (a fragrant sour lime); regular lime works well too. Add the lime juice at the very end and do not boil after adding it to preserve the fresh citrus flavour. The soup base without the lime keeps refrigerated for 3 days.
