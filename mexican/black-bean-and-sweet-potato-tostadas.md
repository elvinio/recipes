# Black Bean and Sweet Potato Tostadas

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 × 400 g cans black beans, drained and rinsed
- 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1.5 cm cubes (approx. 500 g)
- 8 tostadas (store-bought) or 8 corn tortillas fried crispy
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp garlic powder
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 small white onion, finely diced (approx. 120 g)
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 125 mL (½ cup) vegetable stock or water
- 2 ripe avocados
- 2 limes, juiced (approx. 60 mL)
- 100 g feta cheese or cotija, crumbled
- 80 g sour cream
- 1 small tomato, diced
- 20 g fresh cilantro, leaves picked
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Hot sauce to serve

## Steps

1. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sweet potato cubes in a single layer and season with salt. Cook, stirring every 2–3 minutes, for 10–12 minutes until golden and tender when pierced with a fork.
2. While sweet potato cooks, heat remaining 1 tbsp oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook for 4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
3. Add drained black beans to the onion pan along with cumin, smoked paprika, garlic powder, and stock. Stir well and cook over medium heat for 5 minutes, mashing about half the beans with the back of a spoon to create a chunky, spreadable consistency. Season with salt.
4. Mash avocados roughly in a bowl with half the lime juice and a pinch of salt. Keep it slightly chunky.
5. Squeeze remaining lime juice over the cooked sweet potato and toss to combine.
6. To assemble: spread a generous layer of black bean mash onto each tostada. Spoon sweet potato cubes on top, then add a dollop of smashed avocado.
7. Finish each tostada with crumbled feta or cotija, a little sour cream, diced tomato, and fresh cilantro leaves.
8. Serve immediately with hot sauce on the side.

## Special Notice

> Tostadas go soft quickly once topped, so assemble and eat right away. The black bean mash and sweet potato can both be made 2 days ahead and refrigerated separately; reheat the beans with a splash of water before assembling. Cotija cheese is the traditional choice here and worth seeking out for its salty, crumbly texture.
