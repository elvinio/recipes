# Lentil and Chorizo Soup

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 300 g green or brown lentils, rinsed
- 200 g Mexican-style fresh chorizo (or cured chorizo, casing removed and crumbled)
- 1 medium white onion, finely diced (approx. 180 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced into 1 cm cubes (approx. 180 g)
- 2 celery stalks, diced (approx. 100 g)
- 400 g canned diced tomatoes
- 1 chipotle in adobo, finely minced, plus 1 tsp adobo sauce
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 2 dried bay leaves
- 1.25 litres (5 cups) chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice (approx. 1 lime)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 30 g fresh cilantro, chopped, to serve
- 4 tbsp sour cream, to serve
- Lime wedges, to serve
- Crusty bread or warm tortillas, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat. Add crumbled chorizo and cook, breaking up with a spoon, for 4–5 minutes until rendered and beginning to crisp. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the rendered fat in the pot.
2. Add diced onion to the chorizo fat in the pot. Cook over medium heat for 4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
3. Add diced carrot and celery. Cook, stirring, for 3 minutes until slightly softened.
4. Add cumin, smoked paprika, and oregano. Stir and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
5. Add rinsed lentils, canned tomatoes, chipotle and adobo sauce, stock, and bay leaves. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.
6. Cook uncovered for 20–22 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely tender but not mushy. Add a splash more stock or water if the soup thickens too much.
7. Return chorizo to the pot. Discard bay leaves. Stir in lime juice and taste — season generously with salt and pepper. The soup should be rich, smoky, and slightly spiced.
8. Ladle into deep bowls. Top each with a spoonful of sour cream, a scatter of fresh cilantro, and a wedge of lime. Serve with warm tortillas or crusty bread for mopping.

## Special Notice

> Mexican chorizo (fresh, loose sausage) and Spanish cured chorizo behave differently — fresh chorizo renders more fat and delivers a more vivid red colour; both are delicious here. The soup thickens considerably as it cools; thin with extra stock when reheating. Keeps refrigerated for up to 4 days and freezes well for 2 months.
