# Beef Picadillo with Rice

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g lean beef mince
- 300 g long-grain white rice
- 525 mL (2¼ cups) water (for rice)
- 400 g canned diced tomatoes
- 1 medium white onion, finely diced (approx. 180 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced into 1 cm cubes (approx. 200 g)
- 1 medium waxy potato, peeled and diced into 1 cm cubes (approx. 200 g)
- 1 medium green bell pepper, diced (approx. 150 g)
- 60 g green olives, pitted and halved
- 30 g raisins
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- ½ tsp smoked paprika
- 1 chipotle in adobo, finely minced
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 125 mL (½ cup) beef stock
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 20 g fresh cilantro, chopped, to serve
- Lime wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Rinse rice under cold water until water runs clear. Combine rice, water, and a pinch of salt in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil, stir once, then cover tightly and reduce heat to the lowest setting. Cook for 15 minutes, then remove from heat and let steam, covered, for 5 minutes.
2. While rice cooks, heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 4 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
3. Add beef mince. Break it up with a wooden spoon and cook for 5–6 minutes over high heat until browned and any liquid has evaporated.
4. Add diced carrot, potato, and green pepper. Stir to combine, then add cumin, oregano, cinnamon, smoked paprika, and minced chipotle. Cook for 2 minutes, stirring frequently.
5. Add canned tomatoes and beef stock. Stir well, then bring to a simmer. Cover partially and cook for 12 minutes until the potato and carrot are tender.
6. Uncover, add olives and raisins, and stir through. Cook for 2 more minutes uncovered to reduce the sauce slightly. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Fluff the cooked rice with a fork.
8. Serve the picadillo over a generous mound of rice. Scatter fresh cilantro on top and offer lime wedges on the side.

## Special Notice

> Picadillo is a Mexican home-cooking staple and is even better the next day as the flavours meld — it keeps refrigerated for up to 3 days. The sweet-savoury balance from the raisins and olives is traditional; do not omit them. For a lighter variation, replace half the beef with finely diced mushrooms.
