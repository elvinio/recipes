# Pork Carnitas Burritos

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g boneless pork shoulder, cut into 6 cm chunks
- 4 large flour tortillas (30 cm diameter)
- 1 orange, zested and juiced (approx. 80 mL juice)
- 1 lime, juiced (approx. 30 mL)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp ground coriander
- 125 mL (½ cup) chicken or pork stock
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 300 g cooked white rice
- 400 g canned black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 tsp cumin (for beans)
- 100 g shredded cheddar or Monterey Jack
- 150 g salsa (store-bought or fresh pico de gallo)
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) sour cream
- 1 ripe avocado, sliced
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 15 g fresh cilantro, chopped

## Steps

1. Season pork chunks generously with salt, pepper, cumin, oregano, and ground coriander. Toss to coat.
2. Set electric pressure cooker to sauté on high. Add 1 tbsp oil and sear pork in batches for 2–3 minutes per side until browned. Remove and set aside.
3. Add garlic to the pot and sauté for 30 seconds. Deglaze with orange juice, lime juice, and stock, scraping up any browned bits. Return pork to the pot.
4. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 25 minutes. Release pressure manually. The pork should be completely tender and easily shredded.
5. Remove pork and shred coarsely with two forks. Reserve 60 mL of the cooking liquid.
6. Heat remaining 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet over high heat. Add shredded pork in a single layer (in batches) and press down with a spatula. Cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until the underside is crispy. Drizzle reserved cooking liquid over the top and toss.
7. Warm black beans in a small saucepan with a splash of the pork cooking liquid and 1 tsp cumin. Season with salt.
8. Warm each flour tortilla in a dry skillet for 20 seconds per side. In the centre of each tortilla, layer rice, beans, crispy carnitas, cheese, salsa, avocado, and sour cream. Scatter cilantro over the top.
9. Fold the sides in, then roll tightly from the bottom up into a burrito. Optionally, place seam-side down in the hot skillet for 1 minute per side to seal and toast.

## Special Notice

> Crisping the shredded pork in a dry skillet at the end is the secret to authentic carnitas — do not skip this step. Leftover carnitas (without burrito assembly) keep refrigerated for 4 days or frozen for up to 2 months. Re-crisp from the fridge in a hot dry pan with a splash of the reserved braising liquid.
