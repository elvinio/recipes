# Prawn Tostadas with Avocado

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw prawns, peeled and deveined
- 8 tostadas (store-bought) or 8 corn tortillas, fried crispy
- 3 ripe avocados
- 3 limes, juiced (approx. 90 mL)
- 1 small red onion, finely diced (approx. 100 g)
- 1 jalapeño, finely diced (seeds removed for mild)
- 30 g fresh cilantro, finely chopped, plus leaves to garnish
- 1 large ripe tomato, deseeded and diced (approx. 150 g)
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 100 g sour cream or Mexican crema
- 50 g queso fresco or feta, crumbled
- Salt to taste
- Hot sauce, to serve

## Steps

1. Make the guacamole: halve and pit avocados. Scoop flesh into a bowl. Add half the lime juice, half the diced red onion, half the jalapeño, and half the cilantro. Mash to your preferred texture — some people like chunky, others smooth. Season with salt. Press cling film directly onto the surface to prevent browning.
2. Make the pico de gallo: combine diced tomato, remaining red onion, remaining jalapeño, remaining cilantro, and 1 tbsp lime juice. Season with salt and set aside.
3. Pat prawns dry with paper towels. Toss with garlic powder, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and a squeeze of lime juice.
4. Heat oil in a large skillet over high heat. When shimmering, add prawns in a single layer. Cook for 90 seconds per side until pink and curled. Do not overcook. Remove from heat and squeeze a little lime juice over them.
5. If making your own tostadas: heat 1 cm of oil in a skillet over medium-high heat to 175°C. Fry corn tortillas one at a time for 2–3 minutes, turning once, until completely flat and crispy. Drain on paper towels and season with salt immediately.
6. Spread a generous layer of guacamole on each tostada — this acts as the "glue" that holds the toppings in place.
7. Arrange 4–5 prawns on each tostada. Spoon pico de gallo over the prawns. Drizzle with sour cream and scatter crumbled queso fresco on top.
8. Garnish with cilantro leaves and serve immediately with hot sauce and remaining lime wedges on the side.

## Special Notice

> Assemble tostadas immediately before serving — once topped, the base will soften within minutes. Pressing cling film directly onto the guacamole surface prevents oxidisation if making it ahead. The prawns can be seasoned up to 30 minutes ahead but should be cooked at the last moment.
