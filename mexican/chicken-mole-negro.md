# Chicken Mole Negro

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg bone-in chicken thighs and drumsticks (approx. 8 pieces)
- 3 dried mulato chiles, stems and seeds removed (reserve seeds)
- 2 dried ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 2 dried pasilla chiles, stems and seeds removed
- 1 dried chipotle chile, stem removed
- 3 tbsp sesame seeds, plus extra to garnish
- 2 tbsp pepitas (pumpkin seeds)
- 3 tbsp raisins
- 50 g dark chocolate (70% cocoa), roughly chopped
- 1 corn tortilla (day-old or slightly stale), torn into pieces
- 4 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- 1 large white onion, half roughly chopped, half sliced into rings
- 2 ripe tomatoes, halved
- 2 tomatillos, husks removed, halved
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- ¼ tsp ground cloves
- ½ tsp ground black pepper
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 750 mL chicken stock
- Salt to taste
- Steamed white rice and warm tortillas, to serve

## Steps

1. Toast dried chiles in a dry skillet over medium heat for 20 seconds per side until fragrant and pliable. Do not burn or the mole will be bitter. Soak in 500 mL of hot water for 15 minutes, then drain, reserving 125 mL soaking liquid.
2. In the same dry skillet over medium heat, toast sesame seeds and pepitas, stirring, for 2–3 minutes until golden. Remove. Toast the reserved chile seeds for 30 seconds (a few blackened seeds add authentic bitterness to negro mole). Toast the torn tortilla pieces until deep brown. Toast raisins for 30 seconds until puffed. Set all toasted elements aside separately.
3. Char the garlic, onion rings, tomatoes, and tomatillos in the dry skillet over high heat, turning occasionally, until deeply blistered on all sides, about 6–8 minutes total. Peel garlic.
4. Working in batches, blend rehydrated chiles with their reserved soaking liquid, charred vegetables (garlic, onion, tomato, tomatillo), toasted sesame seeds, pepitas, raisins, tortilla pieces, chile seeds, cumin, cinnamon, cloves, and black pepper. Blend each batch until completely smooth, adding up to 250 mL stock as needed. Combine all batches.
5. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot (ideally enamelled cast iron) over medium-high heat. Add the blended mole in one go — it will spit vigorously. Fry the paste, stirring constantly, for 5 minutes until it darkens further and begins to pull away from the sides of the pot.
6. Add remaining chicken stock gradually, stirring to incorporate. Add chopped dark chocolate and stir until melted. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer uncovered for 10 minutes, stirring frequently. The mole should coat a spoon thickly. Season well with salt.
7. Season chicken pieces with salt. Heat remaining 1 tbsp oil in a separate skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken for 3–4 minutes per side until golden. Transfer seared chicken to the mole pot, submerge in sauce, cover partially, and simmer for 20 minutes until cooked through.
8. Serve chicken pieces over steamed rice, generously napped with mole sauce. Scatter extra sesame seeds on top. Offer warm tortillas on the side.

## Special Notice

> Mole negro is one of Mexico's most complex and celebrated sauces — take time with the charring and toasting steps as these build the sauce's depth. The mole sauce alone keeps refrigerated for 1 week and freezes beautifully for up to 3 months; make a double batch of sauce while you're at it. Do not substitute milk chocolate — the bitterness of 70% cocoa is essential.
