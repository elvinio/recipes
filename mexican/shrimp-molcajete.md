# Shrimp Molcajete

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Mexican | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails on)
- 400 g plum tomatoes, halved (approx. 4 medium)
- 4 dried chile de árbol, stems removed
- 2 serrano chiles, halved lengthways
- 1 large white onion, cut into thick rings
- 6 garlic cloves, unpeeled
- 1 large green bell pepper, cut into 3 cm strips
- 1 large red bell pepper, cut into 3 cm strips
- 200 g fresh mushrooms (cremini or oyster), halved if large
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 2 tsp smoked paprika
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 60 mL (¼ cup) fresh lime juice (approx. 2 limes)
- 100 g Oaxaca cheese or mozzarella, torn into chunks
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 20 g fresh cilantro, to serve
- Warm corn tortillas, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat a dry cast-iron skillet or heavy pan over high heat until smoking. Place tomato halves cut-side down, serrano chiles, chile de árbol, onion rings, and unpeeled garlic in the pan. Char for 3–4 minutes per side, turning once, until deeply blistered and softened. Work in batches if needed.
2. Peel the charred garlic cloves. Transfer charred tomatoes, serranos, chile de árbol, and garlic to a blender. Add oregano, cumin, a pinch of salt, and 60 mL water. Blend until a slightly chunky, rustic sauce forms. Taste and adjust salt.
3. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large skillet over high heat. Add bell pepper strips and charred onion rings. Cook, stirring, for 3–4 minutes until tender with some charred edges. Add mushrooms and cook 2 more minutes. Season with salt. Remove and set aside.
4. Season shrimp with smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Heat remaining 2 tbsp oil in the same skillet over the highest heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook for 90 seconds per side until pink and lightly charred. Do not overcook. Remove immediately.
5. Pour the blended salsa into the skillet and bring to a vigorous simmer for 3 minutes to cook out the raw chile flavour. Taste and season.
6. Add the cooked vegetables back to the sauce and stir to coat. Nestle the seared shrimp into the sauce on top. Scatter torn Oaxaca cheese over everything and cover the pan with a lid for 1 minute to allow the cheese to begin melting.
7. Squeeze lime juice over the top. Scatter cilantro generously.
8. Serve directly from the pan family-style, with a stack of warm corn tortillas on the side for scooping.

## Special Notice

> The key to a true molcajete flavour is aggressive charring at every stage — do not be timid about letting the tomatoes and chiles blacken. This dish is best eaten immediately as the shrimp will overcook if left in the hot sauce. For an authentic presentation, serve in a preheated basalt molcajete.
