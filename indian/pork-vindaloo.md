# Pork Vindaloo

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless pork shoulder, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- 6 dried red chillies (Byadgi or Kashmiri), deseeded, soaked in hot water 10 min
- 6 garlic cloves
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 6 black peppercorns
- 4 cloves
- 1 cinnamon stick (4 cm)
- 3 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely diced
- 2 medium tomatoes (200 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- 160 mL water

## Steps

1. Drain soaked chillies and blend with garlic, ginger, cumin seeds, peppercorns, cloves, cinnamon, and vinegar to a smooth paste. Add a splash of water to help the blender if needed.
2. Toss pork cubes with the vindaloo paste and ½ tsp salt. Marinate for at least 15 minutes (the acid begins tenderising the meat while you prep other ingredients).
3. Cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, reduce to lowest heat, cover, cook 12 minutes, rest off heat 5 minutes.
4. Heat oil in a large heavy pan over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 7–8 minutes until well browned.
5. Add tomatoes and turmeric; cook for 4–5 minutes until tomatoes are pulpy and oil floats at the edges.
6. Add marinated pork with all its paste. Stir to combine and cook on high heat for 3 minutes, searing the meat.
7. Add 160 mL water, remaining ½ tsp salt, and sugar. Bring to a vigorous simmer, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cover and cook for 18–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until pork is tender and sauce is thick and clinging.
8. Taste and balance with extra vinegar for tang or sugar if too sharp. Serve over steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Vindaloo is better the next day — the vinegar and spices mellow beautifully overnight. For an even more tender result, cut the stovetop simmer to 10 minutes and finish in a 180°C oven for 15 minutes uncovered, which also develops a slight crust on the surface pork.
