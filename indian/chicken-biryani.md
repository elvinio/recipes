# Chicken Biryani

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g bone-in chicken thighs and drumsticks
- 350 g (1¾ cups) aged basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 20 minutes
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp ghee, divided
- 2 large onions (400 g), thinly sliced
- 120 mL (½ cup) plain yoghurt
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 4 green cardamom pods
- 1 cinnamon stick
- 2 dried bay leaves
- Large pinch of saffron soaked in 3 tbsp warm milk
- Small handful fresh mint leaves
- Small handful fresh cilantro leaves

## Steps

1. Heat oil in the pressure cooker on sauté mode. Fry sliced onions in batches over medium-high heat for 12–14 minutes, stirring often, until deep golden brown and crispy. Remove half the onions and drain on paper towels (these are the fried onions for garnish).
2. Add garlic and ginger to the remaining onions in the pot; stir for 1 minute. Add garam masala, cumin, Kashmiri chilli, and turmeric; cook 30 seconds.
3. Add chicken pieces and stir to coat. Add yoghurt, salt, and 60 mL water. Mix well and cook on sauté mode for 5 minutes, turning chicken once.
4. Drain the soaked rice. Spread the rice evenly over the chicken — do not stir. Tuck in cardamom pods, cinnamon stick, and bay leaves. Pour 480 mL water carefully down the side of the pot (not directly on rice).
5. Drizzle saffron milk and 1 tbsp ghee over the rice. Scatter half the mint and cilantro on top.
6. Seal the lid, set valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 6 minutes. Allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then quick-release remaining pressure.
7. Open the lid and check rice is cooked and fluffy. Drizzle remaining 1 tbsp ghee over the top.
8. Gently fluff and layer with a large spoon, bringing rice from the bottom up. Serve topped with reserved crispy onions and remaining fresh herbs.

## Special Notice

> The key to biryani is not stirring — the layered cooking creates the characteristic saffron-streaked top layer and fragrant bottom layer. Aged basmati rice (1 year or more) absorbs water more cleanly and stays separate. Serve with cucumber raita or a simple onion salad.
