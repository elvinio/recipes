# Beef Keema Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g lean beef mince
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (about 200 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp garam masala
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 150 g frozen peas
- 2 tbsp plain yoghurt
- Small handful of fresh cilantro, chopped
- 1 green chilli, sliced (optional, for garnish)

## Steps

1. Heat oil in a large heavy-bottomed pan over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook, stirring frequently, for 7–8 minutes until golden.
2. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add all dry spices — cumin, coriander, turmeric, Kashmiri chilli powder, and salt — and cook, stirring, for 45 seconds. Add a small splash of water to prevent sticking.
4. Add beef mince, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook over high heat for 5–6 minutes, stirring often, until all the meat is browned and any liquid has evaporated. The keema should be frying, not stewing, at this stage.
5. Stir in chopped tomatoes. Cook for 6–7 minutes until tomatoes are fully broken down and oil begins to surface around the edges.
6. Reduce heat to medium-low. Stir in yoghurt, mixing well to incorporate. Add frozen peas and 60 mL water. Cover and cook for 5 minutes until peas are tender.
7. Add garam masala and stir through. Taste and adjust salt.
8. Garnish with fresh cilantro and sliced green chilli. Serve with naan, roti, or as a filling for wraps.

## Special Notice

> Keema freezes exceptionally well — portion into airtight containers and freeze for up to 2 months. For a lower-fat version, drain excess rendered fat after browning the mince before adding tomatoes. This dish is also excellent stuffed inside samosas or piled onto toast.
