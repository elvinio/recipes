# Beef Nihari

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g beef shin (osso buco style) or beef shank, bone-in
- 3 tbsp ghee or neutral oil
- 1 large onion (200 g), very thinly sliced
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- **Nihari spice mix:** 1 tsp fennel seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, ½ tsp black peppercorns, 4 cloves, 2 black cardamom pods (seeds only), ½ tsp ground mace, ¼ tsp ground nutmeg — toasted together and ground fine
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt, plus more to taste
- 3 tbsp plain flour (or wheat flour)
- 600 mL beef stock or water
- 8 naan, sheermal, or crusty rolls
- **To finish:** julienned ginger, sliced green chilli, fried onions, lemon wedges, fresh cilantro

## Steps

1. Heat ghee in the pressure cooker on sauté mode over high heat. Brown beef pieces in batches for 3–4 minutes per side until deeply caramelised. Remove and set aside.
2. Reduce to medium heat. Fry sliced onion in the same pot for 10–12 minutes until deeply golden and jammy.
3. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute. Add ground nihari spice mix, Kashmiri chilli powder, and turmeric; cook for 1 minute, adding a splash of stock if the spices stick.
4. Add the flour and stir constantly for 2 minutes to cook out the raw taste — this is the thickening agent for the stew.
5. Pour in 600 mL stock gradually, stirring to prevent lumps. Return beef pieces and any resting juices. Add salt and stir.
6. Seal the pressure cooker lid. Cook on high pressure for 30 minutes. Allow a full natural pressure release (about 10–15 minutes).
7. Open the lid and check the beef — it should be very tender and falling from the bone. If the sauce is too thin, switch to sauté mode and reduce for 5 minutes. Skim any excess fat from the surface. Taste and adjust salt.
8. Serve in deep bowls ladled over torn naan, topped generously with julienned ginger, green chilli, fried onions, a squeeze of lemon, and fresh cilantro.

## Special Notice

> Nihari is a slow-cooked breakfast stew traditionally eaten in winter — the bone marrow from beef shin enriches the broth dramatically; do not discard the marrow bones when serving. If you can find nihari masala (a pre-made spice blend), use 2 tbsp in place of the homemade spice mix. The stew reheats beautifully the next day with even deeper flavour.
