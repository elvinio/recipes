# Mushroom and Pea Biryani

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 350 g (1¾ cups) aged basmati rice, rinsed and soaked for 20 minutes
- 350 g cremini or portobello mushrooms, sliced 1 cm thick
- 150 g frozen peas
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp ghee, divided
- 2 large onions (350 g), thinly sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 120 mL (½ cup) plain yoghurt
- 1½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 4 green cardamom pods, crushed
- 1 cinnamon stick (5 cm)
- 2 dried bay leaves
- Large pinch of saffron, soaked in 3 tbsp warm milk
- Fresh mint and cilantro, to garnish

## Steps

1. Heat oil in the pressure cooker on sauté mode. Fry onions over medium-high heat, stirring often, for 12–14 minutes until deep golden and beginning to crisp. Transfer half the fried onions to paper towels for garnish.
2. Increase heat. Add mushrooms to the pot with remaining onions; cook for 5 minutes until they shrink and release their liquid, then the liquid evaporates and mushrooms colour. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute.
3. Add garam masala, ground cumin, Kashmiri chilli powder, and turmeric; toast for 30 seconds. Remove from heat briefly and stir in yoghurt in two additions, mixing well to prevent splitting. Return to low heat.
4. Add frozen peas, salt, and stir to combine. Taste and adjust seasoning — this is the biryani base.
5. Drain the soaked rice and spread evenly over the vegetable base. Do not stir. Tuck in cardamom pods, cinnamon, and bay leaves. Pour 420 mL water carefully down the side of the pot.
6. Drizzle saffron milk and 1 tbsp ghee over the rice. Scatter half the fresh mint and cilantro.
7. Seal the lid, set to high pressure, and cook for 5 minutes. Natural release for 10 minutes, then quick-release remaining pressure.
8. Open lid, drizzle remaining ghee, gently fluff and mix layers with a wide spoon. Serve topped with reserved crispy onions and remaining fresh herbs.

## Special Notice

> Do not stir the rice into the vegetable base — the distinct layers are what make biryani special, with fragrant saffron rice on top and spiced vegetables beneath. For the crispiest fried onions, ensure they are completely dry before frying and fry in batches so they don't steam.
