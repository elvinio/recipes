# Tarka Dal with Jeera Rice

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 220 g (1 cup) red lentils (masoor dal), rinsed well
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- **For the dal:** ½ tsp ground turmeric, 1 tsp fine salt, 800 mL water
- **For the tarka (tempering):** 2 tbsp ghee, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp black mustard seeds, 2 dried red chillies, 4 garlic cloves (thinly sliced), 10 curry leaves, 1 small onion (120 g) finely diced, 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder, 1 medium tomato (120 g) finely chopped, ½ tsp garam masala
- **For the jeera rice:** 1 tbsp ghee, 1½ tsp cumin seeds, ½ tsp fine salt
- Fresh cilantro and lemon wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine rinsed lentils, turmeric, salt, and 800 mL water in a medium saucepan. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cook uncovered for 20–22 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lentils are completely soft and have dissolved into a thick, porridge-like consistency. Add a splash of water if they thicken too much.
2. For the jeera rice: heat ghee in a separate saucepan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle for 30 seconds until fragrant. Add drained rinsed rice and stir for 1 minute to coat each grain in ghee. Add 540 mL water and salt. Bring to a boil, cover tightly, and reduce to the lowest heat. Cook for 12 minutes, then remove from heat and rest, lid on, for 5 minutes.
3. While the dal simmers, prepare the tarka: heat ghee in a small frying pan over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds; once they splutter (about 20 seconds), add dried red chillies and curry leaves (step back — they spit). Fry for 15 seconds.
4. Add sliced garlic to the tarka pan. Fry for 1 minute until golden at the edges.
5. Add diced onion; cook for 4–5 minutes until golden. Add Kashmiri chilli powder; stir for 20 seconds.
6. Add chopped tomato; cook for 2–3 minutes until softened.
7. Pour the hot tarka into the cooked dal and stir well. Add garam masala, taste, and adjust salt. If the dal is thicker than you'd like, stir in a little hot water.
8. Fluff the jeera rice with a fork. Serve the dal in deep bowls alongside the cumin-scented rice, with fresh cilantro and lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> The tarka is poured in last and should sizzle loudly when it hits the dal — this is the defining technique of this dish and the hot fat blooms the chilli into the lentils instantly. Leftover dal thickens significantly overnight; thin with water to your preferred consistency when reheating. This is an excellent weeknight staple that can be doubled and frozen.
