# Chicken Tikka Masala

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 120 mL plain full-fat yoghurt
- 2 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 large onion (about 200 g), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 180 mL (¾ cup) heavy cream
- 1 tsp sugar
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- Fresh cilantro, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine yoghurt, 1 tsp garam masala, cumin, coriander, paprika, and ½ tsp salt in a bowl. Add chicken and toss to coat. Set aside for at least 10 minutes while you prepare the sauce.
2. Cook basmati rice in 540 mL (2¼ cups) salted water over medium heat: bring to a boil, reduce to the lowest setting, cover tightly, and cook for 12 minutes. Remove from heat and let steam, lid on, for 5 minutes.
3. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a large heavy skillet over high heat. Sear marinated chicken pieces in a single layer, 2–3 minutes per side until lightly charred. Transfer to a plate; the chicken will finish cooking in the sauce.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil and the onion; cook, stirring often, for 6–7 minutes until golden and softened.
5. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 90 seconds until fragrant. Add remaining 1 tsp garam masala and cook 30 seconds more.
6. Pour in crushed tomatoes and scrape up any browned bits. Simmer for 5 minutes until slightly thickened.
7. Stir in cream and sugar. Return chicken and any resting juices to the pan. Simmer gently for 5–6 minutes until chicken is cooked through and sauce coats the back of a spoon.
8. Taste and adjust salt. Serve over rice, garnished with fresh cilantro.

## Special Notice

> Chicken thighs stay juicier than breast meat and are strongly recommended here. Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 3 days and the flavour deepens overnight — reheat gently over low heat with a splash of water to loosen the sauce.
