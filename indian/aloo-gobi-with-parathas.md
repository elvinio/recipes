# Aloo Gobi with Parathas

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g floury potatoes (e.g. Maris Piper or Russet), peeled and cut into 2 cm cubes
- 400 g cauliflower florets (about ½ medium head), cut into bite-sized pieces
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion (160 g), finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (180 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 8 store-bought or homemade whole-wheat parathas
- 1 tbsp ghee or butter, for finishing
- Fresh cilantro, to serve
- Plain yoghurt, to serve

## Steps

1. Parboil potato cubes in well-salted boiling water for 5 minutes until just starting to soften at the edges (they should not be fully cooked). Drain and set aside.
2. Heat oil in a large heavy pan or kadai over medium-high heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle for 20 seconds. Add cauliflower florets and cook, stirring occasionally, for 5–6 minutes until they develop golden-brown patches. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add onion to the same pan; cook over medium heat for 6 minutes until golden. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook for 4–5 minutes until pulpy. Add ground coriander, cumin, turmeric, and salt; stir for 30 seconds.
5. Add parboiled potatoes and stir to coat with the masala. Add 80 mL water, cover, and cook for 7–8 minutes over medium-low heat until potatoes are tender, stirring twice.
6. Return cauliflower to the pan. Gently stir through, taking care not to break the florets. Cover and cook for 3–4 more minutes until everything is tender and the masala clings without being watery.
7. Stir in garam masala and ghee. Taste and adjust salt.
8. Warm parathas directly on the induction burner or in a dry pan. Serve alongside aloo gobi with fresh cilantro and cool yoghurt.

## Special Notice

> The dry cooking style (bhuna) is key — resist adding too much water so that the sabzi stays slightly dry and the vegetables retain their individual textures. For crispier potatoes, pan-fry the parboiled cubes in a separate pan until golden before adding to the masala.
