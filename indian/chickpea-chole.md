# Chickpea Chole

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 × 400 g cans chickpeas, drained (reserve 120 mL of the liquid)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 2 dried bay leaves
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (200 g), finely chopped
- 1 tbsp chole masala (or substitute 1 tsp garam masala + ½ tsp amchur)
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tsp tamarind paste (or 1 tbsp lemon juice)
- 8 store-bought bhature, puri, or crusty rolls, warmed

## Steps

1. Drain chickpeas and reserve 120 mL of the can liquid. Roughly mash one-quarter of the chickpeas with a fork — this thickens the gravy naturally.
2. Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and bay leaves; sizzle for 20 seconds until seeds splutter.
3. Add onion and cook for 7–8 minutes until deep golden. Add garlic and ginger; cook for 1 minute.
4. Add tomatoes and cook, mashing as you go, for 6–7 minutes until completely pulpy and oil surfaces at the edges.
5. Add chole masala, ground coriander, Kashmiri chilli powder, turmeric, and salt; stir for 45 seconds, toasting the spices.
6. Add all chickpeas (including the mashed portion) and the reserved can liquid. Stir well to combine and bring to a simmer.
7. Cook uncovered for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and chickpeas absorb the flavours. Add a splash of water if it gets too dry.
8. Stir in tamarind paste, taste and adjust salt. Serve in bowls with warmed bhature, puri, or crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> Chole masala is a distinct spice blend (containing dried pomegranate seeds and black cardamom) that gives this dish its characteristic dark, tangy depth — it's worth sourcing from a South Asian grocery. The chole thickens as it sits; stir in a few tablespoons of warm water when reheating.
