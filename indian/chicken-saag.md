# Chicken Saag

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 300 g fresh baby spinach (or 200 g frozen leaf spinach, thawed and drained)
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (180 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 80 mL (⅓ cup) plain yoghurt, whisked smooth
- 60 mL water
- 1 tbsp ghee
- Naan or roti, to serve

## Steps

1. Blanch fresh spinach in boiling salted water for 1 minute. Drain, cool under running water, squeeze out as much liquid as possible, and roughly blend or chop to a coarse paste. Set aside. (If using frozen spinach, simply squeeze dry and chop.)
2. Heat oil in a large heavy pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds; sizzle for 20 seconds. Add onion and cook for 7–8 minutes until deep golden.
3. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, cumin, and turmeric; cook for 30 seconds.
4. Add tomatoes and cook for 5–6 minutes until pulpy and oil surfaces at the edges.
5. Add chicken pieces and stir to coat. Cook over medium-high heat for 5 minutes until the chicken is sealed and starting to colour.
6. Add whisked yoghurt in two additions, stirring well each time. Add 60 mL water and salt. Stir, reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook for 10 minutes until chicken is cooked through.
7. Stir in the spinach paste. Cook uncovered for 4 minutes, stirring, until spinach is fully incorporated and the sauce is thick and glossy.
8. Add garam masala and ghee; stir through. Taste and adjust salt. Serve with warm naan or roti.

## Special Notice

> Blanching and squeezing the spinach removes bitterness and preserves its bright colour. For a creamier saag, stir in 60 mL cream with the spinach. Chicken breast can be substituted but cook it for only 8 minutes in step 6 to avoid drying out.
