# Egg Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 2 dried bay leaves
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (200 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 120 mL water
- 2 tbsp plain yoghurt
- Fresh cilantro, to garnish
- Naan or roti, to serve

## Steps

1. Place eggs in a saucepan, cover with cold water by 2 cm, and bring to a boil over high heat. Once boiling, reduce heat to medium and cook for exactly 9 minutes. Transfer immediately to an ice bath. Once cool, peel the eggs.
2. Score each peeled egg with 4–5 shallow cuts using a sharp knife — this helps the curry sauce penetrate and gives the eggs a golden crust.
3. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Gently fry the scored eggs, turning, for 2–3 minutes until lightly golden on all sides. Remove and set aside.
4. In the same pan, add cumin seeds and bay leaves; sizzle for 20 seconds. Add onion and cook, stirring, for 7–8 minutes until golden.
5. Add garlic and ginger; cook for 1 minute. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, cumin, and turmeric; stir for 30 seconds.
6. Add chopped tomatoes and salt. Cook for 6–7 minutes until tomatoes are completely broken down and the masala is thick and fragrant.
7. Reduce heat to low. Stir in yoghurt in two additions and cook for 2 minutes. Add 120 mL water and garam masala; simmer for 3 minutes until the sauce reaches a thick, gravy-like consistency.
8. Gently nestle the fried eggs into the sauce, spooning sauce over them. Simmer for 2 minutes. Garnish with cilantro and serve with naan.

## Special Notice

> Scoring the boiled eggs before pan-frying allows the spiced oil to flavour the egg white and creates a satisfying textured surface that holds the sauce. For a richer sauce, blend the tomato-onion base before adding the eggs.
