# Lamb Seekh Kebabs with Raita

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g lamb mince (20% fat)
- 1 small onion (100 g), very finely grated and squeezed dry in a cloth
- 4 garlic cloves, minced to a paste
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 green chilli, very finely minced
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 2 tbsp fresh cilantro, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh mint, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp neutral oil, for cooking
- **For the raita:** 250 mL (1 cup) full-fat plain yoghurt, ½ cucumber (100 g) grated and squeezed dry, ½ tsp ground cumin, pinch of salt, 1 tbsp fresh mint

## Steps

1. Combine all kebab ingredients (lamb mince through mint) in a large bowl. Mix with your hands for 2 minutes until the mixture is smooth and homogenous — this develops the protein strands that hold the kebab together. Refrigerate for 10 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, make the raita: combine yoghurt, squeezed grated cucumber, cumin, salt, and fresh mint. Stir well and refrigerate until serving.
3. Divide the lamb mixture into 12 equal portions (about 50 g each). With wet hands, mould each portion into a sausage shape about 10 cm long, pressing firmly to compact.
4. Heat a large cast-iron or heavy pan with oil over medium-high heat until very hot. Working in two batches to avoid crowding, place kebabs in the pan.
5. Cook for 3 minutes without moving. Turn 90° and cook another 3 minutes. Turn again for a final 2 minutes to cook the underside. Total about 8 minutes per batch; the kebabs should be cooked through with a nicely browned crust.
6. Rest for 2 minutes. Serve immediately with cold raita, sliced red onion, lemon wedges, and warm naan or flatbread if desired.

## Special Notice

> Squeezing the grated onion completely dry is critical — excess moisture will make the kebabs fall apart during cooking. Chilling the mixture before shaping firms the fat and helps the kebabs hold their shape on the hot pan. These also grill excellently at high heat.
