# Tofu Matar

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g firm tofu, pressed for 10 minutes, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 200 g frozen peas
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp cumin seeds
- 1 medium onion (160 g), finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (180 g), finely chopped (or 200 g canned crushed tomatoes)
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 80 mL water
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- Fresh cilantro, to serve

## Steps

1. Press tofu cubes between paper towels and press firmly to remove surface moisture. This step is key for a good sear.
2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a large non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add tofu in a single layer and cook without stirring for 3 minutes until golden. Flip and cook another 2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
3. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the same pan. Add cumin seeds; sizzle for 15 seconds. Add onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
4. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 45 seconds. Add ground coriander, cumin, turmeric, and Kashmiri chilli powder; toast for 30 seconds.
5. Add tomatoes and salt. Cook, stirring, for 4–5 minutes until tomatoes collapse and the masala is thick.
6. Add 80 mL water and frozen peas. Stir and cook for 3 minutes until peas are tender.
7. Return tofu to the pan. Gently stir through and cook for 2 minutes to heat through and coat the tofu in the sauce.
8. Add garam masala and lemon juice. Taste and adjust salt. Serve garnished with fresh cilantro, with roti, naan, or rice.

## Special Notice

> Extra-firm tofu produces the best results here — it holds its shape during stirring and develops a satisfying golden crust. For a more traditional flavour, substitute paneer (an equal weight) for the tofu and reduce pan-frying time to 90 seconds per side.
