# Chicken Chettinad

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g bone-in chicken, cut into 8 pieces (thighs and drumsticks)
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- **Chettinad spice paste:** 2 tbsp coriander seeds, 1 tbsp cumin seeds, 1 tsp black peppercorns, 1 tsp fennel seeds, 4 dried red chillies, 3 cloves, 2 green cardamom pods, 1 cinnamon stick (3 cm) — all dry-toasted and ground to a fine powder
- 2 large onions (350 g), finely diced
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 10 fresh curry leaves
- 1 sprig fresh or 2 tsp dried kalpasi (stone flower / dagad phool) — optional but traditional
- 3 medium tomatoes (280 g), finely chopped
- 1 tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 200 mL water
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Fresh cilantro, to serve

## Steps

1. Dry-toast all Chettinad spice paste ingredients in a dry pan over medium heat for 2–3 minutes until fragrant and lightly darkened, shaking constantly. Cool briefly, then grind to a fine powder in a spice grinder. Set aside.
2. Cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, cover, reduce to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes, rest off heat 5 minutes.
3. Heat oil in a large heavy pan over medium heat. Add curry leaves and kalpasi (if using); sizzle for 20 seconds. Add onions and cook for 10–12 minutes, stirring frequently, until deeply golden brown.
4. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute until the raw smell disappears.
5. Add tomatoes and turmeric. Cook, stirring and pressing the tomatoes, for 7–8 minutes until fully broken down, oil separates, and the masala is very thick and dark.
6. Add the ground Chettinad spice powder and salt; stir for 1 minute to bloom the freshly ground spices.
7. Add chicken pieces and turn to coat thoroughly in the dark masala. Cook over high heat for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, to sear the chicken.
8. Add 200 mL water, stir, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover tightly, and cook for 20–22 minutes until chicken is cooked through and the sauce is thick and clinging. Uncover and cook 3 more minutes to reduce if needed. Stir in lemon juice, taste, and adjust salt. Serve over rice with fresh cilantro.

## Special Notice

> Freshly toasted and ground spices are non-negotiable in Chettinad cooking — the aroma and heat of this dish depend entirely on them. Kalpasi (stone flower/dagad phool) is a distinctive lichen used in Chettinad cuisine and can be found at specialty Indian grocers; it adds an earthy, slightly mossy depth. This curry keeps well refrigerated for 3 days.
