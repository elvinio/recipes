# Paneer Butter Masala

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g paneer, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 large onion (200 g), roughly chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, peeled
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 12 raw cashews (about 25 g)
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ¼ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tsp sugar
- 120 mL (½ cup) heavy cream
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi), crumbled
- 8 store-bought or homemade rotis or naan, warmed

## Steps

1. In a medium saucepan, combine onion, garlic, ginger, crushed tomatoes, cashews, and 120 mL water. Bring to a simmer over medium heat and cook, covered, for 12 minutes until onion is completely soft.
2. Let the mixture cool for 5 minutes, then blend until completely smooth. Pass through a coarse sieve if you prefer a silkier sauce.
3. Melt 1 tbsp butter with oil in a large pan over medium heat. Lightly pan-fry paneer cubes for 2 minutes per side until golden. Remove and set aside.
4. In the same pan, melt 2 tbsp butter over medium heat. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, cumin, and turmeric; toast for 30 seconds.
5. Pour in the blended tomato-cashew sauce (careful, it may splutter). Add salt and sugar; stir well and simmer for 5–6 minutes until the sauce thickens and butter begins to separate.
6. Stir in cream. Return fried paneer to the pan and simmer gently for 3 minutes.
7. Add garam masala and crumbled kasuri methi. Stir through, taste, and adjust salt and sugar.
8. Serve with warm rotis or naan.

## Special Notice

> Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) is the secret flavour note that makes this dish taste authentic — don't skip it; find it at any South Asian grocery. Paneer can be soaked in warm salted water for 15 minutes before frying to keep it extra soft and pillowy.
