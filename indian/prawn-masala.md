# Prawn Masala

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw prawns, peeled and deveined, tails on
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tsp black mustard seeds
- 10 fresh or dried curry leaves
- 2 medium onions (300 g), finely diced
- 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 medium tomatoes (200 g), finely chopped
- 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- ½ tsp ground black pepper
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 tsp tamarind paste
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Fresh cilantro, to garnish

## Steps

1. Cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, cover, reduce to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes, rest off heat 5 minutes.
2. Pat prawns dry. Season lightly with ¼ tsp turmeric and a pinch of salt. Set aside.
3. Heat oil in a large wide pan over medium-high heat. Add mustard seeds; once they pop, add curry leaves (stand back). Fry for 15 seconds.
4. Add onions and cook, stirring often, for 10 minutes until deep golden brown — this extended cooking builds the rich base.
5. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, remaining turmeric, and black pepper; cook for 45 seconds.
6. Add tomatoes and salt. Cook for 5–6 minutes, pressing and mashing the tomatoes, until fully broken down and the masala is thick and fragrant with oil surfacing.
7. Stir in tamarind paste and 60 mL water. Add prawns and toss to coat in the masala. Cook over high heat for 4–5 minutes, tossing frequently, until prawns are pink, curled, and coated in the sticky masala.
8. Add lemon juice and stir through. Taste and adjust salt. Garnish with cilantro and serve with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> The masala should be dry and clinging to the prawns, not a loose gravy — reduce it down fully before adding the prawns. Frozen prawns work well here; thaw fully and pat very dry to prevent excess water from loosening the masala.
