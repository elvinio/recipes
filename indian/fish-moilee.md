# Fish Moilee

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g firm white fish fillets (cod, barramundi, or tilapia), cut into 6 cm pieces
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 tsp black mustard seeds
- 10 fresh curry leaves
- 1 large onion (200 g), thinly sliced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 green chillies, slit lengthways (deseeded for less heat)
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 400 mL (1 can) full-fat coconut milk
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 1 medium tomato (120 g), thinly sliced into rounds
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Fresh cilantro and sliced green chilli, to garnish

## Steps

1. Cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, cover, reduce to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes, then rest off heat 5 minutes.
2. Pat fish pieces dry with paper towels. Rub lightly with ¼ tsp turmeric and a pinch of salt; set aside.
3. Heat coconut oil in a large wide pan over medium heat. Add mustard seeds — once they begin to pop, add curry leaves (step back as they spit). Fry for 15 seconds.
4. Add sliced onion and cook, stirring, for 6–7 minutes until softened and lightly golden. Add garlic, ginger, and green chillies; cook for 1 minute.
5. Add remaining turmeric and stir for 30 seconds. Pour in coconut milk and add salt. Stir gently and bring to a gentle simmer over medium-low heat — do not boil hard or the coconut milk may split.
6. Lay tomato slices on top of the simmering sauce. Place fish pieces in a single layer in the coconut broth. Spoon sauce over the top.
7. Cover and poach gently for 7–8 minutes until fish is opaque and flakes easily with a fork.
8. Stir in lemon juice carefully, moving the pan rather than a spoon to avoid breaking the fish. Garnish with cilantro and serve with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Fish Moilee is a delicate Kerala dish — keep the heat low throughout to preserve the creamy coconut sauce and prevent it from curdling or separating. Leftovers do not reheat well; this is best enjoyed immediately after cooking.
