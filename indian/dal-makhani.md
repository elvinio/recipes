# Dal Makhani

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 200 g whole black urad dal (black lentils), rinsed
- 60 g kidney beans (rajma), rinsed (or 100 g canned, drained)
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, divided
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 medium onion (160 g), finely grated or minced
- 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 1 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp garam masala
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 120 mL (½ cup) heavy cream, plus extra to finish
- 1 tsp sugar
- Warm naan or roti, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine rinsed urad dal and kidney beans in the pressure cooker. Add 800 mL cold water and ½ tsp salt. Seal lid, cook on high pressure for 25 minutes, then natural release for 10 minutes. Quick-release remaining pressure and check lentils are completely soft.
2. Roughly mash about one-quarter of the cooked dal against the side of the pot with the back of a spoon to create a creamy texture. Set aside.
3. In a separate large pan, melt 2 tbsp butter with oil over medium heat. Add the grated onion and cook, stirring, for 8 minutes until golden and dry.
4. Add garlic and ginger; cook for 1 minute. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, and cumin; stir for 30 seconds.
5. Add crushed tomatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, for 6–7 minutes until the masala is thick and oil surfaces around the edges.
6. Transfer cooked dal and beans into the tomato masala. Add remaining ½ tsp salt and sugar. Stir well and simmer over low heat for 8–10 minutes, stirring frequently to prevent sticking.
7. Stir in cream and garam masala. Simmer gently for another 3 minutes.
8. Finish with remaining 1 tbsp butter swirled in at the end. Serve in bowls with a swirl of extra cream and warm naan.

## Special Notice

> Dal makhani improves dramatically the longer it simmers — restaurant versions cook for hours. For a deeper flavour, let the dal simmer on the lowest heat for 20–30 extra minutes, adding splashes of water to maintain consistency. Leftovers thicken considerably; loosen with water or milk when reheating.
