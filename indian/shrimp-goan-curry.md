# Shrimp Goan Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- 1 large onion (200 g), finely sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1½ tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 tsp Kashmiri chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- ½ tsp ground turmeric
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar or coconut vinegar
- 400 mL (1 can) full-fat coconut milk
- 2 medium tomatoes (180 g), roughly chopped
- 6 dried red chillies (Byadgi or Kashmiri), deseeded
- 2 tbsp desiccated coconut, toasted
- Fresh cilantro, to garnish

## Steps

1. Soak dried red chillies in 80 mL boiling water for 10 minutes. Blend with soaking liquid, toasted desiccated coconut, 2 garlic cloves, half the ginger, and vinegar to a smooth paste. Set aside.
2. Cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, cover, reduce to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes, then rest off heat 5 minutes.
3. Heat coconut oil in a large wide pan over medium heat. Add sliced onion and cook, stirring, for 7–8 minutes until golden.
4. Add remaining garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute. Add ground cumin, coriander, turmeric, and Kashmiri chilli powder; cook for 45 seconds.
5. Add the blended chilli paste and stir well, cooking for 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears and the oil starts to separate.
6. Add tomatoes and cook for 4 minutes until pulpy. Pour in coconut milk and salt; bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes.
7. Add shrimp in a single layer. Cook for 3–4 minutes, turning once, just until shrimp are pink and curled — do not overcook.
8. Taste and adjust salt and vinegar. Serve immediately over steamed rice, garnished with cilantro.

## Special Notice

> Shrimp cook very quickly and will become rubbery if overdone — pull the pan off the heat the moment they turn opaque. Coconut vinegar is the traditional souring agent in Goan cooking and adds a more complex flavour than white vinegar if you can find it.
