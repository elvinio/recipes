# Lamb Rogan Josh

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Indian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g bone-in lamb shoulder, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 3 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 dried bay leaves
- 4 green cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 2 black cardamom pods, lightly crushed
- 1 cinnamon stick (about 5 cm)
- 1 large onion (200 g), thinly sliced
- 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 2 tbsp Kashmiri red chilli powder
- 1 tsp ground coriander
- 1 tsp ground fennel
- 1 tsp fine salt, plus more to taste
- 200 mL (¾ cup + 1 tbsp) plain yoghurt, whisked smooth
- 300 mL water
- 300 g (1½ cups) basmati rice, rinsed
- Fresh mint or cilantro, to serve

## Steps

1. Rinse basmati rice and set aside. Start the pressure cooker in sauté mode (or use the stovetop setting) with oil. Once hot, add bay leaves, green and black cardamom, and cinnamon; sizzle for 30 seconds.
2. Add sliced onion and cook, stirring, for 8–10 minutes until deep golden brown — do not rush this step, as browned onion is the backbone of the sauce.
3. Add garlic and ginger; stir for 1 minute. Add Kashmiri chilli powder, ground coriander, fennel, and salt; toast spices for 30 seconds, splashing in 2 tbsp water to prevent burning.
4. Add lamb pieces and stir to coat in the spice mixture. Cook on sauté mode for 3–4 minutes to seal the meat.
5. Add whisked yoghurt in three additions, stirring constantly after each addition so it doesn't split. Pour in 300 mL water and stir well.
6. Lock the pressure cooker lid, set valve to sealing, and cook on high pressure for 22 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then quick-release remaining pressure.
7. Meanwhile, cook rice in 540 mL salted water: bring to boil, cover tightly, reduce to lowest heat, cook 12 minutes, then rest off heat 5 minutes.
8. Open the cooker, switch to sauté mode, and simmer the curry uncovered for 4–5 minutes until the oil surfaces and sauce thickens slightly. Taste and adjust salt. Serve over basmati rice with fresh mint.

## Special Notice

> Kashmiri chilli powder gives this dish its signature brick-red colour with moderate heat — do not substitute regular chilli powder without reducing the quantity. The curry tastes best the next day; reheat over low heat, adding a splash of water if needed. Bone-in lamb adds richer flavour but boneless shoulder also works well.
