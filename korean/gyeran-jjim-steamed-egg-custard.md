# Gyeran Jjim Steamed Egg Custard

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 6 large eggs
- 350 mL anchovy-dashima stock (or water), lukewarm
- 1 tsp fish sauce (or soy sauce for vegetarian)
- ½ tsp fine salt
- ½ tsp sesame oil
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 1 long red chilli, deseeded and finely diced (optional, for garnish)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve
- 1 tsp neutral oil, for the pot

## Steps

1. Crack the eggs into a bowl and beat well. Gradually whisk in the lukewarm stock in a steady stream. The mixture should be completely smooth — strain through a fine sieve if needed.
2. Add the fish sauce, salt, sesame oil, and the white parts of the spring onions to the egg mixture. Stir gently.
3. Lightly oil the inside of a small, heavy-bottomed pot (an 18–20 cm pot works well). Pour in the egg mixture.
4. Place the pot over low heat on the induction stove. Cover with a lid. Cook on the lowest possible setting for 12–15 minutes, without lifting the lid, until the custard is mostly set but still has a slight jiggle in the centre.
5. Remove the lid carefully (wipe condensation from the inside of the lid before opening to prevent drops falling on the custard). The surface should be just set and slightly puffed.
6. Scatter with the spring onion greens, chilli (if using), and sesame seeds. Serve immediately in the pot as a side dish alongside rice and other banchan (side dishes).

## Special Notice

> The key to silky, soufflé-like gyeran jjim is very low heat and patience — high heat will give you a tough, bubbly custard. Using lukewarm (not cold) stock prevents the eggs from seizing up and produces the smoothest texture. This dish deflates quickly once removed from heat, so serve immediately at the table.
