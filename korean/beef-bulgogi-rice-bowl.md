# Beef Bulgogi Rice Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g beef sirloin or ribeye, very thinly sliced (ask the butcher or partially freeze before slicing)
- 400 g short-grain white rice (dry weight), cooked (about 800 mL water)
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean red chilli paste)
- 1 Asian pear or 1 kiwi (about 100 g), grated (natural tenderiser)
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced (whites and greens separated)
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (e.g., sunflower)
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 1 tsp gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes), to taste

## Steps

1. Cook the rice according to package instructions and keep warm.
2. Whisk together the soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, gochujang, grated pear, garlic, ginger, and the white parts of the spring onions in a bowl. Add the sliced beef and toss well to coat. Marinate for at least 10 minutes (or up to overnight in the fridge).
3. Heat a large heavy-based pan or wok over high heat on the induction stove until smoking. Add the neutral oil and swirl to coat.
4. Working in two batches to avoid steaming, spread the beef in a single layer and cook undisturbed for 60–90 seconds until caramelised underneath. Toss and cook a further 30–60 seconds until just cooked through. Remove each batch and set aside; repeat with remaining beef.
5. Return all the beef to the pan, toss briefly over high heat, and taste for seasoning. Add a pinch of gochugaru for extra heat if desired.
6. Divide rice into four bowls, top with bulgogi and any pan juices, scatter over the spring onion greens, and finish with toasted sesame seeds.

## Special Notice

> Leftover bulgogi keeps in the fridge for up to 3 days and reheats quickly in a dry pan. For the best caramelisation, make sure the pan is very hot and the beef is patted dry before cooking. The grated pear can be replaced with ½ cup (125 mL) apple juice in a pinch.
