# Ojingeo Bokkeum Spicy Squid

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g cleaned squid (tubes and tentacles), bodies scored in a crosshatch and cut into 4 cm pieces
- 3 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp gochugaru
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp sugar
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 medium brown onion, cut into thin wedges
- 1 medium carrot (about 100 g), julienned
- 1 red capsicum (bell pepper), cut into thin strips
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm pieces
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve

## Steps

1. Mix the gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, garlic, and ginger in a bowl to form the sauce. Taste — it should be intensely spicy, savoury, and slightly sweet.
2. Pat the squid pieces dry with paper towel — this helps them sear rather than steam.
3. Heat the neutral oil in a large wok or wide pan over high heat on the induction stove until nearly smoking.
4. Add the onion and carrot and stir-fry for 2 minutes until the onion is just translucent.
5. Add the squid and stir-fry for 1–2 minutes. Do not overcook — squid toughens quickly. It should be opaque and just beginning to curl.
6. Add the sauce, capsicum, and spring onions. Toss everything vigorously over high heat for 1–2 minutes until the sauce coats everything and has reduced slightly. Serve immediately with steamed rice, scattered with sesame seeds.

## Special Notice

> Speed is critical: squid cooked beyond 2–3 minutes becomes rubbery. If your squid releases a lot of liquid, increase the heat immediately to evaporate it fast and maintain the stir-fry texture. Scoring the squid tubes in a crosshatch pattern helps it curl attractively and cook more evenly.
