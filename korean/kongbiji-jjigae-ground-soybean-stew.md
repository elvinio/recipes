# Kongbiji Jjigae Ground Soybean Stew

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 200 g dried soybeans, soaked overnight (or at least 8 hours) in cold water
- 200 g kimchi, roughly chopped
- 150 g pork belly or shoulder, thinly sliced (optional)
- 1 medium brown onion, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp gochugaru (optional, for a hint of heat)
- 600 mL water (plus more as needed)
- 4 spring onions, sliced into 3 cm pieces
- Salt, to taste

## Steps

1. Drain the soaked soybeans. Place them in a blender with 300 mL of fresh water and blend until you have a coarse, porridge-like paste. It should still have some texture — do not blend until smooth.
2. In a medium pot over medium heat on the induction stove, cook the pork belly (if using) until lightly browned, about 3 minutes. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for 2 minutes.
3. Add the kimchi and gochugaru; stir-fry for 2 minutes until the kimchi softens slightly.
4. Pour in the soybean paste and remaining 300 mL water. Stir well to combine, scraping the bottom of the pot to prevent sticking.
5. Bring to a gentle boil, then reduce heat to medium-low. Cook, stirring frequently (the thick paste burns easily), for 20–25 minutes until the soybean mixture is cooked through and no longer tastes raw. Add more water if it becomes too thick — it should be a thick, porridge-like stew.
6. Stir in the soy sauce and sesame oil. Taste and adjust salt. Serve topped with spring onions, with steamed rice on the side.

## Special Notice

> Soaking the beans overnight is essential — insufficiently soaked beans will produce a gritty, raw-tasting stew. Stir the pot frequently during cooking as the thick paste scorches easily on the bottom. This humble, deeply nourishing stew is traditionally eaten in winter; the flavour deepens considerably the next day.
