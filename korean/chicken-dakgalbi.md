# Chicken Dakgalbi

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 3 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp gochugaru
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 5 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 200 g napa cabbage (wombok), roughly chopped into 4 cm pieces
- 150 g sweet potato (about 1 small), peeled and cut into 5 mm half-moons
- 1 medium brown onion, cut into wedges
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm lengths
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve

## Steps

1. Combine gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger in a large bowl to make the sauce. Add the chicken pieces and toss thoroughly. Set aside while you prep the vegetables.
2. Heat the neutral oil in a large, wide pan or flat-bottomed wok over medium-high heat on the induction stove.
3. Add the sweet potato and onion wedges and stir-fry for 3 minutes until the sweet potato begins to soften.
4. Add the marinated chicken and spread into an even layer. Cook undisturbed for 2 minutes to develop colour, then stir and continue cooking for a further 4 minutes.
5. Add the napa cabbage and spring onions, toss everything together, and cook for a final 3–4 minutes until the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature 75°C) and the cabbage has wilted.
6. Taste for seasoning, transfer to a serving platter, and scatter with sesame seeds. Serve immediately with steamed rice on the side if desired.

## Special Notice

> Dakgalbi is traditionally cooked at the table on a cast-iron griddle — a wide flat pan achieves the same result. Keep the heat high enough that the sauce caramelises around the edges rather than stewing. Any leftovers can be chopped finely the next day and fried with rice and a fried egg for a fantastic one-pan meal.
