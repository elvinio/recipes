# Pork Samgyeopsal Ssam

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g thick-cut pork belly slices (about 1 cm thick)
- 2 heads of butter lettuce or perilla leaves, leaves separated and washed
- 4 tbsp ssamjang (Korean wrap paste)
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 long green chilli, thinly sliced
- 1 long red chilli, thinly sliced
- 200 g kimchi, roughly chopped, to serve
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- 1 tsp neutral oil, for the pan

## Steps

1. Mix the ssamjang and sesame oil together in a small bowl; set aside as the dipping sauce.
2. Pat the pork belly slices dry with paper towel and season lightly with salt and pepper on both sides.
3. Heat a large cast-iron skillet or heavy pan over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Add just enough neutral oil to lightly coat the surface.
4. Cook the pork belly slices in a single layer (in batches if needed) for 3–4 minutes per side until deeply golden and cooked through with crispy edges. Transfer to a cutting board.
5. While the final batch cooks, add the garlic slices to the rendered fat in the pan and fry for 1–2 minutes until golden and fragrant. Remove and drain on paper towel.
6. Cut the pork into 3–4 cm pieces. Arrange the lettuce leaves, kimchi, sliced chillies, spring onions, sesame seeds, fried garlic, and ssamjang dipping sauce on the table. To eat, place a piece of pork and accompaniments into a lettuce leaf, fold, and eat in one bite.

## Special Notice

> The key to crispy samgyeopsal is a very hot, dry pan — the pork renders its own fat quickly. Serve everything at once in the centre of the table for the full communal experience. Ssamjang can be stored in the fridge for up to 2 weeks and is excellent as a dipping sauce for raw vegetables.
