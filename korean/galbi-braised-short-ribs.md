# Galbi Braised Short Ribs

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.5 kg beef short ribs (flanken-cut or English-cut), cut into 5–6 cm pieces
- 6 tbsp soy sauce
- 3 tbsp brown sugar
- 2 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 Asian pear (about 150 g), peeled and grated (or 125 mL apple juice)
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 medium brown onion, grated
- 250 mL water
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), cut into 3 cm chunks
- 8 dried jujubes (red dates) — optional but traditional
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm lengths, to serve
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Steps

1. Soak the short ribs in cold water for 20 minutes to draw out excess blood; drain and pat dry. Score the meat between the bones if using English-cut pieces.
2. Combine soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, grated pear, garlic, ginger, grated onion, and black pepper in a bowl to make the marinade. Add the ribs and turn to coat thoroughly.
3. Place the marinated ribs in the electric pressure cooker. Add the water, carrot chunks, and jujubes (if using).
4. Seal the pressure cooker lid, set to high pressure, and cook for 25 minutes. Allow natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then carefully quick-release any remaining pressure.
5. Open the lid and transfer the ribs and carrots to a serving dish. If the braising liquid is thin, set the pressure cooker to the sauté/brown function (or transfer to a saucepan) and reduce the liquid over high heat for 5 minutes until slightly syrupy.
6. Spoon the reduced sauce over the ribs. Scatter with spring onions and sesame seeds and serve with steamed rice and kimchi.

## Special Notice

> The 20-minute soak in step 1 is important for removing blood and gaminess from the ribs — do not skip it. Galbi tastes even better the next day once the flavours have had time to deepen; refrigerate overnight and skim the solidified fat from the top before reheating. The jujubes add subtle sweetness and are completely edible.
