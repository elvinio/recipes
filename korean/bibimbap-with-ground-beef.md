# Bibimbap with Ground Beef

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g short-grain white rice (dry weight), cooked
- 400 g lean ground (minced) beef
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp brown sugar
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 150 g fresh spinach, blanched, squeezed dry, and seasoned with ½ tsp sesame oil and salt
- 150 g bean sprouts, blanched 1 minute and seasoned with ½ tsp sesame oil and salt
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g total), julienned and sautéed until just tender
- 150 g shiitake or oyster mushrooms, sliced and sautéed until golden
- 4 eggs (one per bowl)
- 4 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tsp sesame oil (for the gochujang sauce)
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (for frying eggs)
- Toasted sesame seeds, to serve

## Steps

1. Cook rice and keep warm. Prepare all vegetable toppings (spinach, bean sprouts, carrot, mushrooms) individually and set each aside in small piles.
2. In a small bowl, mix the gochujang, 2 tsp sesame oil, rice vinegar, and 1 tsp sugar to make the bibimbap sauce. Set aside.
3. Heat a pan over medium-high heat. Add the ground beef with no oil and cook, breaking it up, for 4–5 minutes until browned. Add the soy sauce, 1 tbsp sesame oil, brown sugar, and garlic. Stir-fry for 1–2 minutes until fragrant and well glazed. Remove from heat.
4. In the same pan with a little neutral oil, fry the 4 eggs sunny-side up over medium-low heat until the whites are just set but the yolks are still runny. Season with a pinch of salt.
5. Divide the rice among four wide bowls. Arrange neat sections of each topping (beef, spinach, bean sprouts, carrot, mushrooms) around the edge of the rice.
6. Place a fried egg in the centre of each bowl. Add a generous spoonful of bibimbap sauce and a sprinkle of sesame seeds. Serve immediately — diners mix everything together at the table.

## Special Notice

> The joy of bibimbap is in the mixing: encourage diners to stir vigorously so the egg yolk coats everything in a glossy sauce. For the classic dolsot (stone pot) style, heat an oven-safe stone bowl in the oven at 200°C for 15 minutes, add a thin layer of sesame oil and rice, then build the toppings — the bottom rice will turn wonderfully crispy. Leftovers work well as a fried rice the next day.
