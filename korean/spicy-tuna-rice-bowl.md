# Spicy Tuna Rice Bowl

> Serves 4 | 10 minutes | Korean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 800 g cooked short-grain white rice (warm; about 400 g dry weight), freshly cooked or day-old
- 3 cans (about 400 g drained weight) tuna in springwater, drained well
- 4 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp mayonnaise (Japanese-style, e.g. Kewpie, preferred)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp gochugaru (optional, for extra heat)
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- ½ cucumber (about 150 g), thinly sliced into half-moons
- 4 sheets roasted seaweed (gim/nori), torn into pieces, to serve
- Kimchi, to serve (optional)

## Steps

1. Drain the tuna thoroughly, pressing out as much liquid as possible with the back of a spoon against the can sieve.
2. Combine the gochujang, mayonnaise, sesame oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sugar, and minced garlic in a bowl. Stir until smooth.
3. Add the drained tuna to the sauce and mix well, breaking up any large chunks. Taste and adjust — add gochugaru for more heat or a touch more mayonnaise to mellow the spice.
4. Divide the warm rice into four bowls.
5. Spoon the spicy tuna mixture over the rice.
6. Top each bowl with sliced cucumber, spring onions, sesame seeds, and torn seaweed pieces. Serve immediately with kimchi on the side if desired.

## Special Notice

> This is a pantry-friendly, no-cook meal — the only preparation needed is rice. Day-old refrigerated rice works perfectly and actually holds its texture better under the sauce. The spicy tuna mixture can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in the fridge, making this an ideal meal-prep lunch.
