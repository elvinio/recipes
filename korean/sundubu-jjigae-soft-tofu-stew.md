# Sundubu Jjigae Soft Tofu Stew

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g silken or soft (sundubu) tofu (two 400 g tubes), scooped into large spoonfuls
- 200 g raw medium prawns (shrimp), peeled and deveined (or clams/mussels)
- 150 g pork mince (optional; omit for seafood-only version)
- 3 tbsp gochugaru
- 1 tbsp gochujang
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 800 mL anchovy-dashima stock (or seafood stock)
- 1 medium brown onion, thinly sliced
- 4 spring onions, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 4 eggs (one per bowl, cracked in at the end)
- Salt, to taste

## Steps

1. Heat the neutral oil in a medium pot over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Add the pork mince (if using) and cook, breaking it up, for 2–3 minutes until no longer pink.
2. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger; sauté for 1 minute. Add the gochugaru and gochujang and stir-fry the paste mixture for 30 seconds until fragrant and slightly darkened.
3. Pour in the stock and bring to a boil. Add the soy sauce and stir to combine.
4. Gently lower spoonfuls of soft tofu into the bubbling broth — do not stir, just nudge them in. Cook for 3 minutes.
5. Add the prawns and cook for 2–3 minutes until pink and just cooked through. Stir in the sesame oil and taste for salt.
6. Crack one raw egg per serving into the stew just before serving (the residual heat cooks the whites while the yolk stays runny). Top with spring onions and serve immediately in the pot with steamed rice alongside.

## Special Notice

> Sundubu jjigae must be served at a rolling boil — traditionally brought to the table in a heated stone pot. The egg is cracked in at the last second; the carryover heat will set the white within 30 seconds. Handle silken tofu very gently as it breaks easily; large rustic spoonfuls are preferred over neat cubes.
