# Japchae Glass Noodles with Beef

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 200 g dangmyeon (sweet potato glass noodles)
- 300 g beef sirloin, cut into thin matchsticks
- 4 tbsp soy sauce (divided)
- 2 tbsp sesame oil (divided)
- 1 tbsp + 1 tsp sugar (divided)
- 150 g fresh spinach
- 150 g shiitake mushrooms, stems removed and caps thinly sliced
- 1 medium carrot (about 120 g), julienned
- 1 medium brown onion, thinly sliced
- 1 red capsicum (bell pepper), thinly sliced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 eggs, beaten with a pinch of salt
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (divided)
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Steps

1. Cook dangmyeon in a large pot of boiling water for 6–7 minutes until just tender. Drain, rinse under cold water, and cut into 15 cm lengths with scissors. Toss with 1 tbsp soy sauce and 1 tsp sesame oil; set aside.
2. Blanch the spinach in boiling water for 30 seconds, drain, rinse under cold water, squeeze dry, and season with ½ tsp soy sauce and a few drops of sesame oil. Set aside.
3. Marinate the beef in 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tsp sesame oil, ½ tsp sugar, and minced garlic for 5 minutes.
4. Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a large wok or pan over high heat. Cook the beef until just browned, about 2 minutes. Remove and set aside. In the same pan, cook the onion, carrot, and capsicum in the remaining oil for 3–4 minutes until softened. Add the mushrooms and cook for 2 more minutes. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper. Remove and set aside.
5. In the same pan over medium-low heat, pour the beaten egg in a thin layer and cook into a thin omelette. Remove, roll up, and slice into thin strips.
6. Combine noodles, beef, sautéed vegetables, and spinach in the pan (or a large bowl). Add remaining 2 tbsp soy sauce, remaining 1 tbsp sesame oil, and 1 tbsp sugar. Toss well over medium heat for 2 minutes until everything is heated through and evenly coated. Taste and adjust seasoning. Top with egg strips and sesame seeds before serving.

## Special Notice

> Japchae tastes best at room temperature, making it ideal for meal prep — it keeps well in the fridge for 3 days. If the noodles clump after refrigeration, a brief toss in a hot pan with a splash of water brings them back to life. Each component can be cooked and seasoned separately ahead of time, then combined at the last minute.
