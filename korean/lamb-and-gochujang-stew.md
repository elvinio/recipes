# Lamb and Gochujang Stew

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 900 g boneless lamb shoulder, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 3 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp gochugaru
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 Asian pear (about 120 g), grated (natural tenderiser and sweetness)
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 medium brown onion, roughly diced
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), cut into 3 cm chunks
- 300 g daikon radish, peeled and cut into 3 cm chunks
- 500 mL beef or lamb stock
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm lengths, to serve
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds, to serve
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Season the lamb pieces lightly with salt and pepper. Heat the neutral oil in a large heavy-based pot over high heat on the induction stove. Brown the lamb in two batches for 3–4 minutes per batch, turning to colour all sides. Remove and set aside.
2. Reduce heat to medium. Add the onion to the same pot and sauté for 3 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and ginger; cook for 1 minute.
3. Add the gochujang and gochugaru to the pot and stir-fry the paste for 1 minute until it darkens slightly and becomes fragrant.
4. Return the lamb to the pot. Add the grated pear, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, and stock. Stir well, scraping up the browned bits from the bottom.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 25 minutes. Add the carrot and daikon, stir, cover again, and continue cooking for a further 15–20 minutes until the lamb is tender and the vegetables are soft.
6. Taste and adjust seasoning. Scatter with spring onions and sesame seeds. Serve with steamed rice or crusty bread to soak up the sauce.

## Special Notice

> Browning the lamb well in step 1 is essential for building a rich base flavour — do not rush this step or crowd the pot. The daikon absorbs the spicy, umami-rich broth beautifully and should not be substituted with turnip if possible. This stew freezes well for up to 3 months.
