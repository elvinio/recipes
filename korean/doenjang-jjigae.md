# Doenjang Jjigae

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 3 tbsp doenjang (Korean fermented soybean paste)
- 1 tbsp gochujang
- 400 g firm tofu, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 200 g zucchini (about 1 medium), cut into 1 cm half-moons
- 150 g mushrooms (shiitake or oyster), stems removed, torn or halved
- 1 medium brown onion, cut into 2 cm chunks
- 1 medium potato (about 180 g), peeled and cut into 1.5 cm cubes
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 long green chilli, sliced
- 800 mL dashima (dried kelp) stock or water
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 4 spring onions, cut into 3 cm lengths
- Salt, to taste

## Steps

1. Bring the dashima stock to a simmer in a medium pot over medium heat on the induction stove.
2. Dissolve the doenjang and gochujang in the stock by stirring vigorously or using a spoon to press through a small sieve. This prevents lumps.
3. Add the potato and onion. Cook for 5 minutes at a steady simmer.
4. Add the zucchini, mushrooms, and garlic. Stir and cook for 4 minutes.
5. Gently add the tofu cubes and green chilli. Simmer for a further 4–5 minutes until the potato is completely tender and the tofu has absorbed the broth.
6. Stir in the sesame oil, taste for salt, and scatter over the spring onions. Serve immediately in individual bowls with steamed rice on the side.

## Special Notice

> Doenjang varies greatly in saltiness between brands — always taste before adding any extra salt. This stew is traditionally served boiling hot in a clay pot (ttukbaegi); a regular pot works perfectly well but serve quickly before it cools. Dried anchovies added to the stock in step 1 (then removed) deepen the umami significantly.
