# Kimchi Jjigae with Tofu

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g well-fermented (sour) kimchi, roughly chopped, plus 3 tbsp of the kimchi brine
- 400 g firm tofu, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 150 g pork belly or shoulder, thinly sliced (optional but traditional; omit for vegetarian)
- 1 medium brown onion, thinly sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- 1 tbsp gochugaru
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 800 mL anchovy or dashima stock (or water)
- 4 spring onions, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- Salt, to taste

## Steps

1. Heat the neutral oil in a medium pot over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Add the pork belly (if using) and cook for 2 minutes until lightly browned.
2. Add the onion and garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant. Add the gochujang and gochugaru and stir-fry the paste with the aromatics for 30 seconds.
3. Add the kimchi and kimchi brine, stir to combine, and cook for 2 minutes to allow the flavours to meld.
4. Pour in the stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a vigorous simmer. Cook uncovered for 10 minutes.
5. Gently add the tofu cubes. Simmer for a further 4 minutes, being careful not to break up the tofu too much.
6. Stir in the sesame oil and soy sauce. Taste and adjust salt. Top with spring onions and serve directly from the pot with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> The older and more sour the kimchi, the better the jjigae — recently made kimchi will taste flat in this stew. Leftovers improve overnight as the flavours deepen; reheat gently to avoid breaking the tofu. A beaten egg stirred in at the end of step 5 adds extra richness.
