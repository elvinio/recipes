# Shrimp and Zucchini Doenjang Stew

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g raw medium prawns (shrimp), peeled and deveined, tails on optional
- 2 medium zucchini (about 350 g total), cut into 1.5 cm half-moons
- 3 tbsp doenjang (Korean fermented soybean paste)
- 1 tbsp gochujang
- 1 tsp gochugaru
- 200 g firm tofu, cut into 2 cm cubes (optional, adds bulk)
- 150 g mushrooms (enoki, oyster, or shiitake), trimmed and torn
- 1 medium brown onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 800 mL dashima or seafood stock
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 4 spring onions, cut into 3 cm lengths
- Salt, to taste

## Steps

1. Bring the stock to a simmer in a medium pot over medium heat on the induction stove.
2. Dissolve the doenjang and gochujang into the stock, stirring or pressing through a spoon to prevent lumps. Add the gochugaru.
3. Add the onion and garlic. Bring back to a simmer and cook for 3 minutes.
4. Add the zucchini, mushrooms, and tofu (if using). Cook for 4–5 minutes until the zucchini is just tender.
5. Add the prawns and cook for 2–3 minutes until they are pink and curled. Do not overcook.
6. Stir in the sesame oil. Taste and add salt if needed (doenjang is salty — it may not need any). Top with spring onions and serve immediately with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> Prawns go in last and cook fast — they are done the moment they turn uniformly pink and opaque. Overcooked prawns become rubbery and lose their sweet flavour. Doenjang brands vary significantly in saltiness; always taste the broth before adding salt. A beaten egg stirred into the finished stew off the heat adds luxurious richness.
