# Haemul Pajeon Seafood Scallion Pancake

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | lunch

## Ingredients

- 200 g mixed seafood (squid rings, raw prawns, small clams or mussels), roughly chopped if large
- 2 bunches (about 200 g) spring onions, trimmed and left whole or halved lengthways
- 150 g (1 cup / 250 mL by volume) plain (all-purpose) flour
- 50 g (⅓ cup) rice flour
- 1 egg
- 240 mL ice-cold water
- ½ tsp salt
- 4 tbsp neutral oil (divided), for frying

**Dipping sauce:**
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp gochugaru
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- 1 spring onion, finely sliced

## Steps

1. Mix the dipping sauce ingredients together in a small bowl and set aside.
2. Whisk the plain flour, rice flour, egg, ice-cold water, and salt together in a bowl until a smooth, thin batter forms. Do not overmix. Rest for 5 minutes.
3. Stir the seafood into the batter.
4. Heat 1½ tbsp neutral oil in a large non-stick pan (about 26 cm) over medium-high heat on the induction stove until shimmering.
5. Lay half the spring onions in a single layer across the pan, covering the base. Pour over half the seafood batter, spreading it evenly over the spring onions. Cook for 4–5 minutes until the edges are set and golden underneath.
6. Carefully flip the pancake (a large spatula helps; alternatively, slide onto a plate and invert back into the pan). Add another 1 tbsp oil around the edges. Press down with the spatula and cook for 3–4 minutes until the second side is deeply golden and crisp. Repeat with remaining ingredients for a second pancake. Cut into wedges and serve immediately with the dipping sauce.

## Special Notice

> Ice-cold water and the addition of rice flour are the two secrets to a crispy, light pajeon — do not use warm water or skip the rice flour. Press the pancake firmly with a spatula while cooking the second side to maximise contact with the pan and achieve maximum crispness. Pajeon is best eaten immediately as it softens quickly.
