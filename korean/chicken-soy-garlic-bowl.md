# Chicken Soy Garlic Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 400 g short-grain white rice (dry weight), cooked
- 5 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp honey or corn syrup (mulyeot)
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 1 tbsp cornflour (cornstarch), dissolved in 2 tbsp cold water
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- ½ tsp freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Cook the rice according to package instructions and keep warm.
2. Whisk together the soy sauce, honey, sesame oil, rice vinegar, garlic, ginger, brown sugar, and black pepper in a bowl to make the sauce.
3. Heat the neutral oil in a large non-stick pan or wok over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Add the chicken pieces in a single layer and cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until golden brown on the bottom. Flip and cook for a further 2–3 minutes.
4. Pour the sauce over the chicken. Toss to coat and let the sauce bubble for 2 minutes, allowing it to reduce and caramelise slightly around the chicken.
5. Stir in the cornflour slurry. Toss continuously for 1 minute until the sauce thickens to a glossy glaze that coats the chicken. Remove from heat.
6. Divide rice into four bowls. Spoon the glazed chicken and any remaining sauce over the rice. Scatter with spring onions and sesame seeds and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> The cornflour slurry added at the end creates the glossy, restaurant-style glaze — add it gradually and keep stirring to avoid lumps. This sauce also works beautifully with prawns (reduce cooking time to 2 minutes total) or firm tofu. Leftovers reheat well with a splash of water to loosen the sauce.
