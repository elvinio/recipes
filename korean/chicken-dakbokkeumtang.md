# Chicken Dakbokkeumtang

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg bone-in chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks), skin on, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 3 medium potatoes (about 500 g total), peeled and cut into 3 cm chunks
- 1 medium brown onion, cut into wedges
- 2 medium carrots (about 200 g), cut into 2 cm rounds
- 3 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp gochugaru
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp brown sugar
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 250 mL water or chicken stock
- 4 spring onions, cut into 4 cm lengths
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds

## Steps

1. Combine gochujang, gochugaru, soy sauce, sesame oil, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger in a large bowl to form the sauce. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat well.
2. Place the coated chicken in a large, wide pot or deep pan over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes until the chicken is browned on most sides and the sauce begins to caramelise.
3. Add the potato, onion, and carrot. Stir to coat the vegetables in the sauce.
4. Pour in the water or stock and stir, scraping up any sticky bits from the bottom of the pan. Bring to a boil.
5. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and cook for 20–25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes are tender and the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature 75°C). Remove the lid for the last 5 minutes to allow the sauce to thicken.
6. Scatter with spring onions and sesame seeds and serve straight from the pot with steamed rice.

## Special Notice

> This is a one-pot braise that gets better the longer it sits — make it the night before if you have time. The sauce will thicken considerably as it cools; add a splash of water when reheating. Using bone-in chicken adds significantly more flavour to the braising liquid than boneless pieces.
