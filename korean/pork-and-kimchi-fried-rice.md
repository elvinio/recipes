# Pork and Kimchi Fried Rice

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Korean | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g cooked short-grain rice (day-old, cold from the fridge is best)
- 300 g pork belly or pork mince
- 250 g kimchi, roughly chopped, plus 3 tbsp kimchi brine
- 1 medium brown onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (divided)
- 4 eggs
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 1 sheet roasted seaweed (gim/nori), crumbled, to serve (optional)

## Steps

1. If using pork belly, slice it thinly into matchsticks. Mix gochujang, soy sauce, sesame oil, and sugar in a small bowl to form the sauce.
2. Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a large wok or wide pan over high heat on the induction stove. Add the pork and stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until cooked through and lightly caramelised. Add the onion and garlic and cook for 1 minute.
3. Add the kimchi and stir-fry for 2 minutes until the kimchi begins to soften and caramelise at the edges.
4. Add the cold rice, breaking up any clumps with a spatula. Stir-fry for 2–3 minutes, pressing the rice against the hot surface occasionally for some crispy bits.
5. Add the sauce and kimchi brine. Toss everything together vigorously over high heat for 1–2 minutes until evenly coated and fragrant. Taste and adjust seasoning.
6. In a separate pan, fry the eggs sunny-side up in the remaining 1 tbsp oil. Divide the fried rice among four bowls. Top each with a fried egg, scatter with spring onions, sesame seeds, and crumbled seaweed if using. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Day-old cold rice is non-negotiable for great fried rice — freshly cooked rice has too much moisture and will steam rather than fry, resulting in a gluey texture. A very hot wok or pan is equally important. The kimchi brine adds an extra hit of tangy, fermented flavour that ties the whole dish together.
