# Lamb Chops Provencal

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 8 lamb loin chops, about 100 g each, trimmed of excess fat
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 400 g cherry tomatoes, on the vine if possible
- 200 g pitted Kalamata olives
- Zest and juice of 1 lemon
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. In a small bowl, combine 2 tbsp of the olive oil with the garlic, rosemary, thyme, parsley, mustard, and lemon zest. Mix into a rough paste and season with salt and pepper.
2. Rub the herb paste all over the lamb chops, coating both sides. Let them marinate at room temperature for 15 minutes while you prepare the tomatoes.
3. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Toss the cherry tomatoes and olives with the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil and a pinch of salt and pepper in a roasting dish.
4. Roast the tomatoes and olives at 200°C for 10 minutes until the tomatoes are beginning to burst and collapse.
5. Meanwhile, heat an oven-safe frying pan or grill pan over the highest heat on the induction stove until it is very hot and beginning to smoke lightly.
6. Add the herb-coated lamb chops and sear for 2 minutes per side to develop a golden, fragrant crust. Work in two batches if the pan is crowded.
7. Transfer the seared chops to the roasting dish alongside the tomatoes. Squeeze the lemon juice over everything. Return to the oven and roast for 8–10 minutes for medium-rare (internal temperature 60°C).
8. Rest the chops for 5 minutes before serving. Spoon the burst tomatoes, olives, and pan juices generously over the chops. Serve with crusty bread to mop up the juices.

## Special Notice

> Lamb chops cooked Provencal-style are best served medium-rare to medium — longer cooking makes them tough and diminishes the fresh herb aroma. The dish can be prepped to step 2 several hours in advance; a longer marinate in the fridge only intensifies the flavour.
