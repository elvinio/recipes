# Baked Eggs en Cocotte

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | French | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 160 mL heavy cream
- 80 g Gruyère or Comté cheese, finely grated
- 80 g cooked ham or smoked salmon, torn into small pieces
- 4 tsp unsalted butter, softened
- 2 shallots, very finely minced
- 2 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley or chives, finely chopped
- 1/4 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Crusty bread or toasted soldiers, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 180°C. Lightly butter four 200 mL ramekins or small oven-safe cocottes with the softened butter, coating the base and sides completely.
2. Divide the minced shallots evenly among the four ramekins, scattering them over the base. Add the torn ham or smoked salmon on top of the shallots.
3. Pour 2 tablespoons (40 mL) of heavy cream into each ramekin. Season lightly with a pinch of nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.
4. Carefully crack 2 eggs into each ramekin, trying to keep the yolks intact and centred. Season the eggs themselves with a tiny pinch of salt.
5. Scatter the grated cheese evenly over the top of each ramekin, covering the eggs.
6. Place the ramekins in a deep roasting tray. Pour enough boiling water into the tray to come halfway up the sides of the ramekins — this bain-marie ensures gentle, even heat.
7. Bake at 180°C for 12–14 minutes. The whites should be just set and opaque, but the yolks should still be runny and bright. Check at 12 minutes — they continue cooking from residual heat after removal.
8. Remove from the water bath carefully. Scatter with fresh herbs and serve immediately in the ramekins with thick slices of crusty bread alongside for dipping.

## Special Notice

> The water bath (bain-marie) is non-negotiable for eggs en cocotte — without it, the eggs will overcook and turn rubbery around the edges. The exact baking time depends on your oven and ramekin thickness, so check early on your first attempt.
