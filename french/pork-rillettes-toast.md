# Pork Rillettes Toast

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | lunch

## Ingredients

- 600 g pork shoulder, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 150 g pork belly, skin removed, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 200 g duck fat (or lard)
- 3 garlic cloves, bruised
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- 1/2 tsp ground allspice
- 1/2 tsp fine sea salt, plus more to taste
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 8 thick slices sourdough or country bread (pain de campagne)
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- Small bunch of cornichons (gherkins), to serve

## Steps

1. Combine the pork shoulder, pork belly, duck fat, garlic, thyme, bay leaf, allspice, salt, and white pepper in a wide, heavy-based saucepan. Stir to coat everything in the fat.
2. Place over the lowest possible heat on the induction stove. The fat should barely shimmer — never boil. This gentle confit is the key to tender rillettes.
3. Cook uncovered for 35–40 minutes, turning the meat gently every 10 minutes, until the pork is completely tender and falling apart when pressed with a spoon.
4. Remove the garlic, thyme sprigs, and bay leaf. Using two forks, shred the meat directly in the pan, pulling it into coarse threads — do not blend or process.
5. Stir the shredded meat vigorously to incorporate the fat into the fibres, creating a rich, spreadable paste. Taste and adjust salt.
6. Spoon the rillettes into a bowl or ramekins, press flat, and smooth the surface. Ladle a thin layer of the rendered fat over the top to seal. Refrigerate for at least 20 minutes to firm up before serving.
7. Toast the bread slices until golden on both sides. Spread with a thin scrape of Dijon mustard if desired.
8. Bring the rillettes to cool room temperature before spreading generously onto the toast. Serve with cornichons on the side.

## Special Notice

> The fat seal on top preserves the rillettes in the refrigerator for up to 2 weeks — do not break the seal until ready to serve. For best flavour and spreadability, always serve rillettes at room temperature, never cold from the fridge.
