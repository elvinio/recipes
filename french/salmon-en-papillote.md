# Salmon en Papillote

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, about 160 g each, skin on
- 2 medium zucchini (courgettes), thinly sliced into rounds
- 1 fennel bulb, very thinly sliced, fronds reserved
- 2 shallots, thinly sliced
- 4 tbsp dry white wine (60 mL)
- 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 2 lemons, 1 sliced into rounds, 1 for squeezing
- 4 sprigs fresh dill
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 20 g unsalted butter, divided into 4 small pieces

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Cut four large sheets of baking paper, approximately 40 cm x 40 cm each.
2. Fold each sheet in half, then open flat. Arrange a layer of fennel slices and zucchini rounds on one half of each paper sheet, slightly off-centre.
3. Place a salmon fillet on top of the vegetables. Scatter the shallots over and around the fish. Season each fillet with salt and white pepper.
4. Lay lemon slices over each fillet, then place a sprig of dill and a sprig of thyme on top. Add a small piece of butter to each parcel.
5. Drizzle 1 tablespoon of olive oil and 1 tablespoon of white wine over each fillet.
6. Fold the empty half of the baking paper over the fish. Crimp and fold the edges tightly to seal each parcel completely — the steam inside is what cooks the fish.
7. Place the parcels on a large baking tray and bake for 18 minutes. The parcels will puff up as steam builds inside.
8. Transfer each puffed parcel to a serving plate and let diners open their own at the table for dramatic effect. Garnish with reserved fennel fronds and a squeeze of fresh lemon.

## Special Notice

> En papillote cooking is very forgiving — the steam prevents the fish from drying out. Check that your crimping is tight before baking, or steam will escape and the fish may overcook. Serve immediately after opening the parcel.
