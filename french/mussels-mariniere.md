# Mussels Mariniere

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 kg fresh mussels, scrubbed and debearded
- 4 shallots, finely minced
- 4 garlic cloves, finely sliced
- 300 mL dry white wine
- 50 g unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 3 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- 100 mL heavy cream (optional but classic)
- Large handful of flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 1 baguette or crusty bread, sliced and toasted, to serve

## Steps

1. Check the mussels: tap any that are open against the counter — discard any that do not close. Also discard any with cracked shells. This is a safety check, not optional.
2. Heat the olive oil in the largest pot you have over medium heat. Add the shallots and garlic with a pinch of salt and cook, stirring, for 3 minutes until soft and translucent. Do not let them colour.
3. Add the thyme sprigs and bay leaves and stir for 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Pour in the white wine and increase the heat to high. Let the wine boil vigorously for 2 minutes to cook off the alcohol.
5. Add all the mussels to the pot in one go. Cover with a tight-fitting lid and cook over high heat for 4–5 minutes, shaking the pot once or twice during cooking.
6. Lift the lid — the mussels should be wide open. Remove any mussels that are still firmly closed and discard them.
7. Using a slotted spoon, transfer the mussels to warmed serving bowls. Discard the thyme and bay leaves from the broth. Stir the butter and cream (if using) into the hot broth until the butter melts. Taste and season with salt and pepper.
8. Pour the fragrant broth over the mussels and scatter generously with chopped parsley. Serve immediately with plenty of toasted bread to soak up every drop of the broth.

## Special Notice

> Moules mariniere must be eaten the moment they are cooked — they deteriorate quickly and should never be reheated. Buy mussels on the day you plan to cook them and store in the coldest part of the fridge in a bowl covered with a damp cloth, not airtight.
