# Pork Chops Normande

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 bone-in pork loin chops, about 220 g each, 2 cm thick
- 2 large apples (Granny Smith or Cox), peeled, cored, and cut into 1 cm wedges
- 2 shallots, finely sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 150 mL dry cider (cidre brut)
- 150 mL chicken stock
- 150 mL crème fraîche
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped, to garnish

## Steps

1. Pat the pork chops completely dry with paper towels and season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil and 1 tbsp butter in a large, heavy frying pan over high heat until foaming.
2. Add the pork chops and sear for 3–4 minutes per side until golden brown and just cooked through. Transfer to a warm plate and loosely tent with foil.
3. Reduce heat to medium and add the remaining butter to the pan. Add the apple wedges and cook for 3–4 minutes, turning once, until golden on both sides. Transfer to the plate with the pork.
4. Add the shallots and garlic to the pan and cook for 3 minutes until softened, scraping up all the browned pork bits.
5. Add the thyme and cook for 30 seconds, then pour in the cider. Let it bubble vigorously, reducing by half, about 3 minutes.
6. Pour in the chicken stock and continue to simmer until reduced by a third, another 3 minutes. Stir in the Dijon mustard and crème fraîche.
7. Simmer the sauce gently for 2–3 minutes until it is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon. Taste and adjust seasoning.
8. Return the pork chops and apples to the pan to warm through in the sauce for 1 minute. Serve immediately, garnished with fresh parsley. Steamed green beans or roasted potatoes make excellent sides.

## Special Notice

> The Normande style pairs pork with the great products of Normandy: apples, cider, and cream. For extra richness, a splash of Calvados (apple brandy) added after step 4 and flambéed briefly is a traditional touch. Do not overcook the pork — it should be just barely pink in the centre.
