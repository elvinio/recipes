# Quiche Lorraine

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | lunch

## Ingredients

- 1 sheet store-bought shortcrust pastry (or 200 g homemade), chilled
- 180 g lardons or thick-cut smoked bacon, diced
- 4 large eggs
- 250 mL heavy cream (crème fraîche works beautifully)
- 80 mL whole milk
- 80 g Gruyère cheese, finely grated
- 1/4 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
- 1/2 tsp fine salt
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- 1 tsp unsalted butter (for the pan)
- Simple green salad, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 190°C. Lightly butter a 23 cm tart tin with a removable base. Press the chilled pastry into the tin, trim the edges, and prick the base all over with a fork.
2. Line the pastry shell with baking paper and fill with baking beans or dried rice. Blind bake for 12 minutes, then remove the paper and beans and bake a further 5 minutes until the base looks dry and pale golden. Remove from oven.
3. Meanwhile, cook the lardons in a dry frying pan over medium heat on the induction stove for 5–6 minutes until rendered and lightly golden. Drain on paper towels.
4. Whisk together the eggs, cream, and milk in a large bowl until smooth. Season with the nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.
5. Scatter the cooked lardons evenly over the base of the pastry shell. Sprinkle two-thirds of the Gruyère over the lardons.
6. Carefully pour the egg custard over the filling, filling right to the top. Scatter the remaining Gruyère over the surface.
7. Bake at 190°C for 28–30 minutes until the filling is just set with a slight wobble in the very centre. It will firm up as it cools.
8. Allow to cool in the tin for at least 10 minutes before slicing. Serve warm or at room temperature with a dressed green salad.

## Special Notice

> A properly baked quiche Lorraine should have a silky, custardy filling — not rubbery. Remove it from the oven while there is still a gentle wobble in the centre. Refrigerate leftovers and reheat slices at 160°C for 10 minutes.
