# Shrimp Bisque

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g whole raw shrimp, shell-on
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 medium carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 3 tbsp tomato paste
- 100 mL dry sherry or brandy
- 600 mL fish stock or light chicken stock
- 200 mL heavy cream (crème fraîche)
- 1/2 tsp sweet paprika
- 1 pinch cayenne pepper
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Fresh chives, snipped, to garnish

## Steps

1. Peel the shrimp and reserve the shells. Set aside 8 whole peeled shrimp for garnish; roughly chop the remaining shrimp flesh and refrigerate.
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-high heat on the induction stove. Add the shrimp shells and cook, stirring constantly, for 4 minutes until deeply pink and fragrant. Add the tomato paste and cook for 1 more minute.
3. Pour in the sherry or brandy — stand back as it may flame briefly. Let it reduce by half, then add the stock. Simmer for 12 minutes. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing the shells firmly to extract all flavour. Discard the shells.
4. In the same saucepan, melt the butter with the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook, stirring, for 8 minutes until very soft.
5. Add the paprika and cayenne to the softened vegetables and stir for 30 seconds. Pour in the strained shrimp stock and bring to a gentle simmer.
6. Add the chopped shrimp to the pot and cook for 2 minutes until just pink. Using a stick blender, blitz the soup until very smooth. Pass through the sieve once more for a silky finish if desired.
7. Stir in the cream and return to a gentle heat. Do not boil. Season generously with salt and white pepper.
8. In a small pan, sauté the reserved whole shrimp in 1 tsp butter for 90 seconds per side. Ladle the bisque into bowls and top with two sautéed shrimp and a scattering of chives.

## Special Notice

> Do not boil the bisque after adding the cream, as this can cause it to split. The soup base (before cream) can be made a day ahead and refrigerated — add cream only when reheating to serve.
