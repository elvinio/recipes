# Beef Bourguignon

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g beef chuck, cut into 4 cm cubes
- 150 g lardons (or thick-cut bacon, diced)
- 250 g button mushrooms, halved
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 medium carrots, sliced into rounds
- 3 garlic cloves, crushed
- 350 mL red Burgundy wine (or other full-bodied red)
- 200 mL beef stock
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 bouquet garni (bay leaf, thyme, parsley stems)
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Season the beef cubes generously with salt and pepper. Set the electric pressure cooker to sauté mode and heat the olive oil until shimmering.
2. Brown the beef in batches — do not crowd the pot — until deep brown on all sides, about 3 minutes per batch. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add the lardons to the pot and cook until golden, about 3 minutes. Add the onion, carrots, and garlic and cook for a further 3 minutes, stirring often.
4. Sprinkle the flour over the vegetables and stir to coat. Cook for 1 minute, then stir in the tomato paste.
5. Pour in the red wine and beef stock, scraping up all the browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Return the beef and any resting juices to the pot. Add the bouquet garni.
6. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 30 minutes. Release pressure naturally for 10 minutes, then manually release any remaining pressure.
7. Switch back to sauté mode and add the mushrooms. Simmer uncovered for 8 minutes until the sauce has thickened and the mushrooms are tender.
8. Discard the bouquet garni. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve over mashed potato or egg noodles.

## Special Notice

> This dish improves significantly when made a day ahead — refrigerate overnight and reheat gently. Any solidified fat on the surface can be lifted off before reheating for a cleaner sauce. Store refrigerated for up to 4 days.
