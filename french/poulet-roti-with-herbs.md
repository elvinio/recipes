# Poulet Roti with Herbs

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1 whole chicken, about 1.6 kg
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, softened (45 g)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 1 lemon, halved
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp fine sea salt
- 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 200 mL dry white wine
- 1 medium onion, quartered

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 220°C. Pat the chicken dry inside and out with paper towels — this is essential for crispy skin.
2. Combine the softened butter with the minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, and parsley in a small bowl. Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.
3. Gently loosen the skin over the chicken breast and thighs using your fingers. Push the herb butter under the skin and spread it evenly over the flesh.
4. Rub the outside of the chicken all over with olive oil, then season generously with salt and pepper. Stuff the cavity with the lemon halves and onion quarters.
5. Place the chicken breast-side up in a roasting pan. Pour the white wine into the base of the pan.
6. Roast at 220°C for 15 minutes to colour the skin, then reduce to 190°C and continue roasting for a further 35 minutes, basting once halfway through with the pan juices.
7. Test doneness by piercing the thickest part of the thigh — juices should run clear. Rest the chicken uncovered for 10 minutes before carving.
8. Pour the pan juices into a small saucepan, skim off excess fat, and bring to a simmer for 2 minutes to make a simple jus. Serve alongside the carved chicken.

## Special Notice

> Rest the chicken for the full 10 minutes — this allows the juices to redistribute and keeps the meat moist when carved. Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 3 days and make excellent sandwiches with Dijon mustard.
