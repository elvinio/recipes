# Lamb Navarin

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 750 g bone-in lamb shoulder or neck chops
- 400 g baby potatoes, halved
- 3 medium carrots, cut into 3 cm batons
- 200 g green beans, trimmed
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, sliced
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 250 mL dry white wine
- 350 mL chicken or lamb stock
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 bouquet garni (bay leaf, thyme, rosemary)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Fresh flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped, to serve

## Steps

1. Season the lamb pieces generously with salt and pepper. Set the electric pressure cooker to sauté mode and heat the olive oil until shimmering.
2. Brown the lamb in two batches until well coloured on both sides, about 3–4 minutes per side. Transfer to a plate.
3. Add the onion and garlic to the pot and cook in the residual fat for 3 minutes until softened. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1 minute.
4. Sprinkle the flour over the onions and stir for 1 minute to cook out the rawness. Pour in the white wine, scraping the bottom of the pot clean.
5. Add the stock and return the lamb with any resting juices. Tuck in the bouquet garni. Seal the pressure cooker and cook on high pressure for 25 minutes.
6. Release pressure naturally for 10 minutes, then manually release the remainder. Switch to sauté mode.
7. Add the potatoes and carrots to the pot and simmer uncovered for 10 minutes until the potatoes are just tender.
8. Add the green beans for the final 3 minutes. Discard the bouquet garni. Taste and adjust seasoning. Scatter with fresh parsley and serve directly from the pot.

## Special Notice

> Navarin is a classic French spring stew — feel free to swap the green beans for fresh peas or asparagus when in season. The braising liquid should be lightly thickened and glossy; if it seems thin, continue simmering uncovered for a few extra minutes.
