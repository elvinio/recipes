# Duck Confit Style Chicken Legs

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 large chicken leg-and-thigh pieces (about 300 g each)
- 200 g duck fat (or good-quality lard)
- 4 garlic cloves, bruised
- 4 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp coarse sea salt
- 1/2 tsp black peppercorns, lightly crushed
- 1/4 tsp ground allspice
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- Lentils du Puy or roasted potatoes, to serve

## Steps

1. At least 30 minutes before cooking, rub the chicken legs all over with the coarse salt, crushed peppercorns, and allspice. Let sit at room temperature to absorb the seasoning.
2. Preheat the oven to 160°C. Wipe off any excess salt from the chicken with a paper towel — too much surface salt will make the finished dish taste over-seasoned.
3. Melt the duck fat in a deep, oven-safe baking dish over low heat on the induction stove. Add the garlic, thyme sprigs, and bay leaves and let them infuse for 2 minutes.
4. Nestle the chicken legs skin-side down into the warm duck fat. The fat should come at least halfway up the sides of the legs; add a little olive oil if needed to top up.
5. Cover the dish tightly with foil and transfer to the oven. Cook at 160°C for 35 minutes until the meat is tender and beginning to pull away from the bone.
6. Remove the foil, increase oven temperature to 220°C, and flip the chicken legs skin-side up. Return to the oven uncovered for 10–12 minutes until the skin is deeply golden and crackly.
7. Lift the chicken from the fat and rest on a wire rack for 5 minutes to drain excess fat and allow the skin to stay crisp.
8. Serve on a bed of warm Puy lentils or roasted potatoes. Spoon a little of the aromatic cooking fat over the lentils for richness.

## Special Notice

> The rendered duck fat, strained and refrigerated, can be reused up to 3 more times for roasting potatoes or confit. The chicken can be cooked to step 5 a day ahead and stored in the fat — reheat by crisping the skin in a hot oven when ready to serve.
