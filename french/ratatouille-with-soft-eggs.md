# Ratatouille with Soft Eggs

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1 medium eggplant (aubergine), cut into 2 cm cubes
- 2 medium zucchini (courgettes), cut into 2 cm rounds
- 2 red bell peppers, deseeded and cut into 3 cm pieces
- 400 g ripe tomatoes, roughly chopped (or 1 x 400 g can crushed tomatoes)
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, sliced
- 4 large eggs
- 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp dried herbes de Provence
- 1 tbsp fresh basil leaves, torn, to serve
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- Crusty bread, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Toss the eggplant cubes with 1 tbsp olive oil and a generous pinch of salt on a large baking tray. Roast for 15 minutes, turning once, until golden and tender. Set aside.
2. Meanwhile, heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large, oven-safe frying pan or cast iron skillet over medium heat on the induction stove. Add the onion and cook for 5 minutes until softened.
3. Add the garlic, bell peppers, and zucchini to the pan. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 8 minutes until the peppers and zucchini are beginning to soften.
4. Add the chopped tomatoes and herbes de Provence. Season generously with salt and pepper. Stir to combine and cook for 5 minutes until the tomatoes have broken down into a sauce.
5. Stir in the roasted eggplant. Taste the ratatouille and adjust seasoning — it should be rich, sweet, and deeply savoury. Remove from the stovetop heat.
6. Make four wells in the ratatouille using the back of a spoon. Crack one egg into each well. Drizzle the remaining olive oil over the eggs.
7. Transfer the pan to the oven at 200°C and bake for 8–10 minutes until the egg whites are just set and opaque, but the yolks remain soft and runny.
8. Remove from the oven and scatter with torn fresh basil. Serve directly from the pan at the table with plenty of crusty bread.

## Special Notice

> The ratatouille base can be made up to 2 days ahead and refrigerated — simply reheat in the pan, make the wells, add the eggs, and bake. This is an excellent way to use a summer vegetable glut. Leftovers without eggs keep beautifully for 4 days.
