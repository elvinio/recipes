# Tofu and Mushroom Vol-au-Vent

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 sheets all-butter puff pastry (approximately 400 g total), cold
- 400 g firm tofu, pressed dry and cut into 1 cm cubes
- 300 g mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, oyster), roughly torn or sliced
- 2 shallots, finely minced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 200 mL dry white wine
- 200 mL vegetable stock
- 150 mL heavy cream
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 egg, beaten (egg wash)
- Salt and white pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 210°C. Line a baking tray with baking paper. Using a sharp knife or large round cutter (about 12 cm), cut four circles from the puff pastry. On each circle, score an inner circle about 1.5 cm from the edge — cut only halfway through the pastry, not all the way. This inner circle will become the lid.
2. Brush the pastry circles with beaten egg, being careful not to let the egg wash drip down the sides (this prevents even rising). Bake for 18–20 minutes until deeply golden and well puffed.
3. While the pastry bakes, press the tofu dry with paper towels. Heat the olive oil in a wide frying pan over high heat. Add the tofu in a single layer and sear without moving for 3 minutes until golden on the bottom. Flip and cook a further 2 minutes. Remove and set aside.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add the butter to the pan. Add the shallots and garlic and cook for 3 minutes until soft. Add the mushrooms and cook over medium-high heat for 5 minutes until the mushrooms are golden and their liquid has evaporated.
5. Sprinkle the flour over the mushrooms and stir for 1 minute. Add the thyme. Pour in the white wine and let it bubble, scraping the pan clean, then add the vegetable stock.
6. Simmer for 4 minutes until the sauce thickens. Stir in the cream and Dijon mustard. Return the tofu to the pan and fold gently to combine. Season assertively with salt and white pepper.
7. Remove the pastry cases from the oven. Using the tip of a knife, carefully lift out the scored inner lids and set aside. If the inside is doughy, return to the oven for 3 more minutes. Hollow out any excess pastry from inside the cases.
8. Fill each vol-au-vent case generously with the tofu and mushroom ragout. Rest the pastry lid at an angle on top of each one. Serve immediately — vol-au-vents go soggy quickly once filled.

## Special Notice

> Vol-au-vent cases must be filled and served immediately after baking — they soften within minutes of being filled. The tofu-mushroom filling can be made fully ahead and reheated just before serving. For a richer sauce, replace half the vegetable stock with a splash of dry sherry or Madeira.
