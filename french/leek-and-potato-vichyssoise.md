# Leek and Potato Vichyssoise

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | lunch

## Ingredients

- 4 large leeks (white and pale green parts only), cleaned and sliced
- 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper or Yukon Gold), peeled and diced
- 1 medium onion, diced
- 2 garlic cloves, sliced
- 1 litre light chicken or vegetable stock
- 200 mL heavy cream, plus extra to finish
- 30 g unsalted butter
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1/4 tsp freshly grated nutmeg
- Salt and white pepper to taste
- Fresh chives, finely snipped, to garnish

## Steps

1. Melt the butter with the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium-low heat. Add the leeks, onion, and garlic with a pinch of salt. Stir to coat in the fat.
2. Cook over low heat, stirring occasionally, for 10 minutes until the leeks are completely soft and silky — do not allow them to colour. This gentle sweating is the foundation of the soup's sweet, delicate flavour.
3. Add the diced potatoes and stir to combine. Pour in the stock and bring to a gentle boil.
4. Reduce heat and simmer for 18 minutes until the potatoes are completely tender and beginning to break apart.
5. Remove from heat. Using a stick blender, blitz the soup until completely smooth. For a truly silky vichyssoise, pass through a fine-mesh sieve, pressing the solids with the back of a ladle.
6. Return the blended soup to the pan over low heat. Stir in the heavy cream and nutmeg. Season generously with salt and white pepper. The soup should be quite generously seasoned, as chilling mutes flavours.
7. For a hot serving: bring back to a gentle simmer and taste again for seasoning. For a chilled serving: allow to cool fully, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours — vichyssoise is traditionally served cold.
8. Ladle into bowls and finish with a thin swirl of cream and a generous scattering of snipped chives.

## Special Notice

> Vichyssoise is one of the rare soups equally delicious hot or cold. If serving chilled, always taste and re-season just before serving, as cold temperatures suppress saltiness. The soup keeps refrigerated for 3 days.
