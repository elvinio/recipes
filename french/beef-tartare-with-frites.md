# Beef Tartare with Frites

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g beef fillet (or eye of round), trimmed of all sinew, very cold
- 4 egg yolks
- 3 tbsp capers, finely chopped
- 3 tbsp cornichons, finely chopped
- 1 small shallot, finely minced
- 2 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp Tabasco (or to taste)
- Salt and black pepper to taste
- 800 g floury potatoes, cut into thin frites and fried (see note)
- Fresh chives, snipped, to garnish
- Toasted rye bread or baguette, to serve

## Steps

1. Ensure the beef is very cold throughout — place it in the freezer for 15 minutes before cutting to firm it up, making precise knife work easier and safer.
2. Using a very sharp knife, slice the beef across the grain into thin slices, then cut into very fine strips, then finally into a precise 3–5 mm dice. Do not use a food processor — the texture must be hand-cut. Keep refrigerated.
3. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the Dijon mustard, Worcestershire sauce, olive oil, and Tabasco until combined.
4. Add the capers, cornichons, and shallot to the mustard mixture. Stir well to combine.
5. Add the diced beef to the bowl and fold gently to coat. Season assertively with salt and black pepper — tartare needs confident seasoning. Taste as you go.
6. Divide the tartare into four equal portions. Using a ring mould or a large round cookie cutter placed on each plate, press the tartare firmly into a neat disc shape. Carefully lift the mould.
7. Make a small indent in the centre of each portion and nestle a fresh egg yolk in the hollow. Scatter snipped chives over the top.
8. Serve immediately alongside a generous portion of freshly fried, well-salted frites and slices of toasted rye bread. Instruct diners to mix the egg yolk into their tartare at the table.

## Special Notice

> Use only the freshest beef from a trusted butcher for tartare — freshness and hygiene are paramount as the meat is served raw. Prepare and serve immediately; do not prepare tartare more than 20 minutes in advance. Pregnant individuals and those with compromised immunity should avoid this dish.
