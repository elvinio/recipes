# Steak Frites with Bearnaise

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | French | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 entrecôte (ribeye) steaks, about 200 g each, 2 cm thick, at room temperature
- 800 g floury potatoes (e.g., Maris Piper), cut into thin frites
- 3 egg yolks
- 200 g unsalted butter, clarified
- 3 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 2 shallots, very finely minced
- 2 tbsp fresh tarragon leaves, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh chervil or flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- 2 tbsp neutral oil (for steaks)
- 1 litre neutral oil (for frying)
- Coarse salt and black pepper

## Steps

1. Cut the potatoes into 5 mm thick frites and soak in cold water for 10 minutes. Drain and pat very dry with a kitchen towel — moisture causes dangerous spattering and prevents crisping.
2. Make the béarnaise reduction: simmer the vinegar with the shallots and half the tarragon in a small saucepan until reduced to about 1 tbsp. Strain and cool.
3. Heat the frying oil in a deep, heavy saucepan to 160°C (check with a thermometer or a small frite should sizzle without browning). Par-fry the potatoes in batches for 4 minutes until cooked through but pale. Drain on paper towels.
4. Make the béarnaise: Place the egg yolks and the cooled reduction in a heatproof bowl. Set over a saucepan of barely simmering water (do not let the bowl touch the water). Whisk constantly until the yolks thicken and leave a trail, about 3 minutes.
5. Remove from heat and whisk in the clarified butter in a very slow, steady stream — drop by drop at first — until a thick, glossy sauce forms. Season with salt. Stir in the remaining fresh tarragon and the chervil. Keep warm over the hot water.
6. Season the steaks generously with salt and pepper. Heat a heavy cast-iron or stainless steel pan over the highest heat on the induction stove until smoking. Add the oil and sear the steaks for 2 minutes per side for medium-rare. Rest on a warm plate for 5 minutes.
7. While the steaks rest, increase the frying oil to 190°C and fry the par-cooked frites in batches for 2–3 minutes until golden and very crisp. Drain on paper towels and immediately season with coarse salt.
8. Serve the steaks with a generous heap of frites and a ramekin of béarnaise sauce on the side.

## Special Notice

> Béarnaise is temperature-sensitive: if it starts to look grainy, remove from heat immediately and whisk in 1 tsp of cold water to bring it back. The double-fry method (par-cook then blast at high heat) is essential for frites that are fluffy inside and shatteringly crisp outside.
