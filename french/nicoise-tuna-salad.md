# Nicoise Tuna Salad

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | French | lunch

## Ingredients

- 2 x 160 g cans of good-quality tuna in olive oil, drained
- 400 g baby potatoes, boiled until tender, halved while warm
- 200 g green beans, blanched until just tender
- 4 large eggs, soft-boiled (7 minutes) and halved
- 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 80 g Niçoise or Kalamata olives
- 4 anchovy fillets (optional but traditional)
- 100 g mixed salad leaves or butter lettuce
- 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
- 1.5 tbsp red wine vinegar
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 small shallot, very finely minced
- Salt and black pepper to taste

## Steps

1. Boil the baby potatoes in well-salted water until just tender when pierced with a knife, about 12 minutes. Drain and halve while still warm so they absorb the dressing.
2. Blanch the green beans in boiling salted water for 3 minutes until bright green and just tender. Immediately plunge into a bowl of cold water to stop cooking, then drain and pat dry.
3. Cook the eggs in boiling water for exactly 7 minutes for a firm white with a just-set, slightly jammy yolk. Transfer to cold water, peel carefully, and halve.
4. Make the vinaigrette: whisk together the olive oil, red wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, and minced shallot. Season well with salt and pepper.
5. While the potatoes are still warm, toss them gently in half the vinaigrette in a large bowl.
6. Arrange the salad leaves on a large serving platter or in individual bowls. Lay the dressed potatoes, green beans, cherry tomatoes, and olives over the leaves.
7. Break the tuna into generous chunks and scatter over the salad. Nestle the egg halves throughout. Drape the anchovy fillets over the top if using.
8. Drizzle the remaining vinaigrette over everything. Season with a final crack of black pepper and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> The key to a great Niçoise is dressing the potatoes while warm — they absorb the vinaigrette and carry much more flavour than cold potatoes. Assemble the salad just before serving to prevent the leaves from wilting.
