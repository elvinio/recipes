# Stuffed Bell Peppers with Ground Beef

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 large bell peppers (mixed colors), tops cut off and seeds removed
- 450 g ground beef (85/15)
- 200 g (1 cup) cooked long-grain white rice
- 1 can (400 g) diced tomatoes, drained (juice reserved)
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp dried Italian seasoning
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 150 g shredded mozzarella cheese
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 120 mL (1/2 cup) beef broth

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 190°C. Slice the tops off the bell peppers and remove seeds and membranes. If peppers don't stand upright, slice a thin sliver off the bottom.
2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and cook for 3 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
3. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it apart, for 5–6 minutes until browned and no longer pink. Drain excess fat.
4. Stir in drained diced tomatoes, tomato paste, Italian seasoning, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Cook for 2 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in the cooked rice. Taste and adjust seasoning.
5. Pour the reserved tomato juice and beef broth into the bottom of a baking dish large enough to hold all four peppers snugly.
6. Stand peppers upright in the baking dish. Spoon the beef and rice filling tightly into each pepper, mounding it slightly above the rim.
7. Cover the dish tightly with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove foil, top each pepper with shredded mozzarella, and bake uncovered for a further 10 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
8. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving with the pan juices spooned around.

## Special Notice

> Use day-old cooked rice if possible — it is drier than freshly cooked and yields a better filling texture. The broth in the bottom of the dish creates steam that helps cook the pepper walls through; don't skip it. Leftovers reheat very well at 180°C for 15 minutes covered with foil, or in the microwave for 2–3 minutes.
