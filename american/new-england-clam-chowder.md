# New England Clam Chowder

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 cans (185 g each) chopped clams, juice reserved
- 150 g bacon, cut into 1 cm pieces
- 3 medium Yukon Gold potatoes (about 500 g), peeled and cut into 1 cm cubes
- 1 large yellow onion, diced
- 3 celery stalks, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 40 g plain flour (about 4 tbsp)
- 480 mL (2 cups) whole milk
- 240 mL (1 cup) heavy cream
- 240 mL (1 cup) clam juice (from cans, plus water if needed to reach 1 cup)
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp white pepper
- Oyster crackers and fresh chives for serving

## Steps

1. Cook bacon in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat until crispy, about 6 minutes. Remove bacon with a slotted spoon and set aside on paper towels, leaving the fat in the pot.
2. Add butter to the bacon fat. Once melted, add onion and celery. Cook over medium heat for 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and thyme and cook for 1 minute.
3. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir for 2 minutes to cook out the raw taste.
4. Gradually whisk in the clam juice, then the milk, ensuring no lumps form. Add bay leaves, potatoes, salt, and white pepper. Bring to a simmer.
5. Cook for 12–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until potatoes are completely tender.
6. Stir in the heavy cream and drained clams. Reduce heat to low and simmer gently for 3–4 minutes — do not boil once the clams are added or they will toughen.
7. Remove bay leaves. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve topped with the reserved crispy bacon, fresh chives, and oyster crackers.

## Special Notice

> Never boil the chowder once the cream and clams are added — a vigorous boil will cause the dairy to separate and the clams to become rubbery. For a richer flavor, substitute 120 mL of the milk with additional heavy cream. Chowder thickens considerably as it sits; loosen leftovers with a splash of milk when reheating over low heat.
