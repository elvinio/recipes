# Meatloaf with Tomato Gravy

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g ground beef (80/20)
- 150 g plain breadcrumbs
- 1 medium yellow onion, finely grated
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 large eggs
- 60 mL (1/4 cup) whole milk
- 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- **Tomato Gravy:**
- 1 can (400 g) crushed tomatoes
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1/2 tsp dried basil
- 1/2 tsp salt

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 190°C. Lightly grease a 23 x 13 cm loaf pan or line a rimmed baking sheet with foil.
2. In a large bowl, combine breadcrumbs, grated onion, garlic, eggs, milk, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, and thyme. Mix well. Add the ground beef and gently combine with your hands just until uniform — do not overmix or the meatloaf will be dense.
3. Shape the mixture into a loaf about 20 cm long on the prepared pan, or press it evenly into the loaf pan.
4. Make the tomato gravy: stir together crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, olive oil, garlic powder, sugar, basil, and salt in a bowl. Spoon half of the gravy evenly over the top of the meatloaf.
5. Bake for 40–45 minutes until the internal temperature reads 71°C. If the top begins to darken too quickly, tent loosely with foil.
6. Warm the remaining gravy in a small saucepan over low heat for 5 minutes.
7. Let the meatloaf rest for 5 minutes before slicing. Serve with the warm tomato gravy spooned over each slice.

## Special Notice

> Grating the onion rather than chopping it ensures moisture throughout the loaf and eliminates crunchy onion pieces. Meatloaf leftovers make excellent sandwiches — cold slices on toasted bread with the gravy. Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 4 days, or freeze individual slices for up to 2 months.
