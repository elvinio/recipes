# Macaroni and Cheese

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 350 g dried elbow macaroni
- 40 g unsalted butter
- 40 g plain flour (about 4 tbsp)
- 720 mL (3 cups) whole milk, warmed
- 200 g sharp cheddar cheese, grated
- 100 g Gruyère cheese, grated
- 50 g Parmesan cheese, grated (plus extra for topping)
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp white pepper
- 60 g panko breadcrumbs
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter, melted (for topping)

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Cook macaroni in a large pot of well-salted boiling water for 2 minutes less than the package directions (it will finish cooking in the oven). Drain and set aside.
2. Melt 40 g butter in the same pot over medium heat. Whisk in flour and cook for 2 minutes, stirring constantly, to make a roux.
3. Gradually whisk in the warm milk, about 120 mL at a time, ensuring each addition is fully incorporated before adding more. Continue whisking until the sauce is smooth and begins to thicken, about 4–5 minutes.
4. Reduce heat to low. Stir in mustard, garlic powder, smoked paprika, cayenne, salt, and white pepper. Add the grated cheddar, Gruyère, and Parmesan, stirring until completely melted and smooth.
5. Fold the drained macaroni into the cheese sauce until evenly coated. Transfer to a greased 23 x 33 cm baking dish.
6. Combine panko breadcrumbs with the melted butter and a handful of extra Parmesan. Scatter evenly over the top of the mac and cheese.
7. Bake for 20 minutes until bubbling at the edges and the breadcrumb topping is golden brown. Rest for 5 minutes before serving.

## Special Notice

> Grating your own cheese is essential — pre-shredded cheese contains anti-caking agents that prevent it from melting smoothly into the sauce. Undercooking the pasta by 2 minutes before baking prevents it from going mushy. Leftovers keep for up to 4 days refrigerated; reheat covered with foil at 175°C for 15 minutes, adding a splash of milk to loosen the sauce.
