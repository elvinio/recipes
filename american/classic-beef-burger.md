# Classic Beef Burger

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 680 g ground beef (80/20 blend)
- 4 brioche burger buns
- 4 slices cheddar cheese (about 80 g)
- 1 large tomato, sliced
- 4 leaves butter lettuce
- 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
- 8 slices dill pickle
- 2 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tsp garlic powder
- 3 tbsp mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp yellow mustard

## Steps

1. Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 170 g each). Gently shape into patties roughly 1 cm wider than the buns; press a shallow dimple in the center of each to prevent doming. Season generously with salt, pepper, and garlic powder on both sides.
2. Heat a large cast-iron or heavy skillet over high heat until very hot, about 2 minutes. Add butter and let it foam and subside.
3. Place patties in the pan without pressing them down. Cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until a deep brown crust forms on the bottom.
4. Flip patties once and cook for another 2–3 minutes for medium doneness (internal temperature 71°C). Place a cheese slice on each patty during the last minute of cooking and cover loosely with a lid to melt.
5. While patties rest for 2 minutes, toast the cut sides of the buns in the same pan over medium heat for 1–2 minutes until lightly golden.
6. Spread mayonnaise on the bottom buns and mustard on the top buns. Layer lettuce, tomato, and onion on the bottom bun, set the cheesy patty on top, then add pickles before closing with the top bun.

## Special Notice

> Burgers are best eaten immediately while the crust is still crisp. For juicier patties, avoid overworking the meat when forming — handle it as little as possible. Leftover cooked patties can be refrigerated for up to 2 days and reheated gently in a covered pan with a splash of water.
