# Tuna Melt Sandwich

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | American | lunch

## Ingredients

- 3 cans (145 g each) tuna in water, well drained
- 8 slices sourdough or sandwich bread
- 8 slices cheddar cheese (about 160 g)
- 80 g (1/3 cup) mayonnaise
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 1/4 small red onion, finely diced
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 1/4 tsp black pepper
- 1/4 tsp celery salt
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter, softened

## Steps

1. Drain the tuna thoroughly, pressing out as much liquid as possible. Flake into a medium bowl.
2. Add mayonnaise, celery, red onion, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, parsley, black pepper, and celery salt to the tuna. Mix gently until evenly combined. Taste and adjust seasoning.
3. Butter one side of each bread slice generously with softened butter.
4. Place 4 slices of bread butter-side down in a large skillet over medium heat. Lay 1 slice of cheese on each, then spoon a quarter of the tuna mixture evenly over the cheese. Top with a second slice of cheese, then close with the remaining bread slices, butter-side up.
5. Cook for 3–4 minutes until the bottom is golden brown and crisp. Carefully flip each sandwich and press down lightly with a spatula. Cook for another 2–3 minutes until the second side is golden and the cheese is fully melted.
6. Remove from heat, let rest for 1 minute, then slice diagonally and serve hot.

## Special Notice

> Pressing out every last drop of water from the tuna is critical — wet tuna makes the bread soggy. Using two slices of cheese (one on each side of the filling) ensures maximum melt and helps hold the sandwich together when flipping. The tuna salad mixture can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored refrigerated; assemble and grill sandwiches just before serving.
