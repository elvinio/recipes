# Chicken Pot Pie Skillet

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into 2 cm pieces
- 3 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 medium carrots, sliced into coins
- 2 celery stalks, sliced
- 150 g frozen peas
- 40 g plain flour (about 4 tbsp)
- 480 mL (2 cups) chicken broth
- 120 mL (1/2 cup) whole milk
- 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 tsp dried)
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1 sheet puff pastry (about 230 g), thawed
- 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

## Steps

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C. Season chicken pieces with salt and pepper.
2. Melt butter in a large oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned. Remove to a plate.
3. In the same skillet, add onion, carrots, and celery. Cook over medium heat for 4–5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and thyme and cook for 1 more minute.
4. Sprinkle flour over the vegetables and stir for 1 minute to cook out the raw flour taste. Gradually whisk in chicken broth, then milk, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom.
5. Return chicken to the skillet along with frozen peas. Bring the filling to a simmer, stirring until it thickens to a creamy consistency, about 3 minutes. Taste and adjust seasoning.
6. Unroll the puff pastry and lay it over the filling, tucking the edges down around the inside of the pan. Brush the top with beaten egg. Cut 3–4 small slits in the pastry to vent steam.
7. Transfer the skillet to the oven and bake for 18–20 minutes until the pastry is deep golden and puffed. Let rest for 5 minutes before serving directly from the skillet.

## Special Notice

> Use an oven-safe skillet (cast iron is ideal) — check that your pan handle is oven-safe before placing it in the oven. Leftovers reheat well at 175°C for 10–12 minutes; the pastry won't be as crisp but the filling is even more flavorful the next day. You can substitute rotisserie chicken (shredded) and skip the browning step, adding it at step 5 to save 10 minutes.
