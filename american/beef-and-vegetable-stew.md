# Beef and Vegetable Stew

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g beef chuck, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 3 medium carrots, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 3 celery stalks, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 2 medium yellow onions, cut into wedges
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 300 g button mushrooms, halved
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 480 mL (2 cups) beef broth
- 120 mL (1/2 cup) dry red wine (or extra broth)
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp plain flour
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

## Steps

1. Pat beef cubes completely dry and season with salt and pepper. Toss with flour to lightly coat.
2. Set the electric pressure cooker to the sauté function on high. Add olive oil. Working in batches, sear beef for 2–3 minutes per side until deeply browned. Do not crowd the pot. Remove to a plate.
3. Add onions and mushrooms to the pot and cook for 3 minutes, stirring to scrape up the browned bits. Add garlic and tomato paste and stir for 1 minute.
4. Pour in the red wine and deglaze the pot thoroughly, scraping every bit from the bottom — this is critical to prevent a burn warning. Let the wine bubble for 1 minute.
5. Return the seared beef and any accumulated juices to the pot. Add beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaves. Stir to combine.
6. Cancel sauté mode. Seal the lid and cook on high pressure for 30 minutes. Allow a natural pressure release for 10 minutes, then quick-release any remaining pressure.
7. Open the lid and add carrots and celery. Seal and cook on high pressure for an additional 5 minutes, then quick-release. Remove bay leaves, taste and adjust seasoning, and serve garnished with fresh parsley.

## Special Notice

> Thorough deglazing in step 4 is mandatory for pressure cooker use — any residue stuck to the bottom will trigger a burn error mid-cook. The stew thickens considerably as it cools; loosen with a splash of broth when reheating. This stew is even better the next day and stores refrigerated for up to 4 days, or frozen for up to 3 months.
