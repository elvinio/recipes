# Smoky Lamb Flatbread Pizza

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 large flatbreads or naan (about 200 g each)
- 400 g ground lamb
- 1 medium red onion, half finely diced, half thinly sliced (for topping)
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp ground coriander
- 1/2 tsp chili flakes
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 240 g (1 cup) tomato passata
- 200 g shredded mozzarella cheese
- 80 g crumbled feta cheese
- 60 g kalamata olives, pitted and halved
- 30 g fresh mint leaves
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- Extra olive oil for drizzling

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat as well.
2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3 minutes. Add garlic and cook 1 minute more.
3. Add ground lamb and cook for 5–6 minutes, breaking it up, until browned and cooked through. Drain excess fat.
4. Stir in cumin, smoked paprika, coriander, chili flakes, cinnamon, and salt. Cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Remove from heat.
5. Spread a thin layer of tomato passata over each flatbread, leaving a 1 cm border. Distribute the spiced lamb evenly over all four flatbreads. Scatter mozzarella over the lamb.
6. Carefully slide the flatbreads onto the preheated baking sheet. Bake for 10–12 minutes until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the edges of the flatbread are golden and crisp.
7. Remove from oven. Top with crumbled feta, sliced red onion, kalamata olives, and fresh mint. Drizzle with olive oil and a squeeze of lemon juice. Slice and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Preheating the baking sheet is a key technique that gives the flatbread a crispier bottom crust — do not skip this step. Ground lamb has a higher fat content than beef, so drain it well after browning to prevent a soggy base. Assembled unbaked flatbreads can be refrigerated for up to 2 hours before baking; add the fresh toppings only after they come out of the oven.
