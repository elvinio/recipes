# Shrimp Po Boy Sandwich

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 French bread rolls or hoagie rolls (about 20 cm each)
- 120 g plain flour
- 60 g cornmeal
- 1 tsp Old Bay seasoning
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 240 mL (1 cup) vegetable oil for frying
- **Remoulade:**
- 120 g (1/2 cup) mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp Creole mustard or whole-grain mustard
- 1 tbsp hot sauce
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- **For serving:**
- 100 g shredded iceberg lettuce
- 2 medium tomatoes, thinly sliced
- 1 lemon, cut into wedges

## Steps

1. Make the remoulade: whisk together mayonnaise, Creole mustard, hot sauce, lemon juice, smoked paprika, and garlic. Season with salt. Refrigerate until needed.
2. In a shallow bowl, combine flour, cornmeal, Old Bay, smoked paprika, cayenne, garlic powder, and salt. Place beaten eggs in a separate bowl.
3. Pat shrimp dry. Dip each shrimp in the egg, letting excess drip off, then dredge in the flour-cornmeal mixture, pressing to adhere. Set on a plate.
4. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat to approximately 180°C. Test with a pinch of flour — it should sizzle immediately.
5. Fry shrimp in batches without crowding, 1.5–2 minutes per side until golden, crisp, and cooked through. Transfer to a paper towel-lined plate. Season with a pinch of salt immediately.
6. Slice the rolls lengthwise, leaving one side attached as a hinge. Toast the cut sides in the pan with a little of the frying oil for 1 minute until golden.
7. Spread a generous amount of remoulade on both cut sides of each roll. Layer shredded lettuce and tomato slices in the roll. Pile in the fried shrimp — be generous. Serve at once with lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Work quickly when frying — shrimp overcook in seconds, so do not leave them unattended. The cornmeal in the dredge is what gives the coating its distinctive crunch; do not substitute all-purpose flour for it. Po'boys are a notoriously messy sandwich; serve with napkins and eat immediately while the shrimp are still crispy.
