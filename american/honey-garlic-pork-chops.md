# Honey Garlic Pork Chops

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 bone-in pork chops, about 2.5 cm thick (roughly 220 g each)
- 1 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tbsp unsalted butter
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 6 garlic cloves, minced
- 80 mL (1/3 cup) honey
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp apple cider vinegar
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

## Steps

1. Pat pork chops dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, apple cider vinegar, and Dijon mustard. Set aside.
3. Heat olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat until the butter foams and subsides.
4. Add pork chops and sear undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until a deep golden crust forms. Flip and cook the other side for 3 minutes.
5. Reduce heat to medium. Add minced garlic and rosemary to the pan alongside the chops; stir and cook for 30 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
6. Pour the honey-soy mixture over the chops. Let it bubble and reduce, basting the chops frequently, for 2–3 minutes until the sauce is thick and glossy and the internal temperature of the chops reaches 63°C.
7. Remove from heat and let the chops rest in the pan for 3 minutes. Spoon the sauce over the top, garnish with parsley, and serve.

## Special Notice

> Bone-in chops retain more moisture and flavor than boneless; if substituting boneless, reduce cooking time by 1–2 minutes per side. The honey garlic sauce can scorch quickly — watch the heat carefully once it goes in and keep basting. Leftovers keep refrigerated for up to 3 days; reheat gently in a covered pan with a splash of water to loosen the sauce.
