# Sweet Potato and Black Bean Bowl

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | lunch

## Ingredients

- 2 large sweet potatoes (about 700 g total), peeled and cut into 2 cm cubes
- 2 cans (400 g each) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 medium red onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp chili powder
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 200 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 avocado, sliced
- 120 mL (1/2 cup) sour cream or plain Greek yogurt
- 30 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 lime, cut into wedges

## Steps

1. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add sweet potato cubes in a single layer. Cook undisturbed for 5 minutes to develop a golden crust, then stir and continue cooking for another 5–6 minutes until tender and caramelized on all sides. Season with half the salt, smoked paprika, and chili powder. Remove to a plate.
2. In the same skillet, heat the remaining olive oil over medium heat. Add red onion and bell pepper; cook for 4–5 minutes until softened.
3. Add garlic, cumin, garlic powder, and remaining salt. Stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add the black beans and stir to coat them in the spices. Cook for 3–4 minutes until heated through. Squeeze in the lime juice and stir.
5. Return the sweet potatoes to the pan and gently fold everything together. Cook for 2 more minutes, tasting and adjusting seasoning.
6. Divide the mixture among four bowls. Top each with cherry tomatoes, sliced avocado, a dollop of sour cream, and fresh cilantro. Serve with lime wedges.

## Special Notice

> Don't rush the sweet potato — letting it sit undisturbed in the pan is what creates the caramelized crust that makes this dish so satisfying. The bowl components keep well separately in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, making this excellent for meal prep; store the avocado and sour cream separately and add fresh at serving time. Add a fried egg on top for an extra protein boost.
