# Corn and Black Bean Chili

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 cans (400 g each) black beans, drained and rinsed
- 300 g frozen or fresh corn kernels
- 1 can (400 g) diced tomatoes
- 240 mL (1 cup) vegetable broth
- 1 medium yellow onion, diced
- 1 red bell pepper, diced
- 1 green bell pepper, diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 2 tbsp chili powder
- 1 tbsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1/2 tsp cayenne pepper
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- Sour cream, shredded cheddar, and cilantro for serving

## Steps

1. Set the electric pressure cooker to the sauté function. Heat olive oil, then add onion and both bell peppers. Cook for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
2. Add garlic, chili powder, cumin, smoked paprika, cayenne, oregano, and salt. Stir and cook for 1 minute until the spices are fragrant.
3. Add the drained black beans, corn, diced tomatoes (with their liquid), and vegetable broth. Stir everything together.
4. Cancel the sauté function. Seal the lid and set to high pressure for 12 minutes.
5. Once cooking is complete, use a quick pressure release. Carefully open the lid.
6. Stir the chili and use the back of a spoon or a potato masher to lightly crush about a quarter of the beans directly in the pot — this thickens the chili naturally. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Serve in bowls topped with sour cream, shredded cheddar, and fresh cilantro.

## Special Notice

> This chili improves dramatically overnight as the spices meld — make it a day ahead for best flavor. It freezes beautifully in individual portions for up to 3 months. For a smokier depth, stir in 1 tsp of chipotle in adobo sauce when adding the spices in step 2.
