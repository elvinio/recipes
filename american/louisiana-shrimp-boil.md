# Louisiana Shrimp Boil

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 900 g large shell-on shrimp (16/20 count), deveined
- 500 g baby potatoes, halved if large
- 4 ears of corn, each cut into 3 pieces
- 200 g andouille sausage, sliced into 2 cm coins
- 3 tbsp Old Bay seasoning
- 1 tbsp cayenne pepper
- 2 tbsp kosher salt
- 4 garlic cloves, smashed
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 lemon, halved (plus more wedges for serving)
- 60 g unsalted butter, melted
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
- 2 L water

## Steps

1. Fill a large pot with 2 L of water. Add Old Bay, cayenne, kosher salt, garlic, bay leaves, and squeeze in the lemon halves, then drop them in the pot. Bring to a rolling boil over high heat.
2. Add the baby potatoes to the boiling water. Cook for 12 minutes until just tender when pierced with a knife.
3. Add the corn pieces and andouille sausage. Cook for 5 minutes.
4. Add the shrimp and cook for just 2–3 minutes, until they curl and turn pink. Do not overcook.
5. Drain the pot through a large colander, discarding the bay leaves and lemon halves. Transfer everything to a large serving platter or spread on a newspaper-lined table in true Louisiana style.
6. Drizzle melted butter over the top and sprinkle with fresh parsley. Serve immediately with lemon wedges and plenty of napkins.

## Special Notice

> Shrimp cook extremely fast — pull them the moment they turn opaque and pink to avoid a rubbery texture. Old Bay seasoning is the backbone of a proper shrimp boil; adjust cayenne up or down to dial in your preferred heat level. Leftovers can be refrigerated for 1 day; peel the shrimp before storing and enjoy cold as a seafood salad.
