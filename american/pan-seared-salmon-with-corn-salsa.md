# Pan Seared Salmon with Corn Salsa

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, skin-on (about 170 g each)
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1 tsp kosher salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
- 300 g fresh or thawed frozen corn kernels (about 2 ears)
- 1 red bell pepper, finely diced
- 1/2 small red onion, finely diced
- 1 jalapeño, seeded and minced
- 30 g fresh cilantro, chopped
- 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
- 1 tbsp olive oil (for salsa)
- 1/2 tsp salt (for salsa)
- 1 lime, cut into wedges for serving

## Steps

1. Pat salmon fillets completely dry with paper towels — this is key for a good sear. Season flesh side with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika.
2. Make the corn salsa: in a bowl, combine corn kernels, red bell pepper, red onion, jalapeño, and cilantro. Dress with lime juice, olive oil, and salt. Toss well and set aside.
3. Heat a large non-stick or stainless skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add olive oil.
4. Place salmon fillets skin-side down in the pan. Press each fillet gently with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling. Cook undisturbed for 4–5 minutes until the skin is golden and crisp and the flesh is cooked about two-thirds of the way up the side.
5. Flip the fillets and cook for a further 1–2 minutes until the center reaches 52°C for medium, or 60°C for well done.
6. Rest fillets on a plate for 2 minutes. Spoon corn salsa generously over or alongside each fillet and serve with lime wedges.

## Special Notice

> Salmon fillets can vary significantly in thickness; thinner tail pieces will cook faster, so watch them closely. Leftovers can be refrigerated for up to 2 days and are excellent served cold over salad greens with the corn salsa. Do not reheat salmon at high heat as it dries out quickly — a gentle warm in a covered pan with a few drops of water works best.
