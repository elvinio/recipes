# Eggs Benedict with Hollandaise

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs (4 for poaching, 3 for hollandaise, 1 extra as backup)
- 4 English muffins, split
- 8 slices Canadian bacon (about 200 g)
- **Hollandaise Sauce:**
- 3 large egg yolks
- 200 g unsalted butter
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- Pinch of cayenne pepper
- Salt to taste
- **Poaching:**
- 1.5 L water
- 2 tbsp white wine vinegar
- 1 tsp salt

## Steps

1. Make the hollandaise: melt butter in a small saucepan over low heat until just melted and warm (not hot). In a heatproof bowl, whisk egg yolks, lemon juice, and Dijon until pale and slightly thickened, about 1 minute. Set the bowl over a pot of barely simmering water (do not let the bowl touch the water). Whisk constantly for 3–4 minutes until yolks are thick and leave a ribbon trail.
2. Remove the bowl from heat. Very slowly drizzle in the warm melted butter, whisking constantly, until fully emulsified and sauce coats the back of a spoon. Season with cayenne and salt. Keep warm by leaving the bowl over the hot water (off the heat), whisking occasionally.
3. Bring 1.5 L water with vinegar and salt to a gentle simmer in a wide saucepan — bubbles should barely break the surface.
4. While water heats, pan-fry the Canadian bacon in a dry skillet over medium heat for 1–2 minutes per side until lightly browned. Set aside, covered to keep warm. Toast English muffins in the same pan or under the grill.
5. Crack each egg into a small cup. Stir the simmering water to create a gentle swirl, then slide in eggs one at a time. Poach in two batches of 2–3 eggs for 3 minutes each for a runny yolk, or 4 minutes for a set yolk. Remove with a slotted spoon and blot dry on a towel.
6. Assemble: place two English muffin halves on each plate, top each with a slice of Canadian bacon, then a poached egg. Spoon hollandaise generously over the top.
7. Serve immediately with a pinch of cayenne and fresh chives if desired.

## Special Notice

> Hollandaise must be made fresh and served promptly — it cannot be stored safely and will break if reheated. If the sauce begins to curdle, immediately whisk in 1 tbsp of cold water to bring it back. The most common poaching mistake is using vigorously boiling water; the water must only simmer gently to produce neat, compact eggs.
