# Crab Cakes with Remoulade

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | American | dinner

## Ingredients

- 450 g lump crab meat, picked over for shells
- 60 g plain breadcrumbs (panko preferred)
- 1 large egg, beaten
- 3 tbsp mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
- 2 tbsp fresh parsley, finely chopped
- 2 spring onions, finely sliced
- 1/2 tsp Old Bay seasoning
- 1/4 tsp cayenne pepper
- 3 tbsp olive oil, for frying
- **Remoulade:**
- 120 g (1/2 cup) mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp capers, roughly chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tsp hot sauce
- 1 tsp smoked paprika
- 1 garlic clove, minced

## Steps

1. Make the remoulade: whisk together all remoulade ingredients in a small bowl until smooth. Season with salt to taste. Cover and refrigerate until serving.
2. In a large bowl, gently combine crab meat, breadcrumbs, egg, mayonnaise, Dijon, Worcestershire sauce, parsley, spring onions, Old Bay, and cayenne. Mix carefully to preserve the crab lumps.
3. Divide the mixture into 8 equal portions. Shape each into a patty about 2 cm thick. Place on a plate, cover, and refrigerate for 15 minutes — chilling helps them hold together during cooking.
4. Heat olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
5. Carefully add the crab cakes and cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until the bottom is deep golden brown. Flip gently and cook for another 3 minutes on the second side.
6. Drain briefly on a paper towel. Serve 2 crab cakes per person with a generous dollop of remoulade and lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> The chilling step is not optional — it is the key technique that prevents the cakes from falling apart in the pan. Use the best quality lump crab you can find; canned crab works but fresh or refrigerated pasteurized crab gives a far superior result. Uncooked shaped cakes can be refrigerated overnight or frozen (before frying) for up to 1 month.
