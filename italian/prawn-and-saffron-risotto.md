# Prawn and Saffron Risotto

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 320 g Carnaroli or Arborio rice
- 600 g large raw prawns, peeled and deveined (shells reserved)
- ½ tsp saffron threads
- 1.2 L (5 cups) light fish or vegetable stock
- 1 small onion, finely diced
- 1 shallot, finely minced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 120 mL (½ cup) dry white wine
- 50 g unsalted butter, cold and cubed
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Zest and juice of ½ lemon
- 20 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Salt and white pepper

## Steps

1. Steep saffron in 2 tbsp warm water for 10 minutes. Meanwhile, bring stock to a gentle simmer in a small saucepan and keep warm. If using prawn shells, add them to the stock and simmer for 10 minutes, then strain.
2. Season prawns with salt and white pepper. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over high heat. Sear prawns for 1–1.5 minutes per side until just pink and lightly charred. Transfer to a plate and set aside.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the pan and cook onion and shallot for 5 minutes until soft and translucent. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
4. Add rice and toast, stirring, for 2 minutes. Pour in white wine and stir until absorbed, about 2 minutes. Add the saffron water and stir to combine.
5. Begin adding warm stock one ladleful at a time, stirring continuously, waiting for each addition to be almost absorbed before adding the next. Continue for 18–20 minutes until rice is al dente.
6. Remove from heat. Add cold butter, lemon zest, and lemon juice and stir vigorously to create a creamy, glossy risotto. Fold in prawns and warm for 1 minute.
7. Serve immediately in warmed bowls, scattered with parsley and a final drizzle of olive oil.

## Special Notice

> Searing the prawns separately and adding them at the end prevents them from overcooking — they only need brief heat to reheat in the final risotto. Infusing the stock with the prawn shells (if available) dramatically deepens the seafood flavor of the dish.
