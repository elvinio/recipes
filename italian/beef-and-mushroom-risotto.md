# Beef and Mushroom Risotto

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 320 g Arborio or Carnaroli rice
- 350 g beef sirloin or rump steak, cut into 1.5 cm strips
- 300 g mixed mushrooms (cremini, shiitake, or portobello), sliced
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 120 mL (½ cup) dry red wine
- 1.2 L (5 cups) beef stock, kept warm in a small saucepan
- 50 g unsalted butter, cold and cubed
- 60 g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 20 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large, wide skillet over very high heat. Season beef strips with salt and pepper. Sear in a single layer for 1.5–2 minutes until browned but still slightly pink inside. Transfer to a plate and rest.
2. In the same pan over high heat, add 1 tbsp oil and sauté mushrooms for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until golden and all liquid has evaporated. Season and set aside with the beef.
3. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil and cook onion for 5 minutes until translucent. Add garlic and thyme sprig and cook 1 minute more.
4. Add rice and toast, stirring constantly, for 2 minutes until the grains turn slightly translucent at the edges. Pour in red wine and stir until absorbed, about 2 minutes.
5. Begin adding warm stock one ladleful (about 120 mL) at a time, stirring continuously and waiting until each addition is almost fully absorbed before adding the next. Continue for 18–20 minutes until the rice is al dente with a creamy consistency.
6. Remove thyme sprig. Add beef and mushrooms back to the risotto and fold gently to warm through for 1 minute.
7. Remove from heat. Add cold butter and Parmigiano and stir vigorously until the risotto is creamy and glossy (this is the mantecatura). Taste, adjust seasoning, and serve immediately scattered with parsley.

## Special Notice

> Risotto waits for no one — have everyone seated before you finish the final stage. The mantecatura (vigorous off-heat stirring with cold butter) is what creates the signature wave-like, all'onda texture; never skip this step.
