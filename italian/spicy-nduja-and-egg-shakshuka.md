# Spicy Nduja and Egg Shakshuka

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 120 g nduja (soft, spreadable Calabrian sausage)
- 2 x 400 g cans whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, thinly sliced
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp sweet smoked paprika
- ½ tsp ground cumin
- ½ tsp dried chilli flakes (adjust to taste)
- 40 g feta cheese, crumbled
- 20 g fresh flat-leaf parsley or basil, roughly torn
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 4–8 thick slices ciabatta or sourdough, toasted, to serve

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large, wide skillet or sauté pan over medium heat. Add onion and bell pepper and cook for 6–7 minutes until softened and beginning to caramelise at the edges.
2. Add garlic, paprika, cumin, and chilli flakes and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add nduja and stir, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. It will melt into the oil and turn everything a vivid red-orange. Cook for 2–3 minutes.
4. Pour in the crushed tomatoes. Season lightly with salt (nduja is salty) and black pepper. Stir well and simmer over medium heat for 8 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil floats on top.
5. Taste and adjust seasoning and heat level. Create 8 shallow wells in the sauce using the back of a spoon. Crack an egg into each well.
6. Cover the pan with a lid and cook for 5–7 minutes until the egg whites are fully set but the yolks are still runny (cook longer for firmer yolks).
7. Scatter crumbled feta and fresh herbs over the top. Serve immediately, straight from the pan, with plenty of toasted bread for dunking.

## Special Notice

> Nduja varies significantly in heat level between producers — taste before adding the chilli flakes and adjust accordingly. If nduja is unavailable, substitute with 80 g of finely chopped spicy Italian sausage plus 1 extra tsp of chilli flakes and 1 tsp of smoked paprika.
