# Pork Milanese with Arugula

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 pork loin chops, boneless (about 160 g each)
- 80 g plain flour
- 2 large eggs, beaten
- 120 g fine dry breadcrumbs (pangrattato)
- 30 g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- 100 mL neutral oil (sunflower or grapeseed), for frying
- 80 g baby rocket (arugula)
- 100 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 30 g shaved Parmigiano Reggiano
- Juice of 1 lemon, plus wedges to serve
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Place pork chops between sheets of plastic wrap and pound with a meat mallet or rolling pin to an even 5 mm thickness. Pat dry and season both sides with salt and pepper.
2. Set up a breading station: flour in one shallow dish, beaten eggs in a second, and breadcrumbs mixed with the grated Parmigiano in a third.
3. Coat each pork chop in flour (shake off excess), dip in egg (let excess drip off), then press firmly into breadcrumb mixture, turning to coat both sides evenly. Set on a plate.
4. Heat neutral oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until shimmering (about 180°C). Test with a pinch of breadcrumbs — it should sizzle immediately.
5. Fry cutlets two at a time for 2.5–3 minutes per side until deep golden and cooked through. Adjust heat as needed to prevent burning. Drain on a wire rack or paper towels. Season immediately with a pinch of salt.
6. In a bowl, toss rocket, cherry tomatoes, shaved Parmigiano, lemon juice, and olive oil. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
7. Place each Milanese on a plate and top generously with the arugula salad. Serve with lemon wedges on the side.

## Special Notice

> The key to a crispy Milanese is oil temperature — too cool and the breading becomes greasy; too hot and it burns before the meat cooks through. Do not move the cutlets once they are in the pan; let them develop a full golden crust before flipping.
