# Eggplant Parmigiana

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 2 large eggplants (about 900 g total), sliced into 1 cm rounds
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 250 g fresh mozzarella, drained and sliced
- 60 g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- 15 g fresh basil leaves
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for frying
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 100 g plain flour, for dredging
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Lay eggplant slices on a wire rack, sprinkle generously with salt on both sides, and let sit for 15 minutes to draw out moisture. Pat thoroughly dry with paper towels.
2. Meanwhile, make the tomato sauce: heat 3 tbsp olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes and oregano, season with salt and pepper, and simmer for 10 minutes until slightly thickened.
3. Heat a thin layer of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Working in batches, dredge eggplant slices in flour, shaking off excess, then dip in beaten egg. Fry for 2–3 minutes per side until golden brown. Drain on paper towels.
4. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce in the base of a 22 x 30 cm baking dish. Arrange a layer of fried eggplant slices over the sauce.
5. Top with mozzarella slices, a few basil leaves, a spoonful of tomato sauce, and a dusting of Parmigiano. Repeat layers, finishing with sauce and a generous covering of Parmigiano.
6. Bake for 20–25 minutes until bubbling around the edges and the top is deep golden.
7. Rest for 5 minutes before slicing and serving, garnished with fresh basil leaves.

## Special Notice

> Salting and thoroughly drying the eggplant is essential — it removes bitterness and prevents the layers from becoming watery. This dish reheats exceptionally well the next day and can be assembled up to 24 hours ahead and refrigerated before baking.
