# Tofu and Spinach Gnocchi

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g shelf-stable potato gnocchi
- 400 g firm tofu, drained and pressed, cut into 2 cm cubes
- 200 g baby spinach
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 400 g canned whole peeled tomatoes, crushed by hand
- 60 mL (¼ cup) dry white wine
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- ½ tsp sweet smoked paprika
- 40 g Parmigiano Reggiano or vegetarian hard cheese, grated (optional)
- 15 g fresh basil, torn
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Press tofu firmly between paper towels for 10 minutes to remove excess moisture. Cut into 2 cm cubes and pat dry again.
2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat. Add tofu in a single layer and cook undisturbed for 3–4 minutes until golden on the underside. Flip and brown the other side for 2–3 minutes. Transfer to a plate and season with salt and paprika.
3. Add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the pan over medium heat. Cook onion for 5 minutes until soft. Add garlic and oregano and cook for 1 minute.
4. Pour in white wine and let it reduce for 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes, season well, and simmer for 10–12 minutes until the sauce is thickened and flavourful.
5. Add baby spinach to the sauce in batches, stirring until fully wilted, about 2 minutes total.
6. Meanwhile, cook gnocchi in a large pot of well-salted boiling water until they float to the surface, about 2–3 minutes. Drain and add directly to the tomato sauce.
7. Return tofu to the pan, fold gently to combine, and cook for 1–2 minutes. Serve immediately topped with torn basil and Parmigiano if using.

## Special Notice

> Thoroughly pressing and patting the tofu dry before frying is what makes the difference between golden, firm cubes and soft, steamed ones. Gnocchi cook very quickly once they float — drain them immediately to avoid a gluey texture.
