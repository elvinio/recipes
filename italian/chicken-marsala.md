# Chicken Marsala

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 180 g each)
- 300 g cremini or button mushrooms, thickly sliced
- 240 mL (1 cup) dry Marsala wine
- 180 mL (¾ cup) chicken stock
- 60 g unsalted butter
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 60 g plain flour, for dredging
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 small shallot, finely minced
- 2 tbsp double cream (optional)
- 20 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Place chicken breasts between two sheets of plastic wrap and pound to an even 1 cm thickness with a rolling pin or meat mallet. Season generously with salt and pepper on both sides. Dredge in flour, shaking off any excess.
2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil and 20 g butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear chicken for 4–5 minutes per side until golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature 74°C). Transfer to a plate and tent with foil.
3. Add remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the pan. Add mushrooms and cook over high heat for 4–5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until well browned and any liquid has evaporated.
4. Add shallot and garlic and cook for 1 minute. Pour in Marsala wine and let it bubble vigorously for 2 minutes, scraping up browned bits from the base.
5. Add chicken stock and continue simmering for 5–6 minutes until the sauce reduces and coats the back of a spoon. Stir in cream if using.
6. Remove from heat and swirl in the remaining 40 g butter, a few pieces at a time, until the sauce is glossy and rich. Taste and adjust seasoning.
7. Return chicken and any juices to the pan. Spoon sauce over the top and heat for 1 minute. Garnish with parsley and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Dry Marsala wine is essential for this recipe — sweet Marsala will make the sauce cloying. Pounding the chicken to an even thickness is key to ensuring it cooks evenly without drying out. Serve with mashed potato or soft polenta to catch all the sauce.
