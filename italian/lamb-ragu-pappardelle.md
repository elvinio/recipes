# Lamb Ragu Pappardelle

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g dried pappardelle
- 500 g ground lamb
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 120 mL (½ cup) dry red wine
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp ground cumin
- 1 tsp dried rosemary, finely crumbled
- ½ tsp ground cinnamon
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 40 g Pecorino Romano, finely grated, to serve
- Fresh mint or flat-leaf parsley, to garnish

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large, wide saucepan over medium-high heat. Add ground lamb, breaking it up with a spoon. Cook for 7–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until deeply browned and any liquid has evaporated. Season with salt and pepper.
2. Add onion and carrot to the pan. Reduce heat to medium and cook for 7 minutes until vegetables are soft and lightly golden.
3. Add garlic, cumin, rosemary, and cinnamon. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
4. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute. Pour in red wine and scrape up any caramelised bits. Simmer for 3 minutes until the wine mostly evaporates.
5. Add crushed tomatoes and bay leaf. Season again and bring to a gentle boil, then reduce to a low simmer. Partially cover and cook for 25–30 minutes, stirring every 5–10 minutes, until the ragu is thick and richly flavored.
6. Cook pappardelle in well-salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 180 mL pasta water before draining. Toss pasta with the ragu over medium heat, adding pasta water to reach a silky consistency.
7. Remove bay leaf and divide among bowls. Top with Pecorino and fresh herbs.

## Special Notice

> The cumin and cinnamon are subtle but lift the lamb beautifully — do not skip them. Ground lamb can release a lot of fat during browning; drain off excess fat after step 1 if needed, leaving about 2 tbsp in the pan for cooking the vegetables.
