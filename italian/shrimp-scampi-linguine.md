# Shrimp Scampi Linguine

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g linguine
- 600 g large raw shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 60 g unsalted butter
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 180 mL (¾ cup) dry white wine
- Juice of 1 lemon
- ½ tsp chilli flakes (optional)
- 30 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Cook linguine in a large pot of generously salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 240 mL (1 cup) pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, pat shrimp dry with paper towels and season lightly with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil and half the butter in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add shrimp in a single layer and cook undisturbed for 1.5 minutes until pink and curled on the underside. Flip and cook 1 minute more. Transfer shrimp to a plate.
4. Reduce heat to medium. Add garlic and chilli flakes to the same skillet and cook for 1 minute until fragrant and just golden — do not burn.
5. Pour in white wine and lemon juice, scraping up any bits. Simmer for 2–3 minutes until reduced by half.
6. Add drained linguine to the skillet along with the remaining butter and 120 mL pasta water. Toss vigorously over medium heat for 1–2 minutes until the sauce emulsifies and coats the pasta.
7. Return shrimp and any collected juices to the pan. Toss to combine, add parsley, and adjust seasoning. Serve immediately with lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Avoid overcooking the shrimp — they continue to cook slightly when returned to the hot pan. Use the freshest shrimp available and taste the pasta water before adding it to the sauce, as it can vary in saltiness.
