# Osso Buco with Gremolata

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 cross-cut pork shanks, about 300 g each, tied with kitchen twine
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 240 mL (1 cup) dry white wine
- 240 mL (1 cup) chicken stock
- 400 g canned diced tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tbsp plain flour, for dredging
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 2 bay leaves
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **Gremolata:** zest of 2 lemons, 1 garlic clove (very finely minced), 30 g flat-leaf parsley (finely chopped)

## Steps

1. Pat shanks dry and season generously with salt and pepper. Dredge lightly in flour, shaking off excess.
2. Set the electric pressure cooker to "Sauté" on high. Heat olive oil and sear shanks for 3–4 minutes per side until deeply golden. Work in batches to avoid crowding. Remove and set aside.
3. Add onion, carrot, and celery to the pot and sauté for 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic, tomato paste, and thyme; cook for 1 minute.
4. Pour in white wine and scrape up all browned bits from the base. Simmer for 2 minutes. Add diced tomatoes, chicken stock, and bay leaves. Stir to combine.
5. Return shanks to the pot, standing them upright if possible. Lock the lid, set to high pressure, and cook for 28 minutes. Allow a 10-minute natural pressure release, then release remaining pressure manually.
6. While the meat rests, prepare the gremolata: combine lemon zest, minced garlic, and parsley in a small bowl and mix well.
7. Remove twine from shanks. Taste the braising liquid, adjust seasoning, and serve shanks with the sauce spooned over. Top generously with gremolata.

## Special Notice

> Tying the shanks with kitchen twine keeps the meat from falling apart during pressure cooking — remove the twine carefully before serving to present them intact. The gremolata is added at the table, not during cooking, to preserve its bright, fresh flavour.
