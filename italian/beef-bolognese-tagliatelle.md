# Beef Bolognese Tagliatelle

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g dried tagliatelle (or fresh, if available)
- 500 g ground beef (15–20% fat)
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 medium carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 150 mL whole milk
- 120 mL dry white wine
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Parmigiano Reggiano, for serving

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large, heavy-based pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery (the soffritto) and cook, stirring often, for 10 minutes until very soft and lightly golden.
2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute. Increase heat to medium-high and add ground beef, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook for 6–8 minutes until no pink remains and the meat is lightly browned.
3. Pour in the milk and simmer, stirring, for 3–4 minutes until it is fully absorbed into the meat mixture.
4. Add white wine and cook for 3 minutes until the alcohol smell dissipates and the wine reduces.
5. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute. Add crushed tomatoes, thyme, and bay leaf. Season well with salt and pepper.
6. Reduce heat to low, partially cover, and simmer for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should be thick, rich, and cling to the back of a spoon. Remove bay leaf.
7. Meanwhile, cook tagliatelle in a large pot of well-salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 120 mL pasta water before draining. Toss pasta with the ragu, adding pasta water as needed to loosen. Serve with Parmigiano.

## Special Notice

> For a richer sauce with deeper flavor, use a mix of ground beef and ground pork. The ragu freezes beautifully for up to 3 months — make a double batch and store in 400 g portions for a quick weeknight dinner.
