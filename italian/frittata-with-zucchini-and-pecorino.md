# Frittata with Zucchini and Pecorino

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | lunch

## Ingredients

- 8 large eggs
- 3 medium zucchini (about 500 g), cut into 5 mm rounds
- 60 g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- 1 small onion, thinly sliced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 30 g fresh basil leaves, roughly torn
- ½ tsp dried chilli flakes (optional)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a 26–28 cm oven-safe skillet over medium-high heat. Add zucchini rounds in a single layer (work in batches if needed) and cook for 3–4 minutes per side until golden and tender. Transfer to a plate and season with salt.
2. Reduce heat to medium. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil and cook the onion for 4 minutes until soft. Add garlic and chilli flakes (if using) and cook for 1 minute.
3. Return the zucchini to the pan, arranging it evenly. Reduce heat to medium-low.
4. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, half the Pecorino, a pinch of salt, and black pepper until well combined.
5. Pour egg mixture over the zucchini in the skillet. Scatter the remaining Pecorino over the top. Cook undisturbed for 4–5 minutes until the edges are set and the base is golden — the centre will still be slightly wobbly.
6. Cover the skillet with a tight-fitting lid and continue cooking on low heat for 4–5 minutes until the top is just set and no longer wet.
7. Slide onto a serving board or plate. Scatter torn basil over the top and serve warm or at room temperature, cut into wedges.

## Special Notice

> A frittata should never be rubbery — pull it off the heat while the very centre still has a very slight jiggle, as residual heat will finish the cooking. Frittata is excellent at room temperature, making it ideal for packed lunches or a light dinner served with a green salad.
