# Roasted Chicken Thighs with Olives and Tomatoes

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (about 1.2 kg total)
- 400 g cherry tomatoes or baby plum tomatoes
- 120 g Kalamata or Castelvetrano olives, pitted
- 6 garlic cloves, skin on, lightly crushed
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 120 mL (½ cup) dry white wine
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- ½ tsp sweet smoked paprika
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Fresh basil leaves, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 220°C. Pat chicken thighs thoroughly dry with paper towels. Score the skin two or three times with a knife to help it crisp. Season generously all over with salt, pepper, oregano, thyme, and paprika.
2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large ovenproof skillet or roasting pan over medium-high heat on the stovetop. Place chicken thighs skin-side down and sear for 5–6 minutes until the skin is deep golden and releases easily from the pan. Flip and sear for 2 minutes on the other side.
3. Remove chicken to a plate. Discard all but 1 tbsp of fat from the pan.
4. Add crushed garlic cloves and tomatoes to the pan. Pour over white wine and remaining 2 tbsp olive oil. Scatter in olives and lemon zest. Stir briefly.
5. Nestle chicken thighs skin-side up into the tomato and olive mixture, ensuring the skin sits above the liquid to stay crisp.
6. Transfer to the oven and roast for 22–25 minutes until chicken skin is crisp and the meat registers 80°C internally. Tomatoes should be collapsed and juicy.
7. Rest for 3 minutes, then scatter with fresh basil and serve directly from the pan with crusty bread or roasted potatoes.

## Special Notice

> Drying the chicken skin completely before seasoning and searing is the single most important step for crispy skin. If the pan drippings look too reduced after roasting, deglaze with 2 tbsp water on the stovetop to loosen all the caramelised bits into a simple sauce.
