# Pork Sausage Ribollita

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g Italian pork sausages, casings removed
- 400 g canned cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 200 g Tuscan kale (cavolo nero), stems removed, leaves roughly chopped
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 400 g canned diced tomatoes
- 1 L (4 cups) chicken stock
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 1 bay leaf
- 4 thick slices stale sourdough or ciabatta bread (about 200 g)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Parmigiano Reggiano rind (optional, added to broth)

## Steps

1. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large, heavy pot over medium heat. Add sausage meat, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook for 6–8 minutes until well browned. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside.
2. Add remaining 1 tbsp oil to the pot. Add onion, carrot, and celery and cook over medium heat for 8 minutes until softened and lightly golden.
3. Add garlic and rosemary and cook for 1 minute. Stir in tomatoes and cook for 3 minutes, scraping up any browned bits.
4. Return browned sausage to the pot. Pour in chicken stock. Add bay leaf and Parmigiano rind if using. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.
5. Add cannellini beans and kale. Simmer for 20 minutes until kale is very tender.
6. Roughly tear or cube the stale bread and stir it into the soup. Simmer for a further 5–8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the bread breaks down and the soup becomes thick and hearty.
7. Remove bay leaf and cheese rind. Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve in deep bowls with a generous drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil and freshly cracked pepper.

## Special Notice

> Ribollita means "reboiled" — this soup is traditionally made a day ahead and reheated, which makes it even thicker and more flavorful. If the soup becomes too thick on reheating, loosen it with a splash of water or stock.
