# Baked Salmon Acqua Pazza

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, skin-on (about 180 g each)
- 400 g cherry tomatoes, halved
- 1 small fennel bulb, thinly sliced, fronds reserved
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 180 mL (¾ cup) dry white wine
- 120 mL (½ cup) water
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp chilli flakes
- 12 green or black olives, pitted
- 20 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Crusty bread, to serve

## Steps

1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper on both sides.
2. Heat 2 tbsp olive oil in a large ovenproof skillet or baking dish over medium heat on the stovetop. Add fennel slices and cook for 4 minutes until slightly softened. Add garlic and chilli flakes and cook for 1 minute.
3. Add cherry tomatoes, olives, white wine, and water to the pan. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 3 minutes, gently crushing a few tomatoes to release their juices.
4. Nestle the salmon fillets skin-side down into the tomato and fennel broth. Drizzle the remaining 2 tbsp olive oil over the salmon. Scatter lemon zest over the top.
5. Transfer the pan to the oven and bake for 14–16 minutes until salmon is just cooked through — it should flake easily when pressed gently but still have a slightly translucent centre.
6. Remove from oven and rest for 2 minutes. Scatter with parsley and reserved fennel fronds.
7. Serve directly from the pan with crusty bread to mop up the fragrant broth.

## Special Notice

> "Acqua pazza" means "crazy water" — the broth should be light and aromatic, not thick. Do not overbake the salmon; a slightly translucent centre means it will be moist and silky. The fennel fronds add a lovely fresh anise note and should not be omitted.
