# Spaghetti Carbonara

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g spaghetti
- 200 g guanciale or pancetta, cut into 1 cm cubes
- 4 large eggs
- 2 egg yolks
- 80 g Pecorino Romano, finely grated
- 40 g Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 tsp freshly cracked black pepper
- Salt, for pasta water

## Steps

1. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti until al dente according to package directions, reserving 240 mL (1 cup) pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, place guanciale in a large cold skillet with olive oil. Set over medium heat and render for 8–10 minutes until crispy and golden. Remove from heat.
3. In a bowl, whisk together the 4 eggs, 2 yolks, Pecorino, and Parmigiano until smooth. Season generously with cracked black pepper.
4. Drain pasta and immediately add to the skillet with the guanciale, off the heat. Toss well to coat in the rendered fat.
5. Add the egg and cheese mixture to the skillet, tossing rapidly while gradually splashing in pasta water, 30 mL at a time, until a glossy, creamy sauce coats every strand.
6. Continue tossing for 1–2 minutes until the sauce is silky and clings to the pasta without scrambling. Adjust consistency with more pasta water as needed.
7. Serve immediately in warmed bowls, topped with extra Pecorino and a final crack of black pepper.

## Special Notice

> Carbonara must be made off direct heat once the eggs are added — residual heat from the pasta and pan is sufficient, and direct heat will scramble the eggs. Leftovers do not reheat well; this dish is best eaten fresh.
