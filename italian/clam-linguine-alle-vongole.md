# Clam Linguine alle Vongole

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g linguine
- 1 kg fresh clams (vongole), scrubbed and rinsed
- 6 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 180 mL (¾ cup) dry white wine
- 4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- ½ tsp chilli flakes
- 30 g flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Soak the clams in cold salted water (30 g salt per litre) for 15 minutes to purge any sand. Drain and discard any clams with broken or open shells that do not close when tapped.
2. Bring a large pot of generously salted water to a boil. Cook linguine until 2 minutes short of al dente. Reserve 300 mL (1¼ cups) pasta water before draining.
3. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a large, wide skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add garlic and chilli flakes and cook for 1 minute until garlic is lightly golden.
4. Add the clams and pour over the white wine. Cover immediately with a lid and steam for 3–5 minutes, shaking the pan once or twice, until all clams have opened. Discard any that remain shut.
5. Lift out clams with a slotted spoon and set aside. Add the under-cooked linguine directly to the clam broth in the pan along with 180 mL of the reserved pasta water.
6. Cook, tossing constantly, for 2 minutes until pasta is perfectly al dente and the sauce has reduced to coat it. Add more pasta water if needed.
7. Return clams to the pan, add lemon zest and parsley, toss once more, and serve immediately with lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Soaking the clams is not optional — it removes grit that would ruin the dish. Do not add salt to the clam broth or sauce until the end, as the clams themselves release a generous amount of brine into the pan as they open.
