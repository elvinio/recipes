# Tuna and Caper Puttanesca

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 400 g spaghetti or rigatoni
- 2 x 160 g cans tuna in olive oil, drained (oil reserved)
- 400 g canned crushed tomatoes
- 80 g pitted Kalamata olives, roughly chopped
- 3 tbsp capers, rinsed
- 6 anchovy fillets in oil, finely chopped
- 5 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp chilli flakes
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 15 g flat-leaf parsley, chopped
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Cook pasta in a large pot of well-salted boiling water until al dente. Reserve 180 mL (¾ cup) pasta water before draining.
2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil and 1 tbsp of the reserved tuna oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add anchovies and cook, stirring, for 1–2 minutes until they dissolve into the oil.
3. Add garlic and chilli flakes and cook for 1 minute until fragrant but not browned.
4. Add crushed tomatoes, olives, and capers. Stir well and simmer for 8–10 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly.
5. Gently fold in the drained tuna, breaking it into medium flakes — do not over-stir, as you want some texture.
6. Add drained pasta to the skillet with a splash of pasta water. Toss over medium heat for 1–2 minutes until the sauce clings to the pasta. Add more pasta water if needed to loosen.
7. Remove from heat, stir in parsley, and taste for seasoning — the anchovies and capers are salty, so additional salt may not be needed. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> This dish is intentionally bold and briny — rinse the capers thoroughly and taste before adding any extra salt. Tuna packed in olive oil produces a far richer result than tuna packed in water; the reserved oil is flavourful and worth using.
