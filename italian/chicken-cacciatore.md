# Chicken Cacciatore

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 1.2 kg bone-in, skin-on chicken pieces (thighs and drumsticks)
- 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
- 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
- 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
- 200 g button mushrooms, thickly sliced
- 400 g canned whole San Marzano tomatoes, crushed by hand
- 120 mL (½ cup) dry white wine
- 2 tbsp tomato paste
- 1 tsp dried oregano
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 2 tbsp capers, rinsed
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper

## Steps

1. Pat chicken pieces dry with paper towels. Season generously on all sides with salt and pepper.
2. Heat olive oil in a large, deep skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Sear chicken skin-side down for 5–6 minutes until deep golden brown. Flip and sear the other side for 3 minutes. Transfer to a plate.
3. Reduce heat to medium. In the same pan, sauté onion and bell peppers for 5 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Add mushrooms and cook for 3 minutes until they begin to release their liquid. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 1 minute.
5. Pour in the white wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Simmer for 2 minutes until the wine reduces by half.
6. Add crushed tomatoes, oregano, rosemary sprig, and capers. Stir to combine. Nestle the seared chicken pieces back into the sauce, skin-side up.
7. Cover and simmer over medium-low heat for 30–35 minutes until chicken is cooked through and registers 75°C internally. Remove rosemary sprig, taste, and adjust seasoning before serving.

## Special Notice

> This dish tastes even better the next day as the flavors meld overnight — store covered in the refrigerator for up to 3 days and reheat gently on the stovetop. Serve with crusty bread, polenta, or steamed rice to soak up the rich sauce.
