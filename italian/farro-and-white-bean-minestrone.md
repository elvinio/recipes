# Farro and White Bean Minestrone

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Italian | dinner

## Ingredients

- 180 g pearled farro, rinsed
- 400 g canned cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 2 carrots, finely diced
- 2 celery stalks, finely diced
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 medium zucchini, cut into 1 cm dice
- 400 g canned diced tomatoes
- 1.2 L (5 cups) vegetable stock
- 3 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
- 1 tsp dried thyme
- 1 tsp dried rosemary
- 100 g kale or savoy cabbage, stems removed, leaves chopped
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 4 thick slices sourdough or ciabatta, toasted and rubbed with garlic, to serve
- Parmigiano Reggiano rind, to add to broth (optional)

## Steps

1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery and cook, stirring occasionally, for 8 minutes until soft and sweet.
2. Add garlic, thyme, and rosemary and cook for 1 minute. Stir in diced tomatoes and cook for 3 minutes, breaking up the tomatoes slightly.
3. Add farro and stir to coat in the vegetables. Pour in the stock. Add Parmigiano rind if using. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer.
4. Simmer uncovered for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
5. Add zucchini and cannellini beans. Continue simmering for a further 8–10 minutes until farro is tender but still has a pleasant chew.
6. Stir in the kale or cabbage and cook for 3 minutes until wilted and tender. Remove Parmigiano rind. Taste and adjust seasoning generously with salt and pepper.
7. Serve in deep bowls with a generous drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil and garlic-rubbed toast on the side.

## Special Notice

> Pearled farro cooks in 20–25 minutes and does not require pre-soaking. The soup thickens considerably as it cools; add a splash of water or stock when reheating. This minestrone keeps well refrigerated for 4 days and is one of those soups that genuinely improves overnight.
