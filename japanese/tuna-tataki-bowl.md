# Tuna Tataki Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g sashimi-grade tuna loin, in one block
- 1 tsp neutral oil
- 2 tsp sesame oil
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain sushi rice, cooked with 2 tbsp rice vinegar, 1 tbsp sugar and 1 tsp salt folded in while warm
- 60 mL soy sauce
- 2 tbsp yuzu juice or lemon juice
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp freshly grated ginger
- 1 small avocado, thinly sliced
- 50 g mixed salad leaves or shredded cucumber
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 4 spring onions, thinly sliced
- Pickled ginger and wasabi, to serve

## Steps

1. Cook and season sushi rice: while hot, fold in rice vinegar mixture and fan to cool to room temperature.
2. Make ponzu dressing: whisk together soy sauce, yuzu juice, mirin and grated ginger. Set aside.
3. Pat tuna loin completely dry. Rub all surfaces with neutral oil and season lightly with salt.
4. Heat a cast iron pan over the highest heat possible for 2–3 minutes until smoking. Sear the tuna block on each of its four long sides for exactly 30 seconds per side — you want a 3–4 mm crust of cooked flesh surrounding a completely raw centre.
5. Transfer tuna immediately to a board and rest for 2 minutes.
6. Slice tuna into 5 mm rounds. If the centre is not raw enough for your preference, a 60-second total sear is sufficient.
7. Divide sushi rice among four bowls. Arrange tuna slices overlapping across the top, fan avocado slices alongside, and add a small mound of salad leaves or cucumber.
8. Drizzle ponzu dressing over the bowl, scatter sesame seeds and spring onions, and serve with pickled ginger and wasabi on the side. Finish with a few drops of sesame oil.

## Special Notice

> Use only sashimi-grade (sushi-grade) tuna purchased from a trusted fishmonger — this dish involves a raw centre. The pan must be genuinely smoking hot before the tuna touches it; a lukewarm pan causes steaming instead of searing and turns the outer layer grey. Assemble bowls just before serving to keep the rice at ideal temperature and the avocado from browning.
