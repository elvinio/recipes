# Chicken Katsu Curry

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 chicken breast fillets (about 180 g each)
- 60 g plain flour
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 120 g panko breadcrumbs
- Neutral oil for frying (about 500 mL)
- 2 medium onions, finely diced
- 2 medium carrots, diced into 1 cm cubes
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tbsp neutral oil (for curry)
- 2 tbsp Japanese curry powder (or S&B curry powder)
- 1 tbsp plain flour (for roux)
- 700 mL chicken stock
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp honey
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Make curry sauce: heat 1 tbsp oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add onion and cook, stirring, for 8–10 minutes until deeply golden and caramelised. Add garlic and carrot, cook for 2 minutes. Stir in curry powder and 1 tbsp flour; cook for 1 minute until fragrant. Gradually pour in chicken stock, whisking constantly to avoid lumps. Add soy sauce and honey. Simmer over low heat for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until sauce is thick and coats the back of a spoon.
3. While curry simmers, prepare chicken: place each fillet between two sheets of cling film and pound to an even 1.5 cm thickness. Season well with salt and pepper.
4. Set up breading station: flour, beaten egg, and panko in separate shallow dishes. Coat each fillet in flour, egg, then press firmly into panko.
5. Heat frying oil to 170°C in a deep frying pan. Fry two fillets at a time for 4–5 minutes per side until deep golden and cooked through (internal temperature 74°C). Drain on a wire rack.
6. Rest katsu for 2 minutes, then slice diagonally into 2 cm strips.
7. Divide rice onto one side of four plates. Ladle curry sauce generously alongside.
8. Arrange sliced katsu over the rice and curry and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Japanese curry is sweeter and milder than Indian-style curries — the caramelised onion is the foundation of its flavour. For a shortcut, use half a block of store-bought Japanese curry roux (such as S&B Golden Curry) in place of the curry powder-and-flour roux. The curry sauce keeps refrigerated for 4 days and freezes well for up to 3 months — batch it ahead of time.
