# Shrimp Tempura Don

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 16 large raw shrimp (about 600 g), peeled and deveined, tails on
- 120 g cake flour or plain flour, plus extra for dusting
- 1 egg yolk
- 180 mL ice-cold sparkling water
- Neutral oil for frying (about 1 L)
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked
- 120 mL dashi stock (or 120 mL water + 1 tsp dashi powder)
- 3 tbsp mirin
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp caster sugar
- 4 sheets nori, halved (optional garnish)
- 1 tsp grated daikon radish per bowl, to serve

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions and keep warm.
2. Make tentsuyu sauce: combine dashi, mirin, soy sauce and sugar in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over medium heat, stir until sugar dissolves, then remove from heat.
3. Straighten shrimp: make 4–5 shallow cuts across the belly of each shrimp and gently press flat to prevent curling. Pat dry thoroughly with paper towels.
4. Heat oil in a deep pot to 180°C. Keep a thermometer clipped to the pot.
5. Make tempura batter: sift flour into a cold bowl. Add egg yolk and sparkling water all at once. Mix with chopsticks using just 10–12 strokes — lumps are fine and essential for a light batter. Set the bowl over a tray of ice water.
6. Dust shrimp lightly in plain flour, shaking off excess. Holding each shrimp by the tail, dip into batter and lower gently into the oil. Fry 4 shrimp at a time for 2–3 minutes, turning once, until batter is pale gold and crisp.
7. Drain on a wire rack. Maintain oil temperature between batches.
8. Divide rice among four bowls, ladle 3–4 tbsp tentsuyu sauce over the rice, and top with 4 shrimp tempura per bowl. Add grated daikon on the side and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> The secret to feather-light tempura is keeping everything cold — use ice water and chill the bowl before mixing. Never over-stir the batter. Tempura does not keep well; serve within 5 minutes of frying. If you need to hold it briefly, rest on a rack in a 120°C oven — never cover with foil or it will steam and go limp.
