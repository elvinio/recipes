# Clam Miso Udon

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g fresh or frozen clams (littleneck or manila), scrubbed clean
- 4 portions fresh or dried udon noodles (about 800 g fresh or 320 g dried)
- 1.2 L water
- 100 mL sake
- 4 tbsp white (shiro) miso paste
- 1 tbsp red (aka) miso paste
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp dashi powder
- 2 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, thinly sliced into matchsticks
- 100 g baby spinach or mitsuba (Japanese parsley)
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- Shichimi togarashi, to serve

## Steps

1. If using clams in shells, soak them in cold salted water (1 tbsp salt per litre) for 10 minutes to purge sand. Drain and rinse well.
2. Combine water, sake, garlic and ginger in a large pot. Bring to a boil over high heat.
3. Add clams to the boiling liquid, cover the pot and steam over high heat for 4–5 minutes, shaking the pot once, until all shells are opened. Discard any that remain closed.
4. Reduce heat to a low simmer. Dissolve dashi powder in the broth. Place both miso pastes in a ladle, lower into the broth and whisk to dissolve before releasing. Add soy sauce. Taste and adjust seasoning.
5. Cook udon noodles in a separate pot of boiling unsalted water according to package directions (fresh: 2–3 min; dried: 8–10 min). Drain well.
6. Divide noodles among four deep bowls.
7. Add spinach or mitsuba to the clam broth off the heat and stir until just wilted, about 30 seconds.
8. Ladle the broth, clams and greens over the noodles. Drizzle with sesame oil, scatter spring onions, and serve with shichimi on the side.

## Special Notice

> The clam steaming liquid is packed with natural umami — do not discard it. Miso must never be added to a full boil; reduce to a gentle simmer first to preserve the flavour. Clam miso udon is best consumed immediately, as the noodles absorb the broth rapidly. For a richer bowl, add 30 g of unsalted butter swirled in just before serving.
