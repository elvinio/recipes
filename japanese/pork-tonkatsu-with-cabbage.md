# Pork Tonkatsu with Cabbage

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 pork loin chops (about 180 g each), 1.5 cm thick
- 60 g plain flour
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 120 g panko breadcrumbs
- Neutral oil for frying (about 600 mL)
- 400 g white cabbage, very finely shredded
- 4 tbsp tonkatsu sauce (store-bought or mix 3 tbsp Worcestershire sauce + 2 tbsp ketchup)
- 2 tsp Japanese mustard (karashi), optional
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked
- Lemon wedges to serve

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Using a meat mallet or rolling pin, pound each pork chop to an even 1 cm thickness. Score the fat and connective tissue along the edge at 1 cm intervals to prevent curling. Season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Set up a three-stage breading station: flour in one shallow dish, beaten egg in a second, and panko in a third. Coat each chop in flour (shake off excess), dip in egg, then press firmly into panko on both sides for a thick, even crust.
4. Pour oil into a deep frying pan to a depth of 3 cm. Heat over medium heat to 170°C.
5. Fry two chops at a time for 4 minutes per side until deep golden brown. The internal temperature should reach 68°C. Drain on a wire rack; never stack.
6. Rest the tonkatsu for 3 minutes on the rack before cutting into 2 cm strips — this keeps the crust from steaming itself soggy.
7. Mound shredded cabbage on each plate beside a serving of steamed rice. Arrange sliced tonkatsu against the cabbage.
8. Drizzle tonkatsu sauce over the pork and serve with lemon wedges and mustard if using.

## Special Notice

> Shredding the cabbage as finely as possible — ideally on a mandoline — gives the classic restaurant texture. The cutlet can be breaded up to 4 hours ahead and kept uncovered on a rack in the refrigerator; this helps the crust adhere and stay crispy during frying. Store leftovers in a single layer in an airtight container for up to 2 days; reheat uncovered in a 200°C oven for 10 minutes.
