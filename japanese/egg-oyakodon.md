# Egg Oyakodon

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 2 cm pieces
- 6 large eggs
- 1 medium onion, halved and thinly sliced
- 240 mL dashi stock (or 240 mL water + 1 tsp dashi powder)
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 3 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked and kept warm
- 3 spring onions, cut into 3 cm pieces
- Shichimi togarashi (seven-spice powder), to serve (optional)

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions and keep warm.
2. In a wide sauté pan or donabe, combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin and sugar over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves and bring to a gentle simmer.
3. Add sliced onion and cook for 4–5 minutes until softened and translucent.
4. Add chicken pieces in a single layer. Simmer for 5–6 minutes, turning once, until just cooked through. Do not boil vigorously.
5. Beat eggs lightly — only 3–4 strokes, leaving streaks of white and yolk visible. This is what gives oyakodon its custardy, silky texture.
6. Scatter spring onion pieces over the chicken. Pour two-thirds of the egg mixture in a circular motion over the pan. Cover and cook on medium-low for 1 minute until the egg is mostly set but still trembling and glossy in the centre.
7. Pour the remaining egg around the edges. Remove from heat immediately and cover for 30 seconds — residual heat will finish the egg.
8. Slide the mixture over mounded rice in four bowls. Serve with a pinch of shichimi if desired.

## Special Notice

> Oyakodon means "parent and child bowl" — the name poetically refers to chicken and egg cooked together. The egg should be barely set and creamy, not fully cooked; err on the side of underdone as it continues to cook in the bowl. A traditional Japanese omelette pan (oyakodon pan) with a pour spout makes plating elegant, but any small non-stick frying pan works for individual portions.
