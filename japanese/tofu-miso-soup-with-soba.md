# Tofu Miso Soup with Soba

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Lunch

## Ingredients

- 180 g dried soba noodles
- 300 g silken or soft tofu, cut into 1.5 cm cubes
- 1.2 L dashi stock (or 1.2 L water + 2 tsp dashi powder)
- 4 tbsp white or mixed (awase) miso paste
- 100 g fresh shiitake mushrooms, stems removed, caps sliced
- 80 g wakame seaweed (dried), rehydrated in cold water for 5 min and drained
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp soy sauce (adjust to taste)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds

## Steps

1. Bring a large pot of unsalted water to a boil. Cook soba noodles according to package directions (typically 4–5 minutes). Drain, rinse immediately under cold running water to stop cooking and remove excess starch. Divide noodles among four deep bowls.
2. In a separate medium saucepan, bring dashi stock to a gentle simmer over medium heat. Add shiitake mushrooms and simmer for 5 minutes until softened.
3. Reduce heat to low. Place miso paste in a ladle and lower it into the stock; whisk it to dissolve using chopsticks before releasing into the broth. Never boil miso — it destroys the probiotic cultures and dulls the flavour.
4. Add silken tofu and rehydrated wakame to the broth. Warm gently for 2–3 minutes without stirring too vigorously to keep the tofu intact.
5. Stir in soy sauce and sesame oil. Taste and adjust seasoning.
6. Ladle the hot miso soup with tofu, mushrooms and wakame directly over the soba noodles in each bowl.
7. Top with sliced spring onions and sesame seeds.
8. Serve immediately while the noodles are still warm.

## Special Notice

> Miso soup should never reach a rolling boil after the paste is added — keep it just below simmering. Soba noodles continue to absorb liquid, so serve promptly or keep noodles and broth separate until serving. For a more substantial bowl, add a soft-boiled egg halved lengthways.
