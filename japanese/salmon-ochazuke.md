# Salmon Ochazuke

> Serves 4 | 10 minutes | Japanese | Lunch

## Ingredients

- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) cooked Japanese short-grain rice, hot or warm
- 200 g hot-smoked or grilled salmon, skin removed and flaked (store-bought is ideal)
- 600 mL freshly brewed green tea (sencha or genmaicha), hot
- 2 tbsp white miso paste
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 20 g wasabi peas or 1 tsp prepared wasabi
- 4 sheets nori, cut into thin strips
- 2 tbsp freeze-dried salmon flakes or furikake (optional)

## Steps

1. Brew green tea at 70–80°C (not boiling, to avoid bitterness) — steep for 2 minutes, then strain.
2. Whisk miso paste and soy sauce into the hot tea until fully dissolved. Taste; the broth should be lightly savoury and aromatic.
3. Divide hot cooked rice among four deep bowls, pressing it down gently to fill the base.
4. Arrange flaked salmon over the rice, concentrating it in the centre.
5. Pour the hot miso-green tea broth slowly around the edges of the bowl so as not to disturb the salmon on top — use about 150 mL per bowl.
6. Scatter spring onions, sesame seeds and nori strips over the top.
7. Add a small mound of wasabi on the side or dot with prepared wasabi directly on the salmon.
8. Serve immediately, with furikake or freeze-dried salmon flakes alongside for extra flavour.

## Special Notice

> Ochazuke is Japan's answer to leftover rice — it transforms cold or day-old rice into an elegant, soothing meal in minutes. Genmaicha (roasted rice tea) adds a subtle nuttiness that pairs beautifully with the salmon. The broth should be poured just before eating so the rice does not become waterlogged.
