# Chicken Karaage with Kewpie Slaw

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 5 cm pieces
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 3 cloves garlic, grated
- 120 g potato starch
- Neutral oil for frying (about 800 mL)
- 300 g white cabbage, finely shredded
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 3 tbsp Kewpie mayonnaise
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked
- Lemon wedges to serve

## Steps

1. Marinate chicken: combine soy sauce, sake, grated ginger and garlic in a bowl. Toss chicken pieces through the marinade and set aside for 20 minutes at room temperature.
2. Cook rice according to package directions while the chicken marinates.
3. Make Kewpie slaw: whisk together Kewpie mayonnaise, rice vinegar and sesame oil in a large bowl. Add shredded cabbage and spring onions, toss well and refrigerate until serving.
4. Pour oil into a deep heavy-based pot to a depth of 6 cm and heat over medium-high heat to 170°C.
5. Lift chicken pieces from the marinade, shake off excess liquid, and toss thoroughly in potato starch until evenly coated.
6. Fry chicken in batches of 6–8 pieces for 4–5 minutes, turning once, until pale golden and cooked through. Transfer to a wire rack.
7. Increase oil temperature to 185°C. Return all fried chicken to the oil and fry for a further 90 seconds until deeply golden and extra crispy. Drain on the rack.
8. Serve karaage immediately over steamed rice alongside the Kewpie slaw and lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Karaage is best eaten straight from the second fry — the double-fry technique creates the signature shatter-crisp crust. Leftover chicken can be reheated in a 200°C oven for 8 minutes to restore crispness. Potato starch is key; cornstarch will work in a pinch but produces a slightly less craggy coating.
