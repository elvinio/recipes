# Pork and Kimchi Fried Rice

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 800 g cold cooked Japanese short-grain rice (day-old rice is essential)
- 300 g pork belly slices or ground pork
- 200 g well-fermented kimchi, roughly chopped, plus 3 tbsp kimchi brine
- 1 medium onion, finely diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 4 eggs
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 1 sheet nori, crumbled (optional)

## Steps

1. If using pork belly slices, cut into 1.5 cm pieces. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
2. Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a large wok or wide frying pan over high heat until very hot and smoking. Add pork and cook undisturbed for 2 minutes until caramelised on the underside. Stir-fry for a further 2 minutes until cooked through. Push to the side.
3. Add onion and garlic to the pan. Stir-fry for 2 minutes until soft and fragrant.
4. Add chopped kimchi and gochujang. Stir-fry everything together for 2 minutes, until kimchi softens and turns darker in colour.
5. Add cold rice, breaking up any clumps with the back of a spatula. Toss and press the rice against the hot pan surface for 3–4 minutes until each grain is coated, separated and slightly crispy.
6. Pour in soy sauce and kimchi brine. Toss to combine. Finish with sesame oil. Taste and adjust with more gochujang or soy sauce.
7. In a separate pan with the remaining oil, fry eggs sunny-side-up over medium heat until whites are set but yolks remain runny.
8. Divide fried rice among four bowls. Top each with a fried egg, scatter spring onions, sesame seeds and crumbled nori. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Day-old, refrigerated rice is non-negotiable — freshly cooked rice has too much moisture and becomes clumpy and gluey when stir-fried. The kimchi brine is a flavour powerhouse; do not skip it. This dish is also excellent with leftover steamed rice from the previous night — make it a planned use of leftovers.
