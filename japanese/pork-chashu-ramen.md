# Pork Chashu Ramen

> Serves 4 | 55 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g pork belly, rolled and tied with kitchen twine
- 80 mL soy sauce
- 60 mL mirin
- 60 mL sake
- 2 tbsp caster sugar
- 400 mL water
- 1.2 L chicken or pork stock
- 2 tbsp white (shiro) miso paste
- 1 tbsp sesame paste (neri goma) or tahini
- 4 portions fresh or dried ramen noodles (about 400 g total)
- 4 eggs, soft-boiled (7 minutes in boiling water, peeled)
- 100 g bamboo shoots (menma), drained
- 4 sheets nori
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp neutral oil

## Steps

1. Heat neutral oil in a heavy-based pot over high heat. Sear pork belly roll on all sides until deeply browned, about 6–8 minutes total. Remove and set aside.
2. Add sake to the pot and deglaze, scraping up the browned bits. Add soy sauce, mirin, sugar and 400 mL water. Return pork belly to the pot; the liquid should come halfway up the meat.
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and braise for 35 minutes, turning the pork every 10 minutes, until a skewer inserted meets little resistance. Remove pork and set aside to rest; reserve braising liquid as tare.
4. While pork braises, bring stock to a gentle simmer in a separate saucepan. Whisk in miso paste and sesame paste until smooth. Keep at a very low simmer — do not boil.
5. Soft-boil eggs, peel and halve lengthways. Slice rested chashu into 1 cm rounds.
6. Cook ramen noodles according to package directions. Drain well.
7. Build bowls: ladle 300 mL hot miso broth into each deep bowl. Add 2 tbsp chashu braising tare. Divide noodles among bowls.
8. Top each bowl with 3 slices of chashu, a halved soft-boiled egg, bamboo shoots, a sheet of nori, and spring onions. Drizzle with sesame oil and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Chashu can be braised the night before and refrigerated in its tare — cold slicing produces cleaner rounds. The braising liquid (tare) is liquid gold; refrigerate it for up to a week and use it to season fried rice, noodles or marinades. For a deeper broth, use the electric pressure cooker and reduce the braise time to 20 minutes at high pressure.
