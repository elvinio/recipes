# Beef Sukiyaki

> Serves 4 | 40 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g beef ribeye or sirloin, paper-thin sliced (ask your butcher or freeze for 30 min and slice yourself)
- 200 g shirataki (konnyaku) noodles, rinsed and drained
- 150 g firm tofu, cut into 3 cm cubes
- 6 dried shiitake mushrooms, soaked in 500 mL warm water for 20 min (reserve soaking liquid)
- 1 medium leek, cut into 2 cm rounds
- 100 g enoki mushrooms, trimmed
- 80 mL soy sauce
- 60 mL mirin
- 60 mL sake
- 2 tbsp caster sugar
- 4 eggs, for dipping (optional)
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked

## Steps

1. Prepare warishita sauce: combine soy sauce, mirin, sake and caster sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Stir until sugar dissolves (about 2 minutes). Remove from heat and set aside.
2. Drain soaked shiitake, reserving soaking liquid. Squeeze out mushrooms and slice off tough stems.
3. Cook rice according to package directions.
4. Heat a wide cast iron or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Add oil, then arrange beef slices in a single layer. Sear for 30 seconds per side until just browned. Push to one side of the pan.
5. Add leek rounds to the empty side and cook for 2 minutes. Pour in half the warishita sauce and 100 mL of the reserved mushroom soaking liquid.
6. Nestle tofu, shiitake, enoki and shirataki noodles into the pan around the beef. Spoon remaining sauce over everything. Simmer gently for 8–10 minutes until leek is tender and noodles have absorbed the broth.
7. If using, crack eggs into individual small bowls and beat lightly; use as a dipping sauce for the beef and tofu.
8. Serve directly from the pan at the table with steamed rice, encouraging everyone to dip pieces in raw egg before eating.

## Special Notice

> Sukiyaki is a communal dish — bring the pan to the table and let guests help themselves. Top up with additional warishita sauce and mushroom soaking water as the liquid reduces. Leftover sukiyaki reheats beautifully the next day; the noodles and tofu absorb more flavour overnight.
