# Gyudon Beef Rice Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g beef chuck or sirloin, shaved very thin (gyudon-style; freeze for 20 min and slice with a sharp knife, or buy pre-sliced)
- 1 large onion, halved and sliced into 5 mm half-moons
- 240 mL dashi stock (or 240 mL water + 1 tsp dashi powder)
- 60 mL soy sauce
- 3 tbsp mirin
- 2 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp neutral oil
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked and kept warm
- 4 raw egg yolks or soft-boiled eggs, to top (optional)
- Pickled red ginger (beni shoga) and shichimi togarashi, to serve

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions and keep warm.
2. Combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin, sake and sugar in a small bowl; stir to dissolve the sugar.
3. Heat oil in a wide frying pan over medium heat. Add sliced onion and cook, stirring occasionally, for 7–8 minutes until softened and beginning to turn golden at the edges.
4. Pour the sauce over the onions and bring to a simmer.
5. Separate the beef slices and add them to the pan, spreading evenly. Simmer gently for 3–4 minutes, occasionally pressing the beef into the liquid, until just cooked through. Do not stir vigorously — you want the meat to stay in slices.
6. Taste the broth and adjust with a splash of soy sauce if needed. The sauce should be savoury-sweet and slightly syrupy.
7. Ladle rice into four large bowls, mounding it in the centre.
8. Spoon the beef and onion mixture generously over the rice and drizzle with the cooking liquid. Top each bowl with a raw egg yolk or halved soft-boiled egg if using. Serve with pickled ginger and shichimi on the side.

## Special Notice

> Gyudon is Japan's ultimate fast comfort food. Using a fatty cut like chuck or short-rib slices results in a richer, more flavourful bowl than lean cuts. The dish keeps well for 3 days refrigerated — reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of water. The raw egg yolk is traditional; omit if preferred or replace with a 6-minute soft-boiled egg.
