# Shrimp and Avocado Chirashi Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Lunch

## Ingredients

- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain sushi rice
- 3 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1½ tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp fine salt
- 400 g cooked and peeled tiger shrimp, chilled
- 2 ripe avocados, halved, pitted and thinly sliced
- 80 g cucumber, halved lengthways and thinly sliced
- 50 g ikura (salmon roe) or tobiko, optional
- 4 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp yuzu juice or lemon juice
- 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- 4 shiso leaves, thinly sliced
- Wasabi and pickled ginger, to serve
- 2 sheets nori, cut into thin strips

## Steps

1. Cook sushi rice according to package directions.
2. While rice is hot, warm rice vinegar, sugar and salt in a small saucepan over low heat, stirring until dissolved. Fold the vinegar mixture into the hot rice using a wooden paddle or spatula with gentle cutting motions. Fan the rice as you fold to cool it quickly to body temperature. Do not refrigerate — keep at room temperature until serving.
3. Make dressing: combine soy sauce, mirin and yuzu juice in a small bowl.
4. Fan or layer seasoned sushi rice into four wide, shallow bowls.
5. Arrange shrimp, avocado slices and cucumber over the rice, distributing evenly among the bowls. If using, spoon ikura or tobiko in a small mound in the centre.
6. Drizzle dressing lightly over the bowls.
7. Scatter shiso, sesame seeds and nori strips over the top.
8. Serve at room temperature with wasabi and pickled ginger on the side.

## Special Notice

> Chirashi means "scattered" in Japanese — the beauty of this dish lies in a colourful, artful arrangement, so take a moment to place toppings with care. Do not refrigerate assembled bowls; cold rice becomes hard and loses its vinegared aroma. If preparing components ahead, keep the seasoned rice at room temperature covered with a damp cloth for up to 2 hours before assembling.
