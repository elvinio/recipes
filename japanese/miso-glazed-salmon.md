# Miso Glazed Salmon

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 salmon fillets, skin-on (about 160 g each)
- 3 tbsp white (shiro) miso paste
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked
- 200 g baby spinach
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced

## Steps

1. Preheat oven grill (broiler) to high, about 230°C. Line a baking tray with foil and lightly oil it.
2. Cook rice according to package directions.
3. Whisk together miso paste, mirin, sake, caster sugar and soy sauce in a small bowl until smooth.
4. Pat salmon fillets dry with paper towels and place skin-side down on the prepared tray. Spoon glaze generously over the flesh of each fillet.
5. Grill salmon on the top oven rack for 8–10 minutes, until the glaze is caramelised and bubbling and the fish flakes easily at its thickest point. Watch closely as miso can burn quickly.
6. While salmon grills, heat sesame oil in a frying pan over high heat. Add spinach and toss for 1–2 minutes until just wilted. Season lightly with salt.
7. Divide steamed rice among four bowls. Top each with a salmon fillet and wilted spinach.
8. Garnish with toasted sesame seeds and spring onions and serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> White miso produces a sweeter, more delicate glaze; substitute with red miso for a deeper, saltier flavour. Leftover glazed salmon keeps refrigerated for up to 2 days and is excellent flaked cold over a salad. Avoid over-marinating — more than 30 minutes can cause the fish surface to cure and turn mushy under the grill.
