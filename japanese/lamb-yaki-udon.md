# Lamb Yaki Udon

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 500 g lamb shoulder or leg, thinly sliced against the grain (5 mm thick)
- 4 portions fresh or frozen udon noodles (about 800 g)
- 1 medium onion, halved and thinly sliced
- 200 g white cabbage, roughly sliced into 3 cm pieces
- 100 g shiitake mushrooms, stems discarded, caps thinly sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp mirin
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 2 tbsp neutral oil
- 1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated
- 2 spring onions, cut into 3 cm lengths
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- Shichimi togarashi and bonito flakes (katsuobushi), to serve

## Steps

1. Cook udon noodles according to package directions (fresh/frozen: 2–3 minutes in boiling water). Drain, rinse under cold water and toss with 1 tsp neutral oil to prevent sticking.
2. Mix soy sauce, mirin and oyster sauce in a small bowl to make the yaki sauce. Set aside.
3. Heat 1 tbsp neutral oil in a wok or large frying pan over high heat until very hot. Add lamb slices in a single layer; cook undisturbed for 90 seconds until browned. Toss and cook for another minute. Remove lamb to a plate.
4. Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil to the pan. Add onion, garlic and ginger; stir-fry over high heat for 2 minutes until fragrant and softened.
5. Add cabbage and shiitake mushrooms. Stir-fry for 3 minutes until cabbage is wilted but still has some crunch.
6. Add drained udon noodles. Toss everything together, pressing noodles against the hot pan for 1–2 minutes to develop a slight char.
7. Return lamb to the pan. Pour yaki sauce over everything and toss vigorously for 1–2 minutes until noodles are evenly coated and glossy.
8. Finish with sesame oil and spring onions. Plate and scatter sesame seeds, shichimi and bonito flakes on top. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Lamb is an unusual but deeply satisfying choice for yaki udon, its slight gaminess complemented by the umami-rich sauce. Slicing the lamb as thin as possible — ideally by partially freezing for 20 minutes first — ensures quick cooking and a tender result. Yaki udon should be eaten straight from the wok while the noodles are still chewy and the lamb still juicy.
