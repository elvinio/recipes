# Agedashi Tofu

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 600 g firm silken tofu (kinugoshi), or medium-firm tofu
- 60 g potato starch or cornstarch
- Neutral oil for frying (about 600 mL)
- 300 mL dashi stock (or 300 mL water + 1 tsp dashi powder)
- 3 tbsp mirin
- 3 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tsp caster sugar
- 50 g daikon radish, finely grated
- 1 tsp fresh ginger, finely grated
- 3 spring onions, thinly sliced
- 2 tsp katsuobushi (bonito flakes), optional
- 1 tsp sesame oil

## Steps

1. Drain tofu thoroughly. Wrap in several layers of paper towel, place on a plate and set a flat heavy object on top. Press for 15 minutes to remove as much moisture as possible — this is essential for safe frying and a crisp crust.
2. Make tentsuyu broth: combine dashi, mirin, soy sauce and sugar in a small saucepan over medium heat. Simmer for 3 minutes until sugar dissolves and broth is fragrant. Stir in sesame oil. Keep warm.
3. Cut pressed tofu into 6 equal rectangular pieces (about 6 cm x 4 cm x 4 cm).
4. Heat oil in a deep pot to 180°C.
5. Working one piece at a time, dust each tofu block in potato starch, shaking off excess, and gently lower into the hot oil. Fry 3 pieces at a time for 3–4 minutes, turning once, until the crust is light golden and set. Do not crowd the pot.
6. Drain fried tofu on a wire rack.
7. Divide warm broth among four shallow bowls (about 75 mL each). Carefully place 2–3 pieces of fried tofu into each bowl — the broth should come halfway up the sides.
8. Top each serving with a mound of grated daikon, a pinch of grated ginger, spring onions and bonito flakes if using. Serve immediately.

## Special Notice

> Agedashi tofu is a race against time — the batter softens quickly in the broth. Assemble and serve within 3 minutes of frying. The contrast between the crisp skin and the custardy interior is what makes this dish special; that texture is only achievable if the tofu is pressed dry and the oil is at the correct temperature. Use a thermometer.
