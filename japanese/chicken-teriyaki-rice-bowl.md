# Chicken Teriyaki Rice Bowl

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless chicken thighs, skin-on
- 60 mL soy sauce
- 60 mL mirin
- 2 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp neutral oil
- 320 g (2 cups / 480 mL) Japanese short-grain rice, cooked
- 1 head broccoli (about 300 g), cut into florets
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
- 2 spring onions, thinly sliced
- Pickled ginger (beni shoga) to serve

## Steps

1. Cook rice according to package directions.
2. Combine soy sauce, mirin, sake and sugar in a small bowl; stir until sugar dissolves. Set teriyaki sauce aside.
3. Pat chicken thighs dry. Score the skin several times with a sharp knife to prevent curling.
4. Heat oil in a large non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Place chicken skin-side down and cook undisturbed for 7–8 minutes until the skin is deep golden and releases easily. Flip and cook flesh-side down for 4 minutes.
5. Pour teriyaki sauce into the pan. It will bubble immediately. Cook, turning chicken every minute, for 3–4 minutes until sauce reduces to a thick, shiny glaze that coats the chicken.
6. Transfer chicken to a board and rest for 3 minutes. While resting, blanch broccoli florets in boiling salted water for 2 minutes, drain.
7. Slice chicken thighs into 1.5 cm strips.
8. Divide rice among bowls, arrange chicken slices over the top, drizzle with any remaining pan glaze and add broccoli. Scatter sesame seeds and spring onions, and add pickled ginger on the side.

## Special Notice

> Cooking the chicken skin-side down first without moving it is the key to a lacquered, non-sticky skin. The teriyaki sauce will continue to thicken off the heat, so pull the pan when the glaze looks slightly too loose. Leftover chicken keeps for 3 days refrigerated and is excellent served cold over rice with extra sauce.
