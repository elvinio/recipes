# Chicken Yakitori Skewers

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 700 g boneless chicken thighs, cut into 3 cm pieces
- 4 spring onions (negi), cut into 3 cm pieces
- 80 mL soy sauce
- 60 mL mirin
- 2 tbsp sake
- 1 tbsp caster sugar
- 1 tsp neutral oil, for the pan
- 16 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for 10 minutes
- Shichimi togarashi or ground sansho pepper, to finish
- Lemon wedges, to serve

## Steps

1. Make tare sauce: combine soy sauce, mirin, sake and sugar in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring until sugar dissolves. Reduce heat and simmer for 5–6 minutes until the sauce thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon. Remove from heat.
2. Thread chicken and spring onion pieces alternately onto pre-soaked bamboo skewers, about 3–4 pieces of chicken per skewer, with a spring onion section between each piece.
3. Lightly brush a cast iron griddle pan or heavy frying pan with neutral oil. Heat over high heat until very hot — a drop of water should evaporate immediately.
4. Place skewers in the pan and cook undisturbed for 3 minutes until nicely charred underneath.
5. Flip skewers, brush generously with tare sauce, and cook for another 3 minutes.
6. Brush again with tare, flip once more, and cook for a final 1–2 minutes until the glaze is glossy and the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature 74°C).
7. Remove from heat and brush one final time with tare while hot.
8. Arrange skewers on a serving plate. Dust with shichimi or sansho pepper and serve with lemon wedges. Offer extra tare in a small dish for dipping.

## Special Notice

> Yakitori is traditionally cooked over binchotan charcoal — a hot cast iron pan gets you closest to that char at home. Apply the tare in the final stages only; adding it too early causes it to burn and turn bitter. Reserve a portion of the tare made before it touches raw chicken as a clean dipping sauce for the table.
