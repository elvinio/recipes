# Grilled Mackerel with Daikon

> Serves 4 | 25 minutes | Japanese | Dinner

## Ingredients

- 4 mackerel fillets, skin-on (about 180 g each)
- 1½ tsp fine sea salt
- 300 g daikon radish, peeled and finely grated
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp yuzu juice or lemon juice
- 1 tsp neutral oil
- 4 shiso leaves, for garnish
- 1 lemon, cut into wedges
- 2 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- 4 small bowls of steamed rice or miso soup, to accompany (optional)

## Steps

1. Pat mackerel fillets dry with paper towels. Score the skin of each fillet three times diagonally with a sharp knife to prevent curling and help the salt penetrate.
2. Sprinkle salt evenly over both sides of the fillets. Set on a rack for 10 minutes — the salt will draw out moisture for crispier skin.
3. While the fish salts, grate the daikon using a fine grater. Gently squeeze excess water from the grated daikon with your hands and set aside in a small bowl.
4. Pat the fillets dry again. Brush the skin side lightly with neutral oil.
5. Heat a cast iron or heavy non-stick frying pan over high heat until very hot. Place fillets skin-side down and press gently with a spatula for the first 30 seconds to prevent curling. Cook undisturbed for 4–5 minutes until the skin is charred and crispy.
6. Flip carefully and cook flesh-side down for 2 minutes until the flesh is opaque throughout.
7. Mix soy sauce and yuzu juice in a small bowl.
8. Serve fillets skin-side up on plates. Place a mound of grated daikon alongside, drizzle the ponzu-style dressing over the fish and daikon, scatter sesame seeds, and garnish with shiso leaf and lemon wedges.

## Special Notice

> Mackerel is an oily fish that benefits from the cleansing sharpness of grated daikon — this combination is called "yakimono with oroshi." Do not cover the pan while cooking or the skin will steam instead of crisping. Fresh mackerel is best consumed on the day of purchase; if using frozen, thaw overnight in the refrigerator and ensure thoroughly dry before cooking.
